# China's Chip Material Suppliers Challenge Japanese Dominance; Guangyuan Invests US$1B in T-Glass

Source: TrendForce (citing Nikkei, 21jingji, EastMoney, ijiwei) | 2026-07-03

China is stepping up its semiconductor materials ambitions, challenging a market long dominated by Japanese companies. According to Nikkei, Chinese manufacturers are accelerating investment and production of advanced materials as Beijing pushes for greater semiconductor self-sufficiency.

## T-Glass / Glass Cloth

**Guangyuan New Material:**
- Recently began shipping T-glass products used in AI semiconductor packaging
- Customers include Japan's Panasonic Holdings and Resonac Holdings
- T-glass resists thermal expansion and warping from heat in AI data processing
- First company in China to develop low-dielectric glass cloth for AI servers and 5G base stations
- Japan's Nittobo has long dominated the global T-glass market
- Investment: US$1 billion factory in Henan Province to boost T-glass and low-dielectric glass cloth output (coming online in phases since last year)
- Scale: plans to increase production and sales to 1 million meters per month
- IPO: officially resumed ChiNext board application in July 2026 (three years after previous attempt)

## Copper-Clad Laminates (CCL)

**Shengyi Technology** (China's largest PCB CCL producer):
- Investment: ~5.2 billion yuan new factory in Guangdong Province
  - Products: high-performance materials for AI servers, EVs, new energy vehicles
  - Phase 1: 2028; Phase 2: 2032
- Overseas: 1.4 billion yuan first overseas factory in Thailand (serving Southeast Asia, Europe, U.S.)
- M9-grade copper-clad laminate certified for NVIDIA Rubin and GB300 platforms

## Sputtering Targets

**Konfoong Materials** (China's leading sputtering target producer):
- Investment: 350 million yuan over next 2 years to build factory in South Korea
- Customers: SK hynix and Samsung Electronics
- Achievement: breakthrough in high-purity 300mm silicon targets for advanced memory, begun volume shipments
- Market context: Japan's JX Advanced Materials and Honeywell hold >80% of global high-end sputtering target market

## Photoresists

- Japanese companies Tokyo Ohka Kogyo and JSR hold ~80% of global photoresist market
- **Red Avenue New Material Group** (Chinese photoresist supplier):
  - Filed for Hong Kong Stock Exchange listing in February 2026
  - Ranked #1 among Chinese suppliers by revenue in semiconductor photoresist and TFT array photoresist (first 9 months of 2025)

## Strategic Context

This report documents China's systematic push into semiconductor materials previously dominated by Japanese/U.S./Taiwanese suppliers. The areas covered (T-glass, CCL, sputtering targets, photoresists) are all upstream supply chain inputs for advanced packaging, especially AI chip packaging.
