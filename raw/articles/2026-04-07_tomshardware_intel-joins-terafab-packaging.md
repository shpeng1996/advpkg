Intel officially announced its participation in the TeraFab project on April 7, 2026, joining SpaceX, xAI, and Tesla in what Elon Musk described as "the most epic chip-building effort ever."

## Intel's Official Statement

Intel posted on X (formerly Twitter):

> "Intel is proud to join the Terafab project with SpaceX, xAI, and Tesla to help refactor silicon fab technology. Our ability to design, fabricate, and **package** ultra-high-performance chips at scale will help accelerate Terafab's aim to produce 1 TW/year of compute."

The explicit mention of **packaging** in Intel's statement highlights that advanced packaging (EMIB, Foveros) is a core pillar of Intel's value proposition to the TeraFab consortium, alongside chip design and fabrication.

## CEO Endorsement

Intel CEO Lip-Bu Tan also posted on X:

> "Elon has a proven track record of reimagining entire industries."

## TeraFab Background

- **Members**: SpaceX, xAI, Tesla, Intel (as of April 7, 2026)
- **Goal**: Produce 1 terawatt (TW) per year of compute capacity
- **Approach**: Combine logic + memory + advanced packaging at unprecedented scale — "refactoring" silicon fab technology
- **Governance**: No formal press release or SEC filing at time of announcement; revealed via X posts
- **Context**: Elon Musk visited Intel facilities that same weekend prior to the announcement

## Advanced Packaging Significance

TeraFab's stated ambition of combining logic + memory + advanced packaging at 1 TW/year scale would require:
- Massive CoWoS-equivalent or superior 2.5D integration for AI accelerators
- High-bandwidth memory (HBM) integration at scale
- Potentially new panel-level packaging approaches to achieve cost/scale targets
- Intel's EMIB (Embedded Multi-die Interconnect Bridge) and Foveros 3D stacking as candidate technologies

## Competitive Context

- TeraFab represents a potential alternative to TSMC-centric AI chip supply chains
- xAI (Musk's AI company) + Tesla (automotive AI compute) are major potential customers
- Intel's participation positions Intel Foundry as a key manufacturing partner for TeraFab's compute ambitions
- No announced timeline for first TeraFab chips or production milestones as of April 2026
