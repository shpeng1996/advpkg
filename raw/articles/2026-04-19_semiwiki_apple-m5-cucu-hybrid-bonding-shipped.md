# Apple M5 Cu-Cu Hybrid Bonding — Shipped March 2026

**Source:** SemiWiki forum thread, posted 2026-04-19 by NY_Sam2
**Reference:** Slide presented at ICEP-HBS 2026 in Japan by John Rau of Unimicron, photographed by Jett C.
**LinkedIn source:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/jett-c-17972258_apples-ultrafusion-is-evolving-from-activity-7451205629236502528-qzT1

## Key Claim

Apple M5 uses **Cu–Cu hybrid bonding** — confirmed shipped in **March 2026**.

## Technology Evolution Context

From M1 Max's silicon bridge with micro-bumps to M5's Cu–Cu hybrid bonding, Apple has systematically advanced interconnect technology:

- **Apple M1 Max** — Silicon bridge with micro-bumps (UltraFusion)
- **Apple M5** — Cu–Cu hybrid bonding (UltraFusion evolution), shipped 2026-03

By separating CPU and GPU and reconnecting them through advanced bonding, Apple is redefining system architecture through packaging.

## Industry Implications

- This is **the first confirmed consumer product shipping with Cu-Cu hybrid bonding** in high-volume production (March 2026)
- Unimicron (Taiwan substrate maker) presented this data at ICEP-HBS Japan 2026, indicating their involvement in the supply chain
- Question raised: Intel EMIB-T also exploring Cu-Cu hybrid bonding variant — timeline unknown
- Confirms Cu-Cu hybrid bonding has crossed from research/HPC-only to consumer products
