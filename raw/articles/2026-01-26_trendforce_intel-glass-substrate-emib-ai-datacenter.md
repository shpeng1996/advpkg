# Intel Reportedly Presents First Thick-Core Glass Substrate with EMIB, Targeting AI Data Centers

**Source**: TrendForce
**Date**: January 26, 2026

## Summary

Intel has reportedly unveiled its first **thick-core glass substrate** integrated with **EMIB (Embedded Multi-die Interconnect Bridge)** technology, targeting AI data center applications. This represents a significant advancement in substrate technology for advanced 2.5D packaging, combining the precision of glass substrates with Intel's established EMIB interconnect approach.

## Key Details

### Glass Substrate Technology
- Intel's glass substrate uses a **thick-core glass** material that offers superior flatness and lower CTE (Coefficient of Thermal Expansion) compared to traditional organic substrates
- Glass substrates enable finer RDL (Redistribution Layer) line/space and better signal integrity for high-speed AI accelerator applications
- Industry context: glass substrates have been in development at Intel, Samsung, and others as the successor to organic ABF (Ajinomoto Build-up Film) substrates

### EMIB on Glass Integration
- Integrating EMIB bridges within a glass substrate combines two key Intel packaging innovations
- EMIB provides high-density die-to-die interconnect (local silicon bridge) without requiring a full silicon interposer
- Glass substrate improves thermal stability and warpage control critical for large AI accelerator packages

### AI Data Center Targeting
- Intel is positioning this technology for large AI accelerator packages beyond current 2.5D limits
- Target customers include hyperscale data center operators seeking alternatives to TSMC CoWoS
- Glass substrates enable larger package footprints needed for next-generation AI chips (>1000mm² total die area)

### Technology Roadmap Context
- Intel's EMIB technology has been evolving: EMIB → EMIB-T (for HBM4 with through-silicon vias) → EMIB on Glass
- This aligns with industry trend toward glass core substrates (VSOP, Intel, Corning collaboration)
- Expected commercial availability: 2027–2028 for high-volume AI data center applications
