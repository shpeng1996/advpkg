Micron broke ground on July 4, 2026 on a ¥1.5 trillion (US$9.3 billion) expansion of its Hiroshima fab (source: Bloomberg, EE Times Japan). Equipment delivery and installation are set to begin in 2H28.

## Key Details

- **Investment**: ¥1.5 trillion (~US$9.3 billion)
- **Location**: Hiroshima fab expansion (western expansion area, ~9.5 hectares)
- **Groundbreaking**: July 4, 2026
- **Equipment installation**: 2H28 (equipment delivery/installation scheduled)
- **Government support**: Japan METI up to JPY 500 billion subsidy
- **Focus**: 1γ (1-gamma) DRAM scaling + HBM output expansion
- **EUV**: Advanced EUV lithography tools to be introduced for next-gen HBM manufacturing
- **Mass production target**: next-generation 1γ DRAM by 2027 (existing commitment)

## Context

Shortly after South Korea's memory makers (Samsung + SK hynix) unveiled a combined KRW 800 trillion investment, Micron demonstrated comparable scale commitment in Japan. This positions Micron's Japanese operations as a critical HBM and leading-edge DRAM hub alongside US (Virginia) and India facilities.
