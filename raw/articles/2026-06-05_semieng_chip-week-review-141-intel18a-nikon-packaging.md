# Chip Industry Week In Review #141

**Published**: June 5th, 2026 | **Source**: Semiconductor Engineering

**Summary**: Computex shows AI ecosystem; fully autonomous chip design; Intel targets AI racks; Nikon's 1.5 micron L/S litho; IC market rises; Apple's chiplet era; 4,500 chips per AI server rack; HBM price hikes; quantum IPO, chip and roadmap.

---

## Computex in Taiwan (Key Packaging/Semiconductor Items)

- **Arm** and **Nvidia** introduced an AI PC platform (RTX Spark), with an Arm-based Grace CPU, Blackwell RTX GPU, and unified memory.
- **Cadence** announced a fully autonomous virtual agentic AI design engineer.
- **Intel** launched **Xeon 6+**, its first data-center CPU built on **Intel Foundry's 18A** process. Also announced partnerships with Siemens, Hitachi, Foxconn.
- **Wiwynn**, **Ayar Labs** and others demonstrated advanced CPO (co-packaged optics) innovations. **Ayar Labs** joined the NVLink Fusion ecosystem, making its products optically and electrically compatible with Nvidia's co-packaged optics and SerDes.
- **Micron** highlighted its **256GB SOCAMM2** module delivering 1/3 the power and footprint vs. standard RDIMMs.
- **Samsung** unveiled a **mock-up of HBM5** with its new Heat Path Block technology.

## Market & Reports

- **WSTS** raised the semiconductor market forecast to **+$1.5T in 2026** and **$1.9T in 2027** (significantly above prior estimates).
- **SIA/WSTS**: global semiconductor sales hit **$110B in April**, up **~94% year-over-year**.
- **SEMI**: Q1 2026 IC equipment billings increased **14% YoY**.
- **SIA/Deloitte**: semiconductors account for **95% of an AI data server rack's value**, with a single AI server rack containing **over 4,500 packaged chips**.
- **Counterpoint** teardown: **Apple M5 Pro chip** marks Apple's move into a **chiplet-style architecture**, separating CPU- and GPU-heavy dies in one package (hybrid bonding confirmed shipping per prior sources).
- **ITIF** warns China's subsidized semiconductor buildout threatens U.S. chip hubs in Arizona, Idaho, N. Mexico, and Oregon.

## Memory

- **HBM contract prices** expected to rise sharply in **2027** as tight DRAM supply gives memory suppliers greater pricing power.
- Global **DRAM revenue** rose 81% sequentially to **$97B in Q1 2026**; contract prices for conventional DRAM increased ~93–98%. Q2 2026 forecast: +58–63% additional rise.
- **SK hynix** plans to **double its wafer capacity** over the next five years (Nikkei Asia).
- Coalition warned the US government that **DRAM/NAND supply** is being pulled away from consumer electronics, broadband gear, cars, and medical devices.

## New Technologies

- **Nikon** introduced a digital **lithography system for back-end advanced packaging** processes capable of **1.5 micron lines and spaces** at a speed of **65 panels per hour**. Target delivery date: later FY2026 (ends March 31, 2027).
- **JEDEC** published two new SiC guidelines for reliability and evaluation in power electronics.

## Americas

- **SpaceX/Tesla Terafab**: SpaceX secured tax incentives from Grimes County, Texas; SpaceX and Tesla plan to invest initial **$55B** in the Terafab project, potentially rising to **$119B** if fully built out.
- **CHIPS Act awards**: $150M to xLight (free-electron laser EUV alternative), $1.6B loan/award to USA Rare Earth, $30M to Powerex.

## Europe

- **European Commission** presented **Chips Act 2.0**, shifting toward wider plan covering full supply chain and linking chip policy to AI/data-center growth.

## Earnings

- **Broadcom**, **Credo**, and **STMicro** posted earnings / updated guidance.
