## Summary

TrendForce (2026-01-05) provides SK Hynix's 2026 strategic outlook for HBM product mix and capacity ramp.

### HBM Product Mix 2026
- **HBM3E** remains dominant in 2026 (~two-thirds of total HBM shipments by volume)
- **HBM4** gradually gains traction throughout 2026; dual strategy adopted
- SK Hynix employs "dual strategy": standard HBM4 + custom HBM4 variants for top customers

### SK Hynix HBM4 Supply & Capacity
- SK Hynix to supply **~two-thirds (~70%)** of NVIDIA's HBM4 demand for the Vera Rubin platform (per 2026-01-28 report)
- New **M15X fab** (1b DRAM node): commercial production started **February 2026** — 4 months ahead of schedule
  - Initial output: ~10,000 wafers/month
  - Plans to scale severalfold by end of 2026
- 2026 full-year capacity reportedly already sold out (per earlier reports)

### HBM4E Development
- SK Hynix and Samsung both targeting **1H 2026** completion of HBM4E development
- Samsung's custom HBM4E design aimed for **May–June 2026** completion
- HBM4E expected to capture **~40% of 2027 HBM market** (per 2025-11-13 TrendForce report)

### Samsung HBM Capacity
- Samsung plans **50% HBM capacity surge in 2026**, targeting ~250,000 wafers/month (from ~170,000)
- Samsung HBM4 officially shipped 2026-02-12; mass production for NVIDIA Rubin GPUs underway
- Samsung trails SK Hynix in HBM4 qualification but competes aggressively

### NVIDIA HBM4 Program
- NVIDIA Vera Rubin GPU platform drives HBM4 demand
- 12-layer HBM4 ramping; 16-layer push by SK Hynix, Samsung, and Micron
- NVIDIA GTC 2026: Samsung & SK Hynix showcased HBM4 (report 2026-02-25)

### HBM Key Specs (from JEDEC JESD270-4 standard, 2025-04)
- HBM4 bandwidth: **1.65+ TB/s** per stack
- 12-high stack standard; 16-high in development
