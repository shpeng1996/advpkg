# Nvidia Vera Rubin Enters Full Production: Samsung, SK Hynix, Micron Named HBM4 Suppliers

By Allen Lee, published Jun 02 2026, 10:21 AM EDT.

Jensen Huang confirmed at the GTC Taipei 2026 keynote (June 1) that Nvidia's Vera Rubin platform has entered full production. Samsung, SK hynix, and Micron were all confirmed as HBM4 suppliers for the platform.

## Supplier allocation

Pre-keynote analyst estimates (explicitly not official Nvidia-published figures) put allocation at approximately:
- SK hynix: ~60–70%
- Samsung: ~25–30%
- Micron: remainder

Samsung began HBM4 mass production in February 2026 and has shipped early HBM4E samples targeting Vera Rubin Ultra (expected late 2027). Micron, previously reported as excluded from initial production runs, secured a supply role by the time of the keynote.

## Vera CPU

Nvidia's first in-house data center CPU, paired with the Rubin GPU in the Vera Rubin platform:
- LPDDR5X memory
- 1.2 TB/s memory bandwidth
- Designed for agentic AI workloads — orchestration, tool-calling, long-context retrieval
- Early units hand-delivered to Anthropic, OpenAI, SpaceX, and Oracle in mid-May 2026

## Manufacturing scale

"Full production" reflects Nvidia plus roughly 150 Taiwanese ODM partners (Foxconn, Quanta, Wistron) now manufacturing NVL72 rack-scale systems at scale. Huang described the supply chain as "twice the size" of the Grace Blackwell generation's. Rack assembly time has been reduced from roughly 2 hours to 5 minutes.

First customer shipments are scheduled for summer 2026, with broad availability in H2 2026. First cloud providers: AWS, Google Cloud, Microsoft Azure, Oracle.

## Market reaction

Following the keynote, Samsung Electronics stock rose 10.1% (a record), and LG Electronics rose approximately 30%. This came ahead of Huang's planned Seoul visit (around June 5) to meet the chairs of LG, SK, Hyundai, and Naver — partly focused on robotics partnerships (LG CLOi humanoid platform integration with Nvidia Isaac). A "Korea Partner Night" dinner was held June 1 in Taipei with executives from Samsung, SK hynix, LG, Hyundai, and Naver.
