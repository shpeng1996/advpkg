TSMC held its Q2 2026 earnings call with overseas expansion in the spotlight. Chairman and CEO C.C. Wei said the company will invest an additional US$100 billion in Arizona to support several fabs for 2nm and below processes, as well as advanced packaging. The company plans to build four additional fabs.

The new commitment brings TSMC's total planned investment in Arizona to US$265 billion, eventually expanding its U.S. footprint to 10 fabs and two advanced packaging facilities. A U.S. official indicated the additional investment may be allocated to three logic fabs and one advanced packaging facility, depending on market conditions.

Wei stated that TSMC continues to expect the ramp-up of its overseas fabs to dilute gross margin by 2–3 percentage points in the early stages, widening to 3–4 percentage points as overseas operations scale over the coming years.

On AI demand: Wei said TSMC closely tracks the progress of AI data center construction and continues to expect AI demand to remain very strong through 2030.

TSMC will also continue to grow its footprint in Taiwan, planning 13 advanced process and advanced packaging facilities in Taiwan.

Japan's JASM Fab 1 is adding capacity for CMOS image sensor applications; Germany's ESMC fab will serve automotive and industrial demand.
