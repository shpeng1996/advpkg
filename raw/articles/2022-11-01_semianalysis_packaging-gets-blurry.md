# The Future Of Packaging Gets Blurry

SemiAnalysis argues that the boundary between 2.1D, 2.3D, and 2.5D advanced packaging is becoming less clear as fanout RDL, ABF substrates, coreless substrates, and embedded silicon bridges improve.

## Extracted Points

- Intel EMIB places a silicon bridge inside a cavity in a build-up ABF substrate to avoid a full silicon interposer and support larger packages.
- AMD MI250X and Apple M1 Ultra illustrate products that combine multiple packaging styles.
- Fanout RDL plus embedded bridge can resemble EMIB functionally, but the process flow differs from build-up-substrate EMIB.
- Amkor SLIM and ASE SPIL NTI were cited at 0.4 um and 0.5 um line/space capability, respectively, but only on the first layer.
- Build-up ABF remains a base layer for advanced packaging and is moving up in performance through coreless substrates and higher layer counts.
- Packaging choices increasingly require simultaneous evaluation of pad pitch, line/space, layer count, reliability, warpage, package cost, yield, and package size.
