# SK Hynix Soars 13% on Nasdaq Debut: HBM Architecture Makes It Irreplaceable in AI Chips

**Source:** Tech Times | **Date:** 2026-07-12 | **Author:** Eloise Jones

## Summary

SK Hynix (SKHY) raised **$26.5 billion** in the largest-ever U.S. listing by a foreign company on July 10, 2026. Closed first day at **$168.01** (+13% over $149 IPO price). Regular-way trading under SKHY begins Monday, July 13, 2026. Options expected Tuesday, July 14.

**Market cap at close:** ~$1.27 trillion (11th largest U.S.-listed company by market cap)

- **ADRs sold:** 177.9 million at $149 each (each ADR = 1/10 ordinary Korean share)
- **Total raised:** $26.5 billion (surpasses Alibaba's ~$25B 2014 listing)

- **Temporary ticker:** SKHYV (converted to SKHY for regular trading July 13)
- **Anchor investors:** Baillie Gifford, Coatue, Situational Awareness Partners (~$7B collectively)

## Why HBM Cannot Be Replaced Once a GPU Is Built (CoWoS Lock-in Analysis)

HBM is physically bonded to the GPU/AI accelerator using **TSMC CoWoS** packaging. Once assembled:
- Cannot be disassembled without destroying both components
- Unlike laptop DRAM modules (swappable), HBM is architectural at silicon level
- SK Group Chairman Chey: "if SK Hynix's HBM is replaced with another product, the AI system may not function properly"

**HBM3E specs:** ~1.2 TB/s memory bandwidth; 1,024-bit bus (vs. 32-bit GDDR)
**HBM4 specs:** 2,048-bit bus; peak ~2 TB/s per stack (JEDEC JESD270-4, April 2025)

## HBM Economics

- 1 GB HBM requires ~3× wafer capacity vs. standard DDR5 DRAM
- SK Hynix Q1 2026: operating profit KRW 37.6T ($27.8B) on KRW 52.6T ($35.5B) revenue → **~71.5% operating margin**

## Market Position

- SK Hynix HBM revenue market share: **~56%** (Q1 2026)
- Samsung: targeting 37% (up from 27% in 2025) per Bernstein/TrendForce projections
- Every Nvidia H100, H200, Blackwell GPU carries SK Hynix HBM

## Key Executive Statements

## Legal Context

Federal antitrust class action: **Garciaguirre v. Samsung Electronics, No. 5:26-cv-06345** (NDCA, Judge Noel Wise; filed June 25, 2026).
- Plaintiffs: 17 (14 consumers + 3 small businesses)
- Allegation: SK Hynix, Samsung, Micron coordinated to curtail DDR3/DDR4 production using HBM transition as cover; drove consumer memory prices up ~700%
- Historical context: Samsung and Hynix pleaded guilty to price-fixing (1999-2002); prior 2018 class action dismissed 2020 (Ninth Circuit 2022)
- Companies deny allegations; case not past initial filing stage

## Korea Discount

- Korean-listed SK Hynix shares historically trade at ~35% discount to U.S. peer Micron (HSBC research, 13-year average)
- ADR structure removes primary structural cause of discount (accessibility to U.S. institutional investors)
- Philadelphia Semiconductor Index inclusion: earliest September 2027 (3-month listing requirement)
