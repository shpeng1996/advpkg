The Trump administration continues to push for reshoring the semiconductor industry to the US. As global foundry giants TSMC, Intel, and Samsung Electronics expand advanced manufacturing capacities stateside, strengthening local OSAT (outsourced semiconductor assembly and test) capabilities is seen as the natural next step in completing a domestic end-to-end semiconductor supply chain.

The headline figure — US chip packaging capacity projected to reach 10% of global share by 2032 — is a packaging-specific capacity target distinct from the previously wiki-recorded "10%→14% global wafer fab share by 2032" SIA figure (see wiki/concepts/geopolitics-advanced-packaging.md).
