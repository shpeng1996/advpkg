# Samsung HBM4E Ships First: Record 756% Profit Surge Triggers Analyst Upgrades on AI Memory Lead

Samsung Electronics began shipping the world's first 12-layer HBM4E samples to major global customers on May 29, 2026 — putting it at least six months ahead of SK Hynix and Micron in the race to supply next-generation AI accelerators. The announcement triggered a wave of analyst upgrades that pushed Samsung's market capitalization past 2,000 trillion won for the first time in history, three months after Samsung began mass-producing HBM4.

## Financial Context
Samsung's semiconductor division posted 53.7 trillion won ($36.1B) in Q1 2026 operating profit — about 94% of the company's total quarterly profit of 57.2 trillion won — a ~756% YoY increase driven almost entirely by AI memory demand. This exceeded Samsung's full-year 2025 profit (43.6 trillion won) and beat the analyst consensus estimate (55.28 trillion won).

## HBM4E Specs vs HBM4
- 12-layer stack, stable pin speed 14 Gbps (scalable to 16 Gbps under heavy workloads) — >20% faster than HBM4
- Bandwidth: up to 3.6 TB/s per stack
- Capacity: 48 GB per stack (30%+ over HBM4's 36 GB); roadmap to 32 GB (8-layer) and 64 GB (16-layer)
- 16% better energy efficiency, >14% reduced thermal resistance vs. HBM4
- Built on Samsung's 6th-gen 10nm-class 1c DRAM process + 4nm logic base die from Samsung Foundry (same foundation as HBM4, expected to speed qualification)
- Caveat: figures are Samsung's own announced specs, not yet independently verified by named third parties; HBM4 received its highest evaluation grade at 11.7 Gbps in Dec 2025 SiP testing as a credibility baseline

## Competitive Landscape
- SK Hynix held ~52-57% of global HBM revenue through most of 2025; targeted HBM4E samples for 2H26, mass production 2027
- Micron moved HBM4 into HVM earlier in 2026; HBM4E ramp also targeted for 2027
- Samsung's HBM4E samples target Nvidia's next-gen GPU platform, following HBM4's deployment on Vera Rubin
- SK Hynix reportedly holds ~two-thirds of Nvidia's 2026 HBM4 supply allocation; Samsung positions to compete for next allocation cycle
- HBM consumes ~3x the wafer capacity of standard DDR5 per GB — concentrating margin and reducing consumer-device supply

## Market Reaction
- Samsung shares rose as much as 6.51%, closed +5.84% at 317,000 won on May 29
- Brokerage price-target raises: Nomura 590,000 won; Korea Investment & Securities 570,000 won; Shinhan 550,000 won; NH Investment & Securities 490,000 won
- Samsung guidance: HBM sales to more than triple in 2026 vs 2025; HBM4 ~half of HBM revenue in 2H26
- May 27 union settlement (≈74% approval; 6.2% salary increase + 10.5% profit-linked bonus) removed near-term production-disruption risk
