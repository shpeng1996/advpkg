Vanguard International Semiconductor (VIS), a TSMC affiliate, commented on its future expansion plans, including those related to VSMC, its Singapore joint venture. VIS Chairman Leuh Fang said the company is evaluating the construction of a second 12-inch fab, as existing capacity has already been fully booked by customers.

The company has adjusted the product mix at its Singapore fab in response to strong advanced packaging demand by adding silicon interposer products. As a result, the facility's originally planned monthly capacity has been revised down from 55,000 wafers to 44,000 wafers.

VSMC's 12-inch fab in Singapore will continue using 30nm to 40nm technologies to produce silicon interposers, with the related technology licensing coming from TSMC. Industry observers believe this could position VIS to enter the AI advanced packaging CoWoS supply chain.

Another key factor behind the fully booked capacity is customers' efforts to diversify geopolitical risks. The Singapore fab provides an opportunity for such diversification. The fab is expected to begin mass production in 2027, while the original timeline for reaching full capacity in 2029 could potentially be accelerated.

## VIS Accelerates Singapore Fab Ramp with TSMC Equipment Support

Investment costs for the Singapore fab will be reduced from the originally planned US$7.8 billion to US$6.7 billion, benefiting from equipment support provided by TSMC. More than 200 tools have already been moved into the Singapore fab with support from TSMC and are currently being used for trial production.

Regarding the project's funding structure, VIS will invest US$2.4 billion for a 60% stake, while joint venture partner NXP Semiconductors will invest US$1.6 billion for a 40% stake in VSMC. The remaining funding gap will be covered through long-term contract prepayments from customers.

## VIS Upgrades 8-Inch Capacity for Finer-Line Demand

VIS plans in 2026 to phase out and upgrade part of its mature-node capacity, shifting toward finer-line processes to meet customers' long-term demand for 8-inch wafer products.

For Q2 2026, recovering customer orders are expected to drive wafer shipments up around 10% to 13% quarter-over-quarter, while average selling prices (ASP) are projected to increase about 2% to 5%. Gross margin is expected to remain above 31%.

## Key Facts Summary
- VSMC location: Singapore; VIS 60%, NXP 40%
- Technology: 30–40nm for silicon interposer production
- Tech licensing: from TSMC
- Capacity: revised from 55K → 44K wafers/month (interposer products need more area)
- Total investment: reduced from $7.8B → $6.7B (TSMC equipment reuse)
- Mass production start: 2027
- Full capacity target: originally 2029, potentially accelerated
- VIS weighing 2nd 12-inch fab due to fully booked capacity
