# NVIDIA's Rubin Ultra Seen Sticking to Dual-Die Design on Packaging Constraints; TSMC 3nm Demand Intact

**Source**: TrendForce (citing Commercial Times and Next Apple)
**Date**: April 1, 2026

## Summary

As NVIDIA's Rubin GPU moves into mass production, industry attention has turned to the Rubin Ultra, the next-generation flagship. Reports indicate Rubin Ultra will stick to a **dual-die (two-reticle)** chiplet design rather than scaling to a monolithic larger die, primarily due to packaging constraints.

## Key Details

### Packaging Constraints Driving Chiplet Architecture
- NVIDIA's Rubin GPU is approaching 5.5x reticle size, meaning a standard 12-inch wafer can accommodate only 7 (or as few as 4) dies per wafer
- Rubin Ultra (NVL576) will feature **four reticle-sized chips** in its full rack configuration
- Target: up to **100 PFLOPS FP4** and **1 TB total HBM4E** capacity across 16 HBM sites
- The dual-die design per GPU module is constrained by current CoWoS interposer size limits (~120mm × 120mm)

### TSMC 3nm Demand Intact
- AI workloads are set to claim **36% of TSMC N3 capacity in 2026**, up from just 5% in 2025
- NVIDIA's Rubin and Rubin Ultra remain on TSMC N3P (3nm-class) process
- Demand for TSMC N3 remains robust despite architectural trade-offs

### CoWoS Scaling Limitations
- Current CoWoS interposer size limits constrain how many HBM stacks and die area can be integrated
- TSMC is developing larger CoWoS formats (up to 14-reticle, announced at 2026 Tech Symposium for 2028 readiness)
- Panel-level CoPoS (2028–29 mass production) represents the long-term solution for even larger packages

### SoIC Integration
- Rubin Ultra is expected to leverage **TSMC SoIC** (3D stacking) for die-to-die integration within the GPU chiplet
- SoIC capacity ramp to ~10,000–15,000 wafers/month by 2026

### Market Implications
- Packaging constraints are becoming the primary architectural constraint for AI GPU scaling
- This validates TSMC's investment in CoPoS and larger CoWoS formats
- Intel EMIB's "foundry-neutral" positioning gains relevance as NVIDIA evaluates alternatives
