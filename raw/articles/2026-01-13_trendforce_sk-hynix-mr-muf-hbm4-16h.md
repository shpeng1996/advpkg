SK hynix is expected to continue using its MR-MUF (Mass Reflow–Molded Underfill) process for HBM4 16-high products. According to DealSite, the company evaluated adopting fluxless bonding for HBM4 16-high but ultimately opted to retain its existing advanced MR-MUF process, concluding that the technology remains premature given performance and cost considerations.

Sources say SK hynix launched full-scale evaluations in Q4 2025 to introduce fluxless bonding for HBM4 16-high, with the aim of applying it across the entire production volume. However, the evaluation results failed to meet expectations.

## MR-MUF Technology Status

According to EE Times, SK hynix unveiled a 16-high HBM4 at CES 2026, using its proprietary MR-MUF technology to thin individual DRAM wafers to just 30 µm, enabling the stack to meet JEDEC's strict 775 µm height limit.

Industry consensus is that advanced MR-MUF can adequately support the 16-high HBM4 design. SK hynix has confirmed in overseas investor briefings that it will maintain its current process through HBM4 and HBM4E 16-high products.

## HBM Stacking Technology Progression

| Generation | Technology | Key Characteristic |
|-----------|------------|-------------------|
| HBM3/3E (8–12H) | TC-NCF | Thermal compression, no-clean flux |
| HBM4 (12–16H) | MR-MUF | Mass reflow, molded underfill; 30µm die thinning |
| HBM (>20H, future) | Fluxless / Hybrid Bonding | Direct Cu-Cu; no bumps |

## Preparing for Post-20-Layer Stacks: Fluxless Bonding as a Bridge

Once stack heights exceed 20 layers, SK hynix believes adopting next-generation technologies will become unavoidable. This led the company to evaluate fluxless bonding at an early stage, viewing it as a transitional step toward hybrid bonding.

Fluxless bonding removes the use of flux while remaining within the MR-MUF process family, which SK hynix sees as lowering the burden of process transition relative to competitors.

## Equipment Orders Context

SK hynix ordered seven TC bonders from ASMPT in November 2025 instead of sourcing from long-time top TC bonder supplier Hanmi Semiconductor. However, a source familiar with SK hynix said the order appears unrelated to fluxless bonding—the company placed orders for existing tools whose supply had already been decided, rather than equipment specifically tied to fluxless processes.

## Namics Agreement Expiry Impact

The approaching expiration of SK hynix's exclusive MR-MUF materials agreement with Namics is influencing its strategy. The company plans to retain its existing process for HBM4 and HBM4E 12- and 16-high products, while a transition to hybrid bonding is seen as the preferred next step, albeit with potentially delayed timing.
