Huawei, Baidu and other industry partners have launched China's first multi-source agreement (MSA) for near-packaged optics (NPO), aiming to establish an open and unified technical standard framework for next-generation high-speed optical interconnects. The OPEN NPO project was announced at the Supernode and GW-level AIDC Technology Forum held in Beijing on July 9.

## Participating Organizations

Huawei, China Mobile Research Institute, JD Cloud, Baidu, China Electronics Standardization Institute, ZTE Software, H3C, Accelink, Ligent, HGTech, Lightelligence, Hero Power, Yamaichi, Hirose, Molex, Luxshare Technology, Qinghong Electronics, Aluksen (Optoelectronics Research Institute of Westlake University), iNano, Joywell Semi.

## NPO Technology Explained

NPO (Near-Packaged Optics) moves the optical engine from the traditional pluggable position on the front panel of a switch closer to the switching or computing chip on the PCB. The optical engine and chip remain separate packages connected through short-distance, high-speed electrical traces.

Position: between conventional pluggable optical modules and fully integrated CPO.
- Reduces electrical transmission distances and power consumption
- Maintains advantages: modularity, repairability, flexibility for multi-vendor sourcing

## Industry Adoption Context

- Alibaba and Tencent: identified NPO as key direction, promoting open standards via Open Data Center Committee (ODCC)
- Meta and Microsoft: prioritizing NPO deployments via OCI-MSA (Optical Compute Interconnect Multi-Source Agreement)
- Amazon: multi-supplier strategy, partnered with STMicroelectronics for NPO solutions
- TrendForce: NPO is near- to mid-term transition solution favored by many cloud service providers (CSPs)

## OPEN NPO Project Roadmap

- Q3 2026: first technical specification released
- Q3 2026: product adaptation and full-scenario testing for NPO solutions and electrical connectors
- 1H 2027: improve collaboration mechanisms, expand industry ecosystem, enable large-scale commercial NPO adoption

## Significance

China's first domestic NPO optical interconnect MSA is designed to build an independent and open high-speed optical interconnect ecosystem in China, reducing dependence on foreign standards (OCI-MSA, OIF). Critical for China's autonomous AI computing infrastructure as Huawei/Baidu build sovereign AI data center infrastructure independent of NVIDIA/US supply chains.
