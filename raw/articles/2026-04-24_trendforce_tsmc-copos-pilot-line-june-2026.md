## Summary

TSMC is advancing its panel-level packaging technology, called CoPoS (Chip-on-Panel-on-Substrate), with its pilot line reportedly on track for June 2026 completion. Tool deliveries to R&D teams began in February 2026.

## Key Details

- **Technology**: CoPoS is TSMC's fan-out panel-level packaging (FOPLP) technology — essentially a square-panel evolution of CoWoS-L and CoWoS-R, replacing the traditional round wafer with a rectangular substrate.
- **Pilot Line Location**: TSMC's Visionchip subsidiary; Chiayi AP7 campus targeted for mass production.
- **Timeline**:
  - February 2026: Tool deliveries to R&D teams begin
  - June 2026: Full pilot line completion (on track)
  - 2027: Pilot facility focuses on refining process to meet partner requirements
  - Late 2028 – Early 2029: Mass production ramp begins
- **Strategic Rationale**: CoPoS offers significantly larger packaging area per unit, reducing cost per die vs. wafer-based CoWoS, critical for next-generation AI accelerators with even more HBM stacks.
- **Market Context**: Yole estimates the panel-level packaging market will quadruple from $160M (2024) to $650M (2030); panel volume nearly triples to ~220,000 panels.
- **Related Players**: ASE and Powertech also expanding FOPLP; Powertech trial yields have reached 90%.

## Context

This follows TSMC's AP7 expansion announcement (January 2026) targeting WMCM (Wafer-level Multi-Chip Module) and CoPoS at Chiayi. Advanced Packaging CapEx is projected to grow at 24% CAGR in 2025–27.
