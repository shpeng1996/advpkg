# China OSAT SJ Semiconductor Raises RMB 5B in IPO

China's chip packaging firm SJ Semiconductor has successfully listed on the STAR Market. SJ Semiconductor (SJSemi) has officially debuted on the Shanghai Stock Exchange's STAR Market, raising a total of about RMB 5 billion (~USD 690M) in its IPO, making it the largest fundraising in China's A-share market so far in 2026 and the biggest IPO on the STAR Market this year. The company's market valuation is approximately RMB 140 billion (~USD 19.3B) on April 21.

## IPO Proceeds Allocation

All funds will be allocated to the chiplet-based multi-chip integration packaging segment, with a focus on 3D multi-chip integration packaging projects and ultra-high-density interconnect 3D multi-chip packaging projects.

## Company Background

SJ Semiconductor is one of the earliest companies in China to achieve mass production of 2.5D silicon-based chip packaging and is among the largest by production scale. The company has filled gaps in China's capabilities in 12-inch wafer mid-end manufacturing, including wafer probing, bumping, and wafer-level packaging.

Its predecessor was established through a joint investment by China's leading foundry SMIC and OSAT provider JCET. The joint venture completed process tuning within just one year of its establishment. By 2016, it had become a qualified supplier for Qualcomm's 14nm wafer bumping — the first company in China to enter the 14nm process.

## Market Position

In 2024, the company held approximately 85% share of China's 2.5D packaging market and about 8% globally (per EE Times China).

Revenue increased fourfold from RMB 1.633 billion in 2022 to RMB 6.521 billion in 2025. Net profit swung from a loss of RMB 329 million to a profit of RMB 923 million over the three-year period.

Customer A (undisclosed) remained SJ Semiconductor's largest client, with its share of revenue rising sharply from 40.56% to 74.40% (2022–H1 2025), indicating significant customer concentration risk.

## Competitive Landscape

SJ Semiconductor holds a first-mover advantage in China's 2.5D packaging. However, JCET is accelerating mass production of its XDFOI chiplet technology for AI and HPC applications, while Tongfu Microelectronics has gained an early lead in chiplet advancements through its close partnership with AMD.

SJ Semiconductor has become an important player in China's domestic supply chain, offering 2.5D and 3D integration capabilities required to support the country's next generation of AI and HPC systems.
