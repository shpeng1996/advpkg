# TSMC Accelerates CoPoS Packaging to Replace CoWoS, as Glass Core Substrates Cut Costs 30% and Boost Wafer Utilization Past 90%

By Hassan Mujtaba, Jun 20, 2026

TSMC is aggressively working on CoPoS (Chip-On-Panel-on-Substrate, panel-level packaging) to replace CoWoS for growing AI/compute demand, with glass core substrates playing a central role.

## Key Claims & Data

- Source report: Commercial Times Taiwan (2026-06-20). TSMC is "aggressively pursuing CoPoS and accelerating the construction of its ecosystem" to surpass CoWoS's physical limits; glass core substrates are key to accelerating mass-production yield.
- Standard CoWoS wafer: ~300mm circular. CoPoS panels: up to 750x620mm (also disclosed 310x310mm and 515x510mm panel formats).
- CoPoS panel format raises material utilization from <70% (12-inch circular wafer) to >90%, addressing geometric waste/cost escalation from reticle-size maximization in ultra-large AI chips post-2028.
- Cost: 20-30% lower cost per unit area vs CoWoS, per Yole Group chart (600x600mm panel w/ 64 interposers ≈ 20% cost decrease, ~81% area efficiency vs ~45% for 300mm wafer).
- Timeline (per Ming-Chi Kuo, citing TSMC's JPCA Show 2026 Japan presentation, June 11, 2026, "Advanced Packaging Technology Essential to the Evolution of AI"): CoPoS targeted for mass production in 2H28, aimed at packages above the 9.5x reticle-size class. Trial production begins 2027, mass production 2028. CoPoS + glass core substrate timeline: 2030+. TSMC Arizona expected to play a role in CoPoS production 2029-2030.
- TSMC is also developing Glass Substrate tech for CoWoS itself (3-layer design: glass core sandwiched between two ABF layers), working with Ibiden and Innolux.
- AMD reportedly will be a key FOPLP + 1.4nm customer for its client-aimed Zen 7 "Grimlock" CPUs.

## New Knowledge Added

- First reporting of TSMC's JPCA Show 2026 (Japan) presentation slide deck details (~40 slides) on CoPoS economics for >9.5x reticle-size packages, with explicit 2H28 mass-production target — more specific than prior pilot-line timing (previously reported as "2028-29 ramp" in earlier TrendForce coverage).
- Quantifies material utilization improvement (<70% → >90%) for panel vs wafer format — a new concrete metric not previously captured in wiki/technologies/copos.md.
- Confirms TSMC Arizona's role in CoPoS production starting 2029-2030.
