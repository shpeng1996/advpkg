# Nvidia at Computex 2026: Jensen Huang Flies to TSMC as Vera Rubin Ramp Strains Taiwan Supply Chain

**By Shannon Harwood — Tech Times, May 24, 2026**

Jensen Huang landed at Taipei's Songshan Airport on Saturday, May 23. His central purpose is a meeting with TSMC Chairman C.C. Wei to lock in production commitments for Nvidia's next-generation Vera Rubin platform — described as "the largest product launch, probably in the history of Taiwan."

## NVIDIA Q1 FY2027 Financial Results

- Revenue: $81.62 billion (+85% YoY), beating analyst expectations by >3%
- Data center revenue: $75.2 billion (+92% YoY), representing 90% of total revenue
- Q2 FY2027 guidance: ~$91 billion
- Additional $80 billion share buyback authorized
- Quarterly dividend raised from $0.01 to $0.25 per share
- Zero China data center compute revenue assumed in guidance

## Vera Rubin Platform Details

The Vera Rubin platform is a six-chip system:
- Vera central processing unit (CPU)
- Rubin graphics processing unit (GPU)
- NVLink 6 switch
- ConnectX-9 SuperNIC
- BlueField-4 data processing unit
- Spectrum-X ethernet switch

NVL72 configuration: 36 Vera CPUs + 72 Rubin GPUs, ~2 million parts, ~150 Taiwanese supply-chain partners.

Performance vs Blackwell predecessor:
- Training: 3.5× improvement
- Inference: 5× improvement
- Inference cost: 1/7th of Blackwell platform

Vera CPU opens what Huang described as a "brand-new $200 billion total addressable market" — the global CPU segment.

## CoWoS as the Critical Packaging Bottleneck

The constraint Huang's TSMC meeting addresses is not silicon fabrication — it is advanced packaging. TSMC's CoWoS technology integrates GPU/CPU dies with HBM into the unified package AI accelerators require.

TSMC capacity expansion:
- Late 2024: ~35,000 CoWoS wafers/month
- End of 2026 projection: 120,000–140,000 wafers/month (~4× increase in under two years)
- TSMC CEO C.C. Wei: "CoWoS capacity remains sold out through 2025 and into 2026"

NVIDIA reportedly pre-committed >50% of TSMC's available CoWoS capacity through 2027. This lock-in squeezes AMD and AI chip startups.

## China Market Share and Geopolitics

- Nvidia's China AI accelerator market share: dropped from ~95% to effectively zero
- Huawei Ascend 950PR entered mass production: March 2026
- Huawei 2026 AI chip revenue projection: $12 billion (+60% from $7.5B in 2025), ~750,000 units
- Morgan Stanley: China AI chip market to reach $67B by 2030, domestic firms capturing majority

Licensed H200 pathway (Dec 2025 framework): approved sales to ~10 Chinese firms (Alibaba, Tencent, ByteDance, JD.com) subject to 25% US revenue share. As of late May 2026, zero H200s delivered — Beijing instructed firms to prioritize Huawei.

## Memory Price Concern

Huang warned that higher memory costs could "significantly affect consumer electronics prices," calling it "a very important form of inflation" and urging memory suppliers (SK Hynix, Micron, Samsung) to expand capacity.

## Export Compliance Investigation

Taiwan prosecutors announced investigation into individuals using forged documents to illegally export Super Micro AI servers containing Nvidia chips to China. Connected to US federal indictment (March 2026) charging Super Micro-linked individuals with conspiring to reroute $2.5 billion in US AI technology to China.
