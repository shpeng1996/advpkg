# Intel Foundry Said to Boost Equipment Orders by 50%+ YoY; 14A May Draw Major Customers by Year-End

**Source**: TrendForce
**Date**: April 20, 2026

## Summary

Intel Foundry (IFS) has reportedly boosted equipment orders by **more than 50% year-over-year** in 2026, signaling an accelerated push to ramp its **14A** (1.4nm-class) process node. Industry sources suggest 14A could attract major external foundry customers by year-end 2026, which would also significantly expand Intel's advanced packaging (EMIB) commercial pipeline.

## Key Details

### 14A Equipment Orders Surge
- Intel Foundry's equipment orders are up **>50% YoY** in 2026 compared to 2025
- Primary target: **14A** process (successor to 18A; uses High-NA EUV + RibbonFET)
- Equipment investment signals confidence in 14A yield progress and customer engagement

### Customer Pipeline for 14A
- Reports indicate major unnamed customers are evaluating 14A for potential tape-out by year-end 2026
- Context: Intel's 18A is targeted at Intel internal products (Panther Lake, Clearwater Forest); external foundry customers remain cautious
- 14A represents the next step where Intel aims to be clearly competitive with TSMC N2/A16

### EMIB-Packaging Linkage
- Intel's foundry strategy and EMIB advanced packaging are increasingly coupled:
  - External foundry customers are expected to use **EMIB packaging** alongside 14A chips
  - This creates a vertical integration opportunity for Intel (fab + package)
  - EMIB's "foundry-neutral" pitch to hyperscalers (Google, Amazon, Meta) complements 14A customer outreach

### Competitive Context
- Intel's 18A-P and 18A-PT external foundry variants are seeing renewed interest after initial skepticism
- TSMC A16 (backside power delivery) also targets 2026 production; both are competing for same customer tier
- Intel's Malaysia advanced packaging complex is operational in 2026, providing co-located fab+package capacity

### Implications for Advanced Packaging
- 14A customer wins → automatic EMIB pipeline (Intel strongly encourages co-packaged solutions)
- Intel's EMIB with glass substrate (announced Jan 2026) positions as the premium AI data center packaging option
- Amkor's Songdo K5 EMIB outsourcing capacity adds flexibility for customers that need Intel packaging without Intel fab
