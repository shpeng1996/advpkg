# Micron Hikes CapEx to $20B with 2026 HBM Supply Fully Booked; HBM4 Ramps 2Q26

*TrendForce — December 18, 2025*

## Key Headlines

- Micron CEO Sanjay Mehrotra confirmed the company has locked in **pricing and volume agreements covering its entire calendar 2026 HBM supply**, including HBM4
- **FY26 CapEx raised to USD 20 billion** (up from USD 18 billion), multi-year supply negotiations ongoing
- Despite aggressive capacity build-out, Micron can currently meet only **50–66% of demand from core customers**
- Tight conditions in both DRAM and NAND set to linger through and beyond calendar 2026

## HBM4 Ramp Status

- HBM4 boasting **industry-leading speeds above 11 Gbps**
- Slated to ramp with high yields in **2Q26** (April–June 2026)
- Aligning closely with customers' product rollout timelines
- Both HBM3E and HBM4 expected to significantly contribute to 2026 revenue

## Financial Performance

- Q2 FY26 revenue guidance: **$18.7B ± $400M** (vs. analyst consensus of $14.2B — a 32% beat)
- Q1 FY26 actual: revenue $13.64B, EPS $4.78 (beat estimates of $12.85B / $3.95)
- Company expects **record Q2 and FY26 results** across revenue, gross margin, EPS, and free cash flow
- DRAM and NAND bit shipments expected to grow ~20% in calendar 2026

## Demand Outlook

- Soaring AI computing demand outpacing supply
- Shortage emerging in simpler PC memory due to shift toward AI datacenter memory production
- Tight supply conditions expected to extend through and beyond calendar 2026

## Singapore Facility Reference

- Micron broke ground on new HBM packaging facility in Singapore (announced January 2025)
- USD 7 billion investment, designed for HBM3E products initially, ~1,400 jobs
