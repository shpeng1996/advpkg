# Samsung Foundry Reportedly Wins Optical Module Order, Steps Up Silicon Photonics and CPO Drive

**Published:** May 1, 2026 | **Source:** TrendForce

## Background: Optical Communication in AI Data Centers

Traditional electrical transmission using copper cables faces physical limitations and may struggle to support the massive data movement required by next-generation AI infrastructure. As a result, optical transmission technologies are becoming increasingly important.

**TrendForce forecast:** Co-packaged optics (CPO) will steadily increase share of optical communication modules in AI data centers, with penetration **potentially reaching 35% by 2030**.

## Samsung Foundry's Optical Module Win (1Q26 Earnings)

Samsung's foundry division reported in its 1Q26 earnings release that it won an order to manufacture optical communication modules. It expects to start shipments to the customer — reportedly a major optical communication module manufacturer — in the second half of 2026.

## Samsung Foundry Enters Silicon Photonics Market (March 2026)

In **March 2026**, Samsung Electronics' foundry division formally announced its entry into the silicon photonics market:
- Completed a **Process Design Kit (PDK)** — production readiness achieved
- Production carried out on a **300mm wafer platform**
- Initial targets: **Photonic Integrated Circuits (PICs)** for data center optical modules and optical engines for CPO

**Samsung CPO roadmap:**
- Near-term (2026 H2): Optical module shipments begin
- **Target: Turnkey CPO services by 2029**

## Competitive Landscape

**TSMC + NVIDIA:**
- TSMC roadmap: integrate optical engines directly into switch packages with NVIDIA
- Quantum-X Photonics InfiniBand switch for AI cluster interconnects (launched in 2025)
- **Spectrum-X Photonics Ethernet switch** planned for H2 2026 (broader networking)

**Samsung's differentiation:**
- Vertically integrated memory capabilities (DRAM + logic + photonics)
- TSMC does not have same level of memory integration

## Significance

Samsung Foundry's entry into silicon photonics creates a second competitive CPO ecosystem against TSMC-NVIDIA. The 2029 Samsung CPO target aligns with the broader industry timeline for CPO maturity, but Samsung's memory integration advantage could differentiate its offering for memory-centric AI workloads.
