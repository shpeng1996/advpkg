Analyst Ming-Chi Kuo revealed that TSMC is working on CoPoS (Chip-on-Panel-on-Substrate), an advanced packaging architecture that replaces conventional wafer-based manufacturing with panel-level processing.

## Key Findings (Ming-Chi Kuo, June 11, 2026)

**Mass production target:** CoPoS expected to enter mass production in 2H28 (second half of 2028).

**Design scope:** Designed to improve the economics of ultra-large packages above the **9.5x reticle-size class** — i.e., packages too large for CoWoS-based approaches to be cost-effective.

**Critical clarification on glass:** Contrary to some early interpretations, **glass is only used as a temporary carrier during manufacturing** rather than becoming part of the finished package itself. The final substrate remains conventional organic material. The new process aims to reduce waste and improve production efficiency without sacrificing performance.

**Material utilization benefit:** The shift to rectangular panels allows for better material utilization and supports significantly larger package sizes, making it particularly attractive for increasingly complex AI accelerators.

**Relationship to CoWoS:** CoPoS is expected to complement TSMC's existing CoWoS packaging rather than replace it outright.

**Early customer:** NVIDIA's future Feynman AI chips have been floated as potential early CoPoS adopters, given the industry's growing demand for ever-larger AI packages packed with compute chiplets and HBM.

## Industry Context

Making chips smaller has dominated the semiconductor conversation for years, but TSMC's next big leap may come from how those chips are packaged instead. Advanced packaging has quietly become one of the semiconductor industry's hottest battlegrounds.

Advanced packaging accounted for roughly 8% of TSMC's revenues in 2025 and is expected to exceed 10% in 2026.
