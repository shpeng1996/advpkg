# TSMC Accelerates CoPoS Development; Taiwan Suppliers Leverage FOPLP for Glass Core Substrates

Source: TrendForce, republished via I-Connect007.

TrendForce reports that TSMC is standardizing its Chip-on-Panel-on-Substrate (CoPoS) panel-level packaging on a 310×310mm panel format. 2026 is described as the critical validation year for the equipment and materials supply chain, with pilot production targeted for 2027 and mass production in the second half of 2028.

Glass core substrates are identified as TSMC's next major focus area after CoPoS, with commercial-scale production not expected until after 2030.

## Technical hurdles — Through-Glass-Via (TGV)

Key technical challenges cited for TGV processing include:
- Inconsistent via dimensions caused by laser energy fluctuation during drilling
- Microscopic glass cracks introduced during laser drilling
- Difficulty achieving sub-10µm via metallization due to limited etchant penetration into the via
- The need for precise dynamic alignment during high-volume manufacturing (HVM)

## Material challenges

- Maintaining nanometer-level flatness across panels larger than 500×500mm
- CTE (coefficient of thermal expansion) mismatch between heterogeneous material layers causing warpage

## Taiwan's first-mover advantage in FOPLP

Taiwan panel makers are already at volume production for Fan-Out Panel-Level Packaging (FOPLP), primarily for mature-node PMICs and RF components, using large 620×750mm package sizes. This leverages fully-depreciated large-generation display production lines, giving Taiwan suppliers a cost and capacity head start.

Taiwan's materials and equipment ecosystem has reportedly made progress on:
- Low-temperature-curing dielectrics (process temperatures below 180°C), already qualified by major international IDMs with growing shipment volumes
- Two-step via formation combining laser modification and chemical etching
