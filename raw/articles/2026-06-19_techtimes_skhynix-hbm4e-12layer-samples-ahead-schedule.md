# SK hynix Ships 12-Layer HBM4E Samples Ahead of Schedule, Tightening the Race With Samsung

SK hynix shipped 12-layer HBM4E samples ahead of schedule on June 18, 2026, reaching 48GB per stack, up to 16Gbps per pin, about 4TB/s of bandwidth, and 20%-plus better power efficiency versus the prior generation. The shipment comes well ahead of the timeline outlined during SK hynix's Q1 2026 earnings call in April, when the company said it planned to provide HBM4E samples in the second half of the year.

The early sampling tightens the race with Samsung, which sent its own 12-layer HBM4E samples to customers on May 29, 2026. SK hynix said its experience mass-producing and supplying HBM3, HBM3E, and HBM4 enabled it to accelerate development of the new product.

On the technical side, SK hynix adopted its sixth-generation 10-nanometer-class "1c" DRAM process for HBM4E — the first use of this node in an HBM product. Previous generations, including HBM4, used the fifth-generation "1b" DRAM process. The company also improved heat resistance by 17% compared to HBM4, enabling more stable operation in high-performance computing environments.
