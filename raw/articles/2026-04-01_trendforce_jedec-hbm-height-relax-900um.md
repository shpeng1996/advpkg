JEDEC (Joint Electron Device Engineering Council) is reportedly considering relaxing standards governing next-generation HBM. According to Chosun Ilbo, sources indicate JEDEC is weighing a plan to raise HBM height specifications to around 900 micrometers (µm). The new standards are expected to take effect starting with seventh-generation HBM4E.

For reference:
- HBM3E (5th gen): height spec ~720 µm
- HBM4 (6th gen): height spec ~775 µm
- HBM4E (7th gen, proposed): height spec ~900 µm

This represents a notable relaxation in height constraints.

## Impact on Production

Industry observers say easing the standards may help relieve production bottlenecks for memory makers and support faster mass production of next-generation products. The need to process ultra-thin DRAM wafers may decline, while error management during stacking could also become less complex.

## Impact on Hybrid Bonding Adoption

A significant relaxation of height standards could delay the adoption of hybrid bonders. With looser specifications, existing TC bonders may still support higher layer counts, allowing Hanmi Semiconductor (dominant TC bonder supplier) to maintain its leading position.

Scaling HBM to up to 20 layers will require thinner DRAM, pushing against technical limits. One solution is hybrid bonding equipment. While conventional TC bonders connect layers using micro-bumps, hybrid bonders enable direct chip-to-chip bonding without bumps, reducing spacing and allowing more DRAM layers within the same height constraints.

The hybrid bonding market is currently led by Netherlands-based BESI. In Korea, Hanwha Semitech plans to supply its second-generation hybrid bonder to customers in the first half of 2026 for performance testing.

## Industry Consensus: Hybrid Bonding Still Inevitable

Industry consensus holds that hybrid bonding will ultimately be unavoidable. Lee Kang-wook, VP of Package Development at SK hynix, stated that hybrid bonding will be essential for HBM stacks of 20 layers or more.

Samsung Electronics recently showcased its hybrid bonding technology at NVIDIA's GTC 2026, claiming over 20% improvement in thermal resistance versus TC bonding and support for stacks exceeding 16 layers. Samsung has also begun introducing new inspection equipment for hybrid bonding and is discussing adoption with NVIDIA.

## BESI Market Position Context

BESI is widely regarded as the leading company in hybrid bonder technology. Competition is emerging from Korean companies including LG Electronics, SEMES, Hanmi Semiconductor, and Hanwha Semitech, as well as Singapore-based ASMPT.
