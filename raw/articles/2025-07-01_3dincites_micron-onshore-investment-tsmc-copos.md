# IFTLE 634: Micron Invests Onshore; TSMC CoPoS

## Micron Onshore Investment Expansion

Micron, the sole U.S.-based manufacturer of advanced memory chips, has announced expanded additions to their previously announced CHIPS Act investment. The overall investment includes:
- Construction of a second chip fabrication facility in Boise, Idaho
- Modernizing its Manassas, Virginia, facility
- Onshoring production of its advanced DRAM technology from Taiwan for the first time

This facility will improve supply chain resiliency for U.S. automotive, industrial markets, and the defense industrial base. Micron's expanded $200 billion investment also includes up to two additional fabrication facilities in New York.

Previously, the Department of Commerce awarded up to $6.165 billion in CHIPS Act direct funding (December 10, 2024) for three fabrication facilities in Idaho and New York. The expanded $200B investment is accompanied by up to $275MM in incremental CHIPS Act direct funding — an additional $30B beyond previous commitments.

The company will keep $150 billion for manufacturing and $50 billion for R&D. The Idaho sites will accelerate development of HBM products and allow it to meet rising global demand for DRAM chips essential in AI and HPC.

## TSMC CoPoS Update

TSMC's CoWoS is moving ahead with its Chip-on-Panel-on-Substrate (CoPoS) technology amid strong AI demand. TSMC's first CoPoS pilot line is set for 2026, with mass production targeted for 2029.

TSMC's CoPoS is essentially a square-panel evolution of CoWoS-L and CoWoS-R, replacing the traditional round wafer with a rectangular substrate. Measuring 310 x 310mm, the rectangular design offers more usable substrate space than traditional round wafers, boosting output and theoretically cutting costs.

## AP7 Campus Architecture

TSMC's AP7 site in Chiayi is the key hub for next-gen advanced packaging:
- **Phase 1 (P1)**: Dedicated WMCM (multi-chip module) base for Apple
- **Phases 2–3**: SoIC production ramp
- **Phase 4**: Large-scale CoPoS production
- Note: CoWoS production NOT planned for AP7 — remains at AP8

TSMC will establish a CoPoS pilot line in 2026 at its Visionchip subsidiary. In 2027, the pilot facility will focus on refining the process to meet partner requirements by year-end. Mass production is projected between end of 2028 and early 2029.

## Competitive Advantage

The extra surface area makes it possible to integrate additional HBM stacks, multiple I/O chiplets, and compute dies in a single package. Unlike wafer-level packaging (WLP), panel-level packaging (PLP) assembles components on large rectangular panels, delivering higher throughput and lower cost per unit.

NVIDIA is expected to be the launch partner for CoPoS — the company plans to leverage the larger panel area to accommodate up to 12 HBM4 chips alongside several GPU chiplets. AMD and Broadcom will reportedly continue using TSMC's CoWoS-L and CoWoS-R for their high-end products.
