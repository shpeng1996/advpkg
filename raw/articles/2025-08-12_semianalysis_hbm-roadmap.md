# Scaling the Memory Wall: The Rise and Roadmap of HBM

SemiAnalysis describes HBM as the dominant on-package memory for leading AI accelerators, balancing capacity, bandwidth, and energy efficiency better than SRAM, DDR, or other DRAM options. The public preview emphasizes that all leading GenAI training and inference accelerators use HBM, and that roadmaps are scaling capacity and bandwidth through more stacks, higher layer counts, and faster HBM generations.

## Publicly Accessible Extracted Points

- HBM combines vertically stacked DRAM chips with ultra-wide data paths.
- HBM3E requires more than 1,000 wires between XPU and HBM, which exceeds what PCB or ordinary package substrates can route.
- A 2.5D package assembly such as CoWoS is therefore required for high-density HBM integration.
- HBM4 brings a major architectural change around custom base dies, shoreline area, memory-controller offload, repeater PHYs, and LPDDR+HBM combinations.
- The public preview also flags China domestic production, Samsung qualification, and HBM stack-count decisions as major analysis areas.
