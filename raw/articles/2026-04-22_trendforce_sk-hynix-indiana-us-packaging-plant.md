SK hynix has officially broken ground on its first semiconductor plant in the United States—the Indiana fab—marking a major milestone roughly two years after unveiling its 2024 investment plan.

The facility is scheduled to begin full-scale operations in the second half of 2028, primarily targeting mass production of next-generation AI memory products, including HBM. Under the roadmap, 7th- and 8th-generation HBM—namely HBM4E and HBM5—are expected to become the core products manufactured at the site.

SK hynix notified local communities on April 17 that it has begun foundation piling work (driving piles into the ground ahead of structural building work). With piling expected to continue for several months, the company is widely seen as moving toward vertical construction as early as the second half of 2026.

SK hynix has committed approximately $3.87 billion (about KRW 5.2 trillion) to build an AI memory-focused advanced packaging base in Indiana. Simultaneously, the company is investing roughly KRW 19 trillion in Cheongju, South Korea, for P&T7.

## DRAM Capacity Ramp-Up Accelerates in Parallel

SK hynix plans to begin equipment installation at its M15X fab in Cheongju once the Phase 4 cleanroom opens. The first fab in the Yongin semiconductor cluster is set to complete its cleanroom in February 2027. SK hynix plans to install around 20 extreme ultraviolet (EUV) lithography systems from ASML across key production lines, reinforcing its shift toward next-generation DRAM manufacturing processes.
