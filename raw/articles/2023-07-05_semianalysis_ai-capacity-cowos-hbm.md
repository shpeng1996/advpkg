# AI Capacity Constraints - CoWoS and HBM Supply Chain

SemiAnalysis identifies CoWoS and HBM as the primary supply bottlenecks for AI accelerator growth during the first generative-AI demand surge.

## Publicly Accessible Extracted Points

- HBM and CoWoS are complementary: HBM's pad count and short-trace requirements demand dense 2.5D packaging.
- CoWoS-S is the high-density silicon-interposer option, while CoWoS-R trades density for lower-cost organic RDL.
- CoWoS-L uses an RDL interposer plus a local silicon bridge, making it conceptually similar to Intel EMIB.
- The public preview argues SoIC is not a good fit for HBM because thermal and cost constraints favor side-by-side 2.5D integration.
- AI demand absorbed available slack in CoWoS/HBM because both technologies were already majority AI-facing.
- TSMC's Advanced Backend Fab 6 in Zhunan is described as a key response to packaging demand.
