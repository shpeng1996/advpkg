The 2026 IEEE Electronic Components and Technology Conference (ECTC) showcased how advanced packaging can redefine the scalability limits of artificial intelligence (AI) and high-performance computing (HPC). Across 20 technical papers, Intel Foundry engineers and collaborators highlighted breakthrough innovations — from EMIB-T enabling large multi-die systems, to CPO delivering dramatically higher bandwidth at reduced power, to future glass substrates that unlock larger form factors.

## Advancing EMIB-T Scaling Capabilities

EMIB-T leverages embedded silicon bridges for high-density lateral 2.5D interconnect, combined with through-silicon via (TSV)-enabled vertical scaling to enhance power delivery. This allows Intel Foundry to construct package-scale systems that function as a single large device while being composed of many optimized chiplets.

Key EMIB-T achievements at ECTC 2026:
- FLI bump pitch scaled down to **25 µm**
- Package form factor increased to **120 × 120 mm**
- Integration of more than **9x reticles** of compute and memory Si content on a single package
- Co-optimized signal and power integrity delivering over **12 Gb/s for HBM4e** and **64 Gb/s UCIe** interface performance
- Collaboration with Siliconware Precision Industries (SPIL): 3D integration of SRAM chiplet in fan-out embedded bridge
- Package architectures for hyper large form-factors for AI and HPC
- Enabling over 12 Gb/s HBM4e with EMIB-T

## Co-packaged Optics (CPO) for Improved Bandwidth

CPO addresses fundamental system-level problems when data center bandwidth scales faster than electrical interconnect can support. By moving optical-to-electrical signal conversion directly to the package, CPO enables higher bandwidth, lower power, and more scalable systems.

Intel Foundry key CPO research at ECTC 2026:
- **High-performance detachable edge optical connector**: glass fan-out couplers + expanded-beam edge connectors; low-loss, compatible with high volume manufacturing; suitable for wafer-level and package-level assembly
- **V-groove based edge coupling glass coupler interface**: rigid optical glass coupler attached to V-grooves in photonic integrated circuits (PICs) — simpler and more robust than fiber-only approach
- Die-to-die attach with low thermal input thermo-compression bonding

## Next-Gen Glass Substrates for Larger Package Sizes

Traditional organic core substrates are reaching their limit for scaling. Intel Foundry's research on glass core substrates focuses on removing this base material as a scaling bottleneck. Glass enables higher performance heterogeneous integration, better power delivery and stability, and low warpage for large AI and HPC packages.

Key glass substrate research at ECTC 2026:
- Advanced through-glass vias (TGVs): from low taper TGVs to mixed TGV sizes to cavities for embedding devices
- Fully copper-filled TGVs survive very harsh thermal cycling without failure (demonstrated reliability)
- Glass stays flat and stable — direct support for continued scaling of multi-chiplet systems

## Advanced Packaging — Hybrid Bonding Research

Intel Foundry and collaborators also presented hybrid bonding research:
- Collaboration with Fouriers Scientific: thermal behavior of hybrid bonding interfaces
- Collaboration with NIST: probing plasticity in hybrid bonding copper via atomic force microscopy
- Die distortion in die-to-wafer hybrid bonding
- Ultra-low temperature hybrid bonding for die-to-wafer scaling
- Collaboration with EV Group: Advanced metrology for heterogeneous chiplet integration with 100% overlay control at high speed
- Selective layer transfer for fine-grain mixed front-end devices
- Infrared laser debond process modeling
- Large form factor patch on interposer (PoINT) scaling
- Fluxless thermo-compression bonding of 4x reticle die stack
