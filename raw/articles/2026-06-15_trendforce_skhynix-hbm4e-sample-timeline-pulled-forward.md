SK hynix has reportedly pulled forward its HBM4E sample shipment timeline, now targeting June/July 2026 for delivering samples to key customers — earlier than the market had previously expected.

Samsung's competing HBM4E specifications reportedly center on a 1c+4nm process combination, targeting 14–16 Gbps pin speed and roughly 4TB/s of bandwidth per stack.

SK hynix's HBM4E is expected to pair a 1c DRAM core with a TSMC 3nm logic base die, continuing the foundry-logic-die partnership established with HBM4.

NVIDIA's upcoming Rubin Ultra platform is expected to push HBM capacity to roughly 384GB per GPU next year, underscoring the bandwidth and capacity pressure driving the accelerated HBM4E timelines across all three major memory suppliers (SK hynix, Samsung, Micron).

The pulled-forward schedule reflects intensifying competition among memory makers to secure design wins with AI accelerator customers ahead of the broader HBM4E ramp expected in 2027.
