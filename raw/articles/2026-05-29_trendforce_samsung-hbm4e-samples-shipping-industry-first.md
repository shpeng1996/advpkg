# Samsung Starts Shipping Industry-First HBM4E Samples 3 Months After HBM4 Ramp; Performance Up 20%+

**Published:** 2026-05-29 | **Source:** TrendForce / Samsung Press Release

## Summary

Just months after rolling out HBM4 shipments in early 2026, Samsung has begun providing samples of the industry's first 12-layer HBM4E to major global partners, according to Samsung's press release. Given that HBM4 shares the same 1c DRAM process and 4nm base die architecture as HBM4E, and is already in mass production, industry observers suggest the newly shipped HBM4E samples are also well positioned to transition into mass production. Samsung plans to proceed with HBM4E mass production in line with client-specific timelines.

## HBM4E Technical Specifications

| Specification | HBM4E | vs. HBM4 |
|---|---|---|
| Pin speed (stable) | 14 Gbps | >20% improvement |
| Pin speed (peak) | 16 Gbps | (for demanding AI workloads) |
| Bandwidth per stack | 3.6 TB/s | >20% higher |
| Capacity (12-layer) | 48 GB | >30% higher than HBM4 |
| Energy efficiency | — | +16% improvement |
| Thermal resistance | — | >14% reduction |
| DRAM process | 1c | same as HBM4 |
| Base die | 4nm | same as HBM4 |

**Planned lineup:** 32 GB (8-layer), 48 GB (12-layer), 64 GB (16-layer)

## Industry Context

Samsung delivered world's first HBM4 commercial shipment in February 2026, achieving 11.7 Gbps in SiP (System-in-Package) testing. HBM4E uses the same architecture and is the industry's first HBM4E sample shipment — approximately 3 months after HBM4 mass production ramp.

Samsung notes: integrated strengths across memory, foundry, and advanced packaging expected to be a key differentiator for HBM4E (customer-specific design flexibility critical from HBM4 generation onward).

## Competitive Status

- **SK Hynix:** Originally planned HBM4E sample shipments in H2 2026; recent reports indicate smoother-than-expected development, bringing forward timeline
- **Micron:** First HBM4E product follows JEDEC standards; mass production targeted for 2027

## Key Claims (TrendForce / Samsung)

1. Samsung is industry's first to supply HBM4E samples (May 2026)
2. HBM4E delivers >20% performance improvement over HBM4 with same 1c+4nm process
3. 48 GB (12-layer) capacity is >30% higher than HBM4
4. 16% energy efficiency improvement, >14% thermal resistance reduction
5. SK Hynix HBM4E timeline likely pulled forward from H2 2026
6. Micron HBM4E mass production remains on 2027 timeline
