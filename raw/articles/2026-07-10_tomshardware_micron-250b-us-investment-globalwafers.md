# Micron lifts U.S. spending to $250 billion

**Published:** July 10, 2026 | **Author:** Luke James | **Source:** Tom's Hardware

## Key Announcements (July 10, 2026)

### Investment Commitments
- Raised planned U.S. spending to **more than $250 billion through 2035**, up from $200 billion (announced June 2025)
- Confirmed first concrete pour at **Clay, New York campus** more than a quarter ahead of schedule
- Ultimate goal: **40% of DRAM produced in the U.S.**
- **$3 billion** total for U.S. IC supply chain; **$500 million** of that to GlobalWafers (Sherman, Texas)
- **10-year wafer supply agreement** with GlobalWafers securing access to 300mm raw silicon

### GlobalWafers Partnership
- Sherman, Texas = **sole operating 300mm raw silicon wafer facility in the U.S.**
- GlobalWafers holds $406M CHIPS Act award (Sherman + St. Peters, Missouri SOI facility)
- 142-acre campus designed for up to 6 phases; currently 1 phase operational
- Micron's other U.S. sites currently draw wafers from Japan, Taiwan, Germany, South Korea

### Supply Chain Context
- Silicon wafer shipments: 3,275 million sq in in Q1 2026, up **13.1% YoY** (AI datacenter demand)
- Wafer suppliers (SUMCO, Shin-Etsu, GlobalWafers, Siltronic, SK Siltron) control overwhelming majority of 300mm supply
- SUMCO winding down 200mm production at Miyazaki while holding off new 300mm expansion
- Micron ID1 (Idaho fab): expected to begin wafer output **mid-2027**
- Clay, NY production: not expected until **~2030**
- Micron Virginia fab: began producing 1-alpha DRAM in May 2026

### Ford Agreement
- Long-term strategic customer agreement with Ford for memory and storage for next-gen vehicles

### Demand Context
- Micron told investors in December 2025: can serve only **half to two-thirds of customer demand**
- Nothing in July 2026 announcements changes DRAM supply position for 2026/2027
- $100 billion in long-term supply agreements (SCA) already signed
