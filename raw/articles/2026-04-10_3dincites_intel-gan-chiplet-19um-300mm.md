# Intel Foundry Achieves Breakthrough with World's Thinnest GaN Chiplet Technology

**Author:** Han Wui Then et al., Intel Foundry Technology Research  
**Published:** 2026-04-10 (3D InCites / IMAPS)  
**Originally presented at:** IEEE IEDM 2025

## Highlights

- Intel Foundry has created the world's thinnest gallium nitride (GaN) chiplet — base silicon measuring just **19 micrometers (µm) thick** — harvested from a 300 mm GaN-on-silicon wafer.
- Researchers have successfully combined GaN transistors with traditional silicon-based digital circuits on a single chip (fully monolithic on-die digital control circuits), allowing complex computing functions to be built directly into power chiplets without needing separate companion chiplets.
- Rigorous testing confirms this new GaN chiplet technology is a promising candidate for real-world deployment — enabling smaller, more efficient electronics for data centers, 5G/6G communications, radar, and photonics.

## Technology Details

**Why GaN?**  
GaN (gallium nitride) is a compound semiconductor with exceptional properties: handles much higher voltages than silicon, switches far faster, loses significantly less energy in the process. Ideal for power conversion and high-frequency RF applications.

**Key innovation:**  
- Ultra-thin GaN chiplet (19 µm base silicon — ~1/5 width of a human hair) harvested from 300 mm GaN-on-silicon wafers
- Industry's first fully monolithic on-die digital control circuits integrated in the same manufacturing process
- Eliminates need for a separate companion chiplet, reducing energy losses from inter-chip signal routing
- Built on 300 mm wafers (industry-standard), enabling potential high-volume production using existing silicon infrastructure

**Manufacturing approach:**  
Thinning to 19 µm requires new process flows. Intel Foundry uses their 300 mm infrastructure for GaN growth, enabling cost-competitive HVM. The integration with CMOS digital logic on the same die is the novel step.

## Applications

| Application | Benefit |
|-------------|---------|
| Data center VRMs | Faster switching, less energy loss, smaller voltage regulators closer to processor |
| 5G/6G base stations | GaN operates at >200 GHz — ideal for cm-wave and mm-wave bands |
| Radar & satellite comms | High-frequency switching performance |
| Photonics | Fast electrical switching to modulate light signals |

## Market Significance

This is a chiplet-based approach to power delivery — packaging GaN power management directly into the heterogeneous integration ecosystem. Rather than discrete GaN power modules, this enables GaN chiplets co-packaged with AI compute dies (e.g., in CoWoS or Foveros packages), potentially improving power delivery efficiency for 5–6 kW AI server racks.

## Key Specs
- **Die thickness**: 19 µm (silicon base)
- **Wafer diameter**: 300 mm (GaN-on-Si)
- **Integration**: Monolithic GaN + CMOS digital control on single die
- **Presented**: IEEE IEDM 2025
