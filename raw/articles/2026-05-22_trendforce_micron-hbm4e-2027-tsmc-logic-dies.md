Two months after its March earnings call, Micron is turning more upbeat on its outlook, while providing additional details on its custom HBM development progress. At the J.P. Morgan 54th Annual Global Technology, Media and Communications Conference, Micron's Global Operations EVP Manish Bhatia said the company's first HBM4E will be a JEDEC-standard product, with ramp-up scheduled for 2027.

While Micron is still using 1-beta DRAM for HBM4, the company is expected to transition to 1-gamma DRAM in the HBM4E era. The logic dies for both standard and custom HBM4E are expected to be manufactured by TSMC.

## Improving Outlook vs. Previous Earnings Call

Micron now expects tight conditions across HBM, DRAM, and NAND to persist well beyond 2026. The financial outlook has strengthened since the company's last earnings call, and it is on track for another substantial record free cash flow in fiscal Q3.

Demand remains very strong. The AI ecosystem is shifting from human interactions to agentic and machine-to-machine workflows, with these agentic workloads increasingly driving inference demand. As inference takes up a larger share of workloads, memory is increasingly seen as a strategic asset for customers.

Micron has signed a first strategic customer agreement (5-year deal, signed March 2026). Since then, meaningful progress on additional SCAs, with other customers showing strong interest in establishing similar strategic relationships.

## Global Capacity Expansion

- **Idaho 1 site**: wafer output timeline pulled forward from 2H27 to mid-2027
- **India Sanand (Gujarat)**: facility began operations late February 2026; entire capacity fully booked; at full capacity could account for up to 10% of Micron's global output
- **Memory shortage outlook**: could extend well beyond 2028 despite aggressive capacity expansion

Customization represents the next evolution of value expansion; as value increases, customers willing to pay more for customization (higher margins expected).
