## Event Overview

COMPUTEX 2026: June 2–5, Taipei. >30 companies CEOs; >US$10 trillion combined market value. Theme: AI Together.

## CPO and AI Infrastructure Highlights

- **Wiwynn + Ayar Labs**: Will showcase latest CPO optical interconnect technology at COMPUTEX 2026.
- **Largan Precision**: COMPUTEX debut with glass components for **fiber array units (FAUs)** — highlighting CPO progress. (Largan traditionally known as Apple lens supplier.)
- **MediaTek**: Demonstrating **400Gbps-per-fiber CPO technology** and MicroLED-based optical solutions for active optical cables (AOCs). Copper-like reliability while cutting power consumption by **50%** vs copper.
- CPO and AI servers are key themes; attention on NVIDIA's Vera Rubin GPU platform entering mass production 2H26.

## AI Bottlenecks

- Wiwynn expects key component shortages to ease only by end of 2027.
- CPU shortages emerging from rapid AI inference, agentic platforms, networking chip demand.
- Beijing AI/chipmaking ambitions remain closely watched by semiconductor industry.

## Intel and Qualcomm

- Intel (CEO Lip-Bu Tan): Regaining momentum; share price reportedly +5× within a year; COMPUTEX keynote.
- Qualcomm: Rising challenger to NVIDIA in AI infrastructure; deal with ByteDance for data center chips (Bloomberg).
- Intel Core 3 "Wildcat Lake" budget CPU (18A process, same as Panther Lake) for Chromebooks/mini PCs.
- Qualcomm Snapdragon C processors for $300 laptops.

## NVIDIA at COMPUTEX / GTC Taipei

- GTC Taipei: June 1, Taipei Music Center.
- Jensen Huang unveiled: NVIDIA RTX Spark (AI PC platform, N1X Arm CPU co-designed with MediaTek, TSMC 3nm).
- Vera Rubin GPU platform entering mass production 2H26.
- NVIDIA N1 mainstream + N1X flagship consumer PC processors debuting.
- 2H26 "surprise new products" hinted by Jensen Huang.

## Robotics

- First dedicated AI robotics zone at COMPUTEX 2026; ~60 exhibitors.
