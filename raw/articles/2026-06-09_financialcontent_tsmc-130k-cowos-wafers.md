# TSMC to Quadruple Advanced Packaging Capacity: Reaching 130,000 CoWoS Wafers Monthly by Late 2026

By TokenRing AI — February 05, 2026

TSMC has finalized plans to expand advanced packaging capacity to 130,000 CoWoS (Chip-on-Wafer-on-Substrate) wafers per month by late 2026 — nearly quadrupling output from late-2024 levels — to relieve AI hardware bottlenecks.

## Key claims and data points

- Target: 130,000 CoWoS wafers/month by late 2026 (~4x late-2024 output).
- TSMC is ramping CoWoS-L (Local Silicon Interconnect / silicon-bridge based) production to link reticle-exceeding multi-die GPUs (e.g., NVIDIA Blackwell's two-die design).
- Early Blackwell production faced CTE (coefficient of thermal expansion) mismatch/warpage issues; TSMC has refined processes at AP6 (Zhunan) and the newly acquired AP8 (Tainan) facility to improve yields.
- Chiayi AP7 complex positioned to become the world's largest advanced-packaging hub, with phases coming online through 2027.
- 2026 strategy emphasizes SoIC ("System-on-Integrated-Chips") — treating the package as a unified system integrating logic, memory and power delivery in 3D stacks.
- Estimated 2026 CoWoS capacity allocation: NVIDIA ~60%, Broadcom ~15% (custom AI ASICs for Google/Meta), AMD ~11% (Instinct MI350/MI400), with Marvell and AWS also competing for space.
- Competitive landscape: Intel positions glass-core substrates against organic-interposer warpage; Samsung pushes a "Turnkey" HBM+packaging offering. TSMC's edge: deep OSAT integration (e.g., ASE) and existing supply-chain depth.
- Chips noted to draw upwards of 1,000W, pushing focus toward liquid cooling and power delivery innovation.
- Long-term roadmap: HBM4-era atomic-scale bonding precision, hybrid bonding to eliminate solder bumps, eventual transition to glass substrates and "wafer-level systems."

## New knowledge added

- Concrete 130K wafers/month figure (vs. previously tracked range of ~115-140K) and explicit "quadrupling from late-2024" framing — useful as a single anchor figure for capacity tracking.
- Specific 2026 capacity-allocation percentage breakdown across NVIDIA/Broadcom/AMD/Marvell/AWS — not previously captured at this granularity.
- Naming of AP8 (Tainan) as a newly acquired advanced-backend facility alongside AP6 (Zhunan) and Chiayi AP7.
