## SK hynix Reportedly to Supply About Two-Thirds of NVIDIA HBM4; Samsung Targets Early Delivery

*Published: 2026-01-28 | Source: TrendForce | Citing: Yonhap News, Hankyung, Dealsite, EBN*

### SK Hynix Secures ~70% of NVIDIA Rubin HBM4

According to Yonhap News, sources say NVIDIA is expected to allocate roughly two-thirds of its HBM4 demand for the Vera Rubin platform to SK hynix, with the company's share understood to be close to 70%—well above earlier expectations of just over 50%.

Analysts cite confidence in SK hynix's long-standing HBM partnerships with major customers, including NVIDIA, as well as trust built on consistently high yields in large-scale production. As the HBM market moves beyond pure technology competition, stable quality and mass-production capability are increasingly recognized as core competitive advantages.

SK hynix put its HBM4 mass-production system in place in September 2025 and has since provided NVIDIA with large volumes of paid samples that cleared final validation without issues. SK hynix is moving toward full-scale production to meet major customer schedules.

### Samsung Mounts HBM4 Counteroffensive

According to Hankyung, Samsung is poised to begin official HBM4 shipments to major AI chip customers including NVIDIA and AMD, following successful completion of final qualification tests.

Samsung's technical differentiators:
- **DRAM process**: 6th-generation (1c) DRAM on 10nm-class process — one generation ahead of competitors
- **Logic die**: 4nm foundry process — several generations ahead in control-core integration
- **Pricing**: Samsung sought pricing parity with SK hynix on HBM4; similar price levels achieved

According to Dealsite, SK hynix and Samsung are set to supply HBM4 to NVIDIA at comparable prices. Although Samsung priced its 12-layer HBM3E products around 30% below SK hynix, it achieved pricing parity in HBM4. Industry sources expect prices for 12-layer HBM4 products to **exceed $600**.

### Market Share Outlook

Rising AI chip demand from NVIDIA, AMD, Google, and Microsoft has pushed HBM demand beyond expectations, lifting prices for HBM3E and HBM4.

Samsung Electronics' share of the global HBM market is expected to surpass 30% in 2026 (EBN). Backed by partnerships with AMD and Google, Samsung could see a sharper rise in HBM sales if AI chip demand from these customers accelerates.
