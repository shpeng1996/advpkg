## Abstract

Hybrid bonding is reshaping advanced packaging by enabling ultra-fine pitch copper-to-copper interconnects, essential for high-bandwidth memory (HBM), chiplet integration, and 3D heterogeneous systems. While alignment precision, oxide control, and chemical mechanical planarization (CMP) techniques have been widely studied, copper grain structure remains a less explored but equally critical factor.

The way copper grains are sized and distributed forms the metallurgical foundation of hybrid bonding, enabling lower bonding temperatures, greater reliability, and stable grain structures throughout integration.

## Connecting the Future with Hybrid Bonding

Hybrid bonding is an advanced 3D integration technique that directly joins copper interconnects and dielectric surfaces between stacked chips or wafers. Unlike traditional flip-chip microbump bonding, typically limited to pitches of 10–50 µm, hybrid Cu/SiO₂ bonding can achieve ultra-fine pitches below 10 µm and is extending toward the submicron scale.

By eliminating the need for solder bumps and underfill, hybrid bonding dramatically increases vertical interconnect density, reduces signal delay, and lowers power loss, enabling smaller, faster, and more energy-efficient 3D packages.

First deployed in HVM for stacking CMOS image sensor wafers, hybrid bonding has since expanded into 3D NAND memory and logic-on-memory integration (e.g., AMD's 3D V-Cache), demonstrating its value in high-bandwidth designs.

## Why Hybrid Bonding Presents Unique Challenges

The industry's push toward smaller nodes, higher bandwidth, and denser integration is accelerating the shift to hybrid bonding. While it enables sub-2 µm pitch interconnects with excellent electrical and thermal performance, large-scale adoption is constrained by several critical challenges:

**Surface Preparation and Process Control**: Achieving a reliable copper-to-copper bond at fine pitch requires exceptional control over surface preparation, alignment, and process conditions. Bonding surfaces must be atomically flat, with nanometer-scale planarity and carefully controlled copper recess.

**Stringent Alignment Tolerances**: Accuracy within just a few nanometers is required to ensure reliable copper-to-copper contact. Even slight misalignment can cause electrical open circuits, signal degradation, or mechanical failures.

**Copper Grain Structure**: While extensively studied for barrier and seed layer applications, copper grain behavior at the bonding interface is under-characterized for hybrid bonding.

## Copper Grain Engineering Strategies

Key strategies for controlling grain size to enhance bond quality, minimize voids, and reduce thermal requirements for HVM include:

1. **Fine-grain copper deposition**: Using additives to control nucleation density and grain size during electroplating
2. **Annealing optimization**: Controlling post-deposition annealing to achieve stable grain structure before bonding
3. **CMP surface conditioning**: Ensuring grain uniformity at the bonding surface
4. **Grain stabilization additives**: Using chemical additives that prevent grain growth during thermal cycling

## Implications for HBM and Chiplet Manufacturing

For HBM applications, where 16-high or 20-high stacks require sub-5µm bump pitches, proper copper grain engineering:
- Reduces void formation during Cu-Cu bonding anneal
- Lowers required bonding temperature (beneficial for thermal budget)
- Improves bond reliability under thermal cycling in AI datacenter environments
- Enables higher yields at ultra-fine pitches approaching 1µm (TSMC's long-term target)
