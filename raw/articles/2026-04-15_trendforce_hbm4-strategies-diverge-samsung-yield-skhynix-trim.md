## HBM4 Strategies Diverge: Samsung Reportedly Chases 80% 1c DRAM Yield While SK hynix Trims Shipments by 30%

*Published: 2026-04-15 | Source: TrendForce | Citing: Chosun Biz, ZDNet, Financial News, Business Post, The Elec*

As memory giants enter the final stage of HBM4 ramp-up ahead of NVIDIA's Rubin launch, they are taking diverging approaches. According to Chosun Biz, Samsung is pushing to lift 1c DRAM yields for HBM4 toward 80%, while ZDNet reports that SK hynix is set to cut HBM4 volumes this year by 20–30%.

### Samsung: Chasing Yield Maturity

Chosun Biz notes that Samsung's yields for HBM4-bound DRAM are still estimated at below 60%, which prompts the company to aim to lift yields toward near-mature levels in the second half of the year to strengthen its ability to serve key AI customers, including NVIDIA. The threshold for mature mass-production yields is generally seen at around 80%.

Despite using the same 1c process, HBM4 DRAM requires extra steps—stacking, advanced packaging, thermal control, and signal integrity tuning—making it far more complex than conventional DRAM. Even if chip-level yields improve, overall yields can still drop during final HBM4 assembly.

Samsung's heavier reliance on EUV lithography—with more layers than rivals—adds process complexity and cost. Without mature yields, the cost burden remains high.

According to Financial News, Samsung has reportedly raised prices for HBM4 logic dies by around 40–50% since early 2026. Samsung's 4nm foundry process for logic dies is operating at higher utilization levels, reducing cost pressure there.

### SK hynix: Cutting HBM4 Outlook on Rubin Delays

SK hynix, the current HBM leader, is set to cut HBM4 shipments by 20–30% from its original target, as NVIDIA's Rubin rollout is delayed (ZDNet). The reduced HBM4 volumes are expected to be offset by increased demand for the prior-generation HBM3E and server DRAM.

Key components of the Rubin platform have yet to be fully optimized. NVIDIA has set a target for HBM4 data processing speeds at around 11Gbps—well above current industry standards. The platform still faces hurdles including a shift in network interconnects from CX8 to CX9, rising power consumption, and the need for more advanced liquid cooling optimization.

**TrendForce GPU platform shift (2026 forecast):**

SK hynix's reduced HBM4 volumes are being redirected to HBM3E and server DRAM (LPDDR), suggesting total memory demand will hold up.

### Micron: Slower HBM4, but Non-HBM Margins Shine

Micron, advancing at a slower pace in HBM4 development, could see initial shipment volumes come under pressure due to ongoing qualification and quality approval hurdles.

However, Micron CEO Sanjay Mehrotra noted that non-HBM margins are currently higher than HBM in the March earnings call, suggesting DDR5 profitability now exceeds that of HBM. Micron projects a gross margin of 81% in 3QFY26, up from a record 75% in the previous quarter.
