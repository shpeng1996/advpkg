## Summary

Leading chip equipment makers—including ASML, Applied Materials, Tokyo Electron (TEL), and Lam Research—are moving decisively into the advanced packaging market in 2026, accelerating investment and new product development.

## Tokyo Electron (TEL) Highlights

### R&D Investment & Kumamoto Hub
- TEL announced a ¥47 billion investment to establish a new advanced packaging equipment development hub at its Kyushu (Kumamoto Prefecture) facility
- New Kumamoto facility scheduled to begin operations in **spring 2026**, located near TSMC's Kumamoto fab
- TEL will allocate more than **¥1.5 trillion ($9.92 billion)** to R&D over five years through March 2029 — a **90% increase** vs. the previous five-year period

### Revenue Mix Shift
- By FY2026, TEL's equipment for advanced chips will rise to nearly **40% of total sales**
- Advanced packaging equipment is a key growth driver, offsetting China revenue slowdown

### Technology Focus
- TEL focuses on **redistribution layer (RDL) processes** — critical for fan-out packaging (InFO, WMCM) and interposer fabrication
- RDL equipment covers: dielectric deposition, Cu plating/CMP, lithography for RDL patterning

### Eteris Joint Venture
- Applied Materials and Tokyo Electron unveiled **Eteris** — a combined company targeting advanced packaging process modules

## ASML's Packaging Role
- ASML's advanced lithography systems are increasingly critical for fine-pitch RDL patterning in fan-out and 2.5D packages
- Sub-2µm RDL lines require DUV (and potentially EUV) exposure capability

## Broader Equipment Landscape

| Equipment Maker | Advanced Packaging Focus |
|----------------|------------------------|
| Tokyo Electron (TEL) | RDL deposition, plating, lithography |
| Applied Materials | Deposition, CMP, hybrid bonding prep; Eteris JV with TEL |
| ASML | RDL lithography (DUV/EUV) |
| Lam Research | Etch, deposition for TSV and RDL |
| Besi | D2W hybrid bonding placement |
| EV Group (EVG) | W2W hybrid bonding, wafer bonding |

## Significance
The convergence of front-end equipment makers into advanced packaging signals that packaging complexity is approaching wafer-fab-level sophistication, requiring semiconductor-grade process equipment.
