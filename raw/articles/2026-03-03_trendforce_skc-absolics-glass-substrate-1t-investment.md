# SKC Reportedly Channels Over Half of ₩1T Capital Increase Into Absolics to Fast-Track Glass Substrates

**Source:** TrendForce, 2026-03-03 (citing Global Economic, The Elec, Chosun Biz, Commercial Times, EE Times Japan, Calian Press)

## Summary

SKC has announced plans to allocate **>₩600 billion (over half of its ₩1 trillion capital increase)** to its glass substrate subsidiary, **Absolics**, to fast-track commercialization of glass core substrates.

## Absolics Strategy

- **Dual-track strategy:**
  1. Embedded solution: targeting high-performance AI data center applications
  2. Non-embedded route: faster commercialization path
- **New CEO:** Kang Ji-ho (previously with Intel and SK hynix) appointed to bolster technical expertise
- **Manufacturing:** World's first dedicated glass substrate manufacturing facility in **Georgia, USA** (completed)
- **Target customers:** NVIDIA and other major fabless players
- **Progress:** Meaningful progress with global big tech clients confirmed

## Global Glass Substrate Race

|---------|--------|

## New Knowledge Added

1. **SKC/Absolics Georgia facility** is the world's **first dedicated glass substrate manufacturing facility** — no other player has a dedicated mass-production facility yet (others are in pilot stage).
2. **Dual-track strategy** (embedded for HPC AI, non-embedded for faster commercialization) is a new commercialization approach not captured in the existing glass-substrate wiki page.
3. **Lens Technology (China)** entering TGV glass substrates — new Chinese player with Apple supply chain connections entering the market.
4. **Google and Microsoft** factoring glass substrate adoption into hardware design from the outset — indicates hyperscaler-level demand validation.
5. **TSMC FOPLP R&D team integrates TGV/glass substrates** with panel-level packaging — this is a CoPoS + glass interposer combination strategy.

## Contradictions / Corrections

The existing wiki glass-substrate page mentions Absolics targeting 2026 mass production. This article clarifies the Georgia facility is **complete** but the target is still securing major orders (not yet mass production). The dual-track strategy (embedded vs non-embedded) adds nuance to the commercialization timeline.
