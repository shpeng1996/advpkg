# Semiconductor Packaging and Testing Capacity Race Intensifies as ASE, Samsung and Amkor Announced New Moves

**Publisher**: TrendForce | **Date**: April 20, 2026

## Summary

Global semiconductor packaging and testing sector has seen a wave of major developments in April 2026, with ASE Group, Samsung Electronics, and Amkor Technology all announcing significant capacity expansion moves.

## Key Developments

### ASE Group — Largest-Ever Fab Construction Program
- ASE has kicked off its largest-ever fab construction program, with **six new facilities slated to break ground worldwide in 2026**
- ASE's K28 advanced packaging facility (targeting 25,000 panels/month for CoWoP) is among the sites progressing

### Samsung Electronics — $4 Billion Vietnam Investment
- Samsung Electronics plans to invest **USD 4 billion** in a new packaging and testing plant in **Vietnam**
- The Vietnam facility reinforces Samsung's OSAT capabilities outside Korea, complementing its advanced packaging work in Korea
- Samsung is also investing in panel-level packaging (FOPLP), choosing **plastic (organic/PCB-type)** materials as its core panel substrate, in contrast to TSMC's glass approach

### Amkor Technology — Continued Expansion
- Amkor announced new moves in packaging capacity expansion, building on its Arizona $7 billion facility targeting 2028 production start
- Amkor's Songdo K5 facility in South Korea continues to ramp EMIB packaging for Intel

## Industry Context

The packaging and testing capacity race reflects surging AI accelerator demand driving foundries and OSATs to rapidly build out advanced packaging infrastructure. TSMC's CoWoS remains the leading advanced packaging technology for AI GPUs, but OSAT players are aggressively expanding to capture overflow demand.

## Market Implications

- Total OSAT advanced packaging capacity expected to grow significantly through 2027
- Vietnam emerging as an important packaging hub in Southeast Asia alongside Malaysia
- Panel-level packaging (FOPLP/CoPoS) investments by all major players signal 2028-2029 as the next capacity battleground
