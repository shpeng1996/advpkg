## Summary

TSMC is advancing its panel-level packaging (PLP) initiative under the technology name **CoPoS** (Chip-on-Panel-on-Substrate). According to TrendForce reporting dated 2026-04-13:

### CoPoS Pilot Line Timeline
- Tool deliveries to R&D teams began in **February 2026**
- Full pilot line on track for completion by **June 2026** at TSMC's **Visionchip subsidiary**
- 2027: Pilot facility focus on refining the process to meet partner requirements by year-end
- Mass production projected to begin between **end of 2028 and early 2029** at TSMC's **Chiayi AP7** facility

### Technology Targets
- CoPoS is primarily aimed at **AI and HPC (High-Performance Computing)** applications
- **NVIDIA** is expected to be the first major customer for CoPoS
- CoPoS uses panel-level (vs. wafer-level) substrates enabling larger die areas and higher throughput vs. conventional CoWoS

### Context: TSMC Advanced Packaging CapEx
- Advanced Packaging CapEx projected to grow at **24% CAGR in 2025–27**
- AP7 (Chiayi) Phase 2 has begun equipment installation and testing; production expected in 2026
- AP7 Phase 1 scheduled for equipment move-in 2026, mass production 2027

### Industry Context
- FOPLP industry players: TSMC (CoPoS), ASE, Powertech Technology
- Powertech FOPLP trial yields reached ~90% (as of 2025-09); targeting >95%
- Resonac partnered with Applied Materials, TEL, and chip giants on panel-level interposers (2025-09)
- Nikon unveiled DSP-100 for panel-level packaging supporting 600mm panels with 9× throughput (2025-07)
