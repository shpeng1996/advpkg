## Key Facts

- Intel and 3DGS plan to invest ~US$3.3 billion to build a substrate manufacturing plant in **Odisha, India** (Bhubaneswar-Khurda region).
- **Buildout timeline**: Five to six years.
- **Production capacity**: ~70,000 glass substrates annually; ~50 million assembled units; ~13,000 advanced 3D heterogeneous-integration modules.
- **Focus**: Advanced packaging glass core substrates, high-density interconnect substrates, related semiconductor technologies.
- Intel-backed 3DGS packaging facility broke ground in April 2026 in Bhubaneswar, Odisha state capital.
- 3DGS founded in 2005 as a U.S. semiconductor technology company.

## Intel's Broader Glass Substrate Push

- **Rio Rancho, New Mexico**: Could become Intel's first volume glass substrate manufacturing site and potentially the **first in the world**. Currently only pilot line in Chandler.
- **India plant**: Complements Rio Rancho with large-scale, lower-cost manufacturing base.

## Global Glass Substrate Race

- **TSMC CoPoS**: Moving from round wafers to square glass/organic substrates; mass production possible as early as 2028.
- **SKC/Absolics**: Expected to begin world's first commercial glass substrate production by end of 2026 (Korea).
- **Samsung Electro-Mechanics**: Glass substrate pilot line at Sejong plant (South Chungcheong); mass production targeted after 2027.
- HAMR (heat-assisted magnetic recording) in AI server HDDs also driving demand for heat-resistant glass substrates.
