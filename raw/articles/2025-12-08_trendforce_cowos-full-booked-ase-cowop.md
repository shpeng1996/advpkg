## Summary

TrendForce (2025-12-08) reports that TSMC's CoWoS-L and CoWoS-S capacity is fully booked, pushing AI chip customers to rely on OSAT partners — with ASE's **CoWoP** (Chip-on-Wafer-on-Panel) technology coming into focus.

### CoWoS Capacity Status (2025-12)
- Both **CoWoS-L** and **CoWoS-S** variants at TSMC are reportedly **fully booked**
- Demand driven primarily by NVIDIA AI accelerators (H100, H200, Blackwell family)
- TSMC's monthly CoWoS capacity: ~75,000–80,000 wafers/month (as of late 2025)
- Target capacity by end of 2026: 120,000–130,000 wafers/month

### ASE's CoWoP Technology
- **CoWoP** = Chip-on-Wafer-on-Panel — ASE's panel-level advanced packaging variant
- ASE's new **Kaohsiung K28 facility** (completion 2026) to expand CoWoS-equivalent capacity
- Projected ASE CoWoS-equivalent capacity: **20,000–25,000 wafers/month** by year-end 2026 (3× current)
- ASE CapEx boost: up to **+16% for 2026**, total ~$3B, covering new areas including final test, burn-in, and system-level test

### Amkor Arizona Fab
- Amkor's expanded US investment raised to **$7B total** (two phases)
- First US-based high-volume advanced packaging facility
- Construction: Phase 1 expected complete mid-2027; production early 2028
- Cleanroom: 750K+ sq ft across both phases
- Technologies: FOCoS (fan-out chip-on-substrate), copper pillar bump, flip-chip BGA for CoWoS-like services and chiplets
- Intel EMIB process already established at Amkor's Songdo K5 (Korea)

### Market Dynamics
- TSMC CoWoS prices reportedly may rise **~20%** due to capacity tightness (2024-11)
- ASE and Amkor competing for outsourced CoWoS-type orders from TSMC overflow
- Chiplet packaging expanding into mobile, automotive, PC segments — OSATs responding
