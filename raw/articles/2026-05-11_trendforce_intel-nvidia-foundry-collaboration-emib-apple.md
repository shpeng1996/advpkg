# Intel CEO Lip-Bu Tan Hints at Exciting New Products With NVIDIA as Foundry Collaboration Rumors Grow

**Published**: 2026-05-11 | **Source**: TrendForce

At Carnegie Mellon University's honorary doctorate ceremony, Intel CEO Lip-Bu Tan personally congratulated Jensen Huang on receiving an honorary doctorate in science and technology, drawing fresh industry attention to the growing ties between Intel and NVIDIA. Tan not only praised Huang's achievement but also revealed that the two companies are working together on "new and exciting products."

## Intel-NVIDIA Joint Development

The remarks come as Intel and NVIDIA continue to deepen cooperation across multiple areas:

- **NVIDIA invested US$5 billion in Intel** in September, and the two companies announced a partnership under which Intel would develop custom data center CPUs for NVIDIA.
- The two companies are expected to jointly develop a **custom Xeon CPU integrated with NVIDIA's NVLink interconnect technology**, giving NVIDIA access to Intel's x86 ecosystem while enabling Intel Xeon SoCs to be paired with NVIDIA's Blackwell and Rubin GPUs for AI hyperscaler platforms.
- Intel is reportedly developing next-generation SoCs, including the rumored **Serpent Lake platform** (arrival ~2028–2029), with NVIDIA providing RTX GPU IP for the graphics portion.

## Advanced Packaging and Foundry Implications

Looking ahead, the relationship could eventually expand into foundry and advanced packaging cooperation:
- With TSMC's capacity remaining heavily booked, Intel Foundry sees opportunities.
- Industry rumors indicate **NVIDIA may be evaluating Intel's 14A and 18A process nodes for the Feynman I/O die**, alongside Intel's **EMIB advanced packaging technology**.
- This would represent a significant breakthrough — Intel's advanced packaging becoming the interconnect for NVIDIA's flagship AI chip.

## Apple-Intel Foundry Agreement

Beyond NVIDIA, Apple and Intel have reached a **preliminary agreement** for Intel to manufacture some of the chips that power Apple devices:
- Intensive talks have reportedly been ongoing for more than a year
- The two sides are said to have reached a formal agreement in recent months
- This is a significant step for Intel Foundry's external customer strategy
