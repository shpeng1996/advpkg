Intel has announced a €5 billion (US$5.7 billion) upgrade of its Ireland campus (Fab 34, Leixlip). The advanced manufacturing equipment now being installed at the site will support production of Intel Xeon 6 processors as well as next-generation Intel Xeon chips built on Intel 3. Naga Chandrasekaran (EVP, Chief Technology and Operations Officer and General Manager, Intel Foundry) noted that Intel 3 is the most advanced technology manufactured in Europe.

Intel 3 will power the I/O tiles of Intel's upcoming Diamond Rapids Xeon 7 processor. Intel's current Granite Rapids (Xeon 6P) already relies on Intel 3, while Clearwater Forest (Xeon 6+) transitions to the 18A node.

## Investment Scale

- €5B = ~30% of Intel's planned US$17B capex for 2026
- Intel has invested ~€30B in Leixlip campus over 40 years
- Bulk of new funding to be deployed by 2027
- Fab 34 (opened 2023): Intel's EUV-enabled manufacturing facility
- In April 2026: Intel reacquired 50% stake in Ireland facility from Apollo Global Management ($14.2B), reflecting growing confidence in recovery

## TSMC's European Approach (Comparison)

- TSMC Dresden fab: 12–16nm and 22–28nm (mature nodes), targeting automotive applications, production end-2027
- Infineon (ESMC shareholder) pushing TSMC for second Dresden fab with more advanced processes
- Intel Ireland Intel 3 = significantly more advanced than TSMC Dresden

## Context: Intel Foundry Momentum

Intel's foundry business is gaining momentum amid soaring AI processor demand. The investment follows Intel's growing confidence in its recovery and its push to secure a bigger role in the booming AI infrastructure market.
