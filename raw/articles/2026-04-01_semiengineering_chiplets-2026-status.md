## Summary

Semiconductor Engineering's 2026 chiplet landscape overview examines where the industry stands across technology maturity, standardisation, supply chain, and geopolitical dimensions.

## Key Claims

### Chiplets as Geopolitical Chokepoint
- Deloitte forecasts chiplets will become a **major geopolitical chokepoint in 2026** due to multi-region die sourcing complexity
- Unlike monolithic chips, chiplets require coordinating dies from potentially multiple foundries in multiple countries
- Export control implications: packaging of chiplets from different origins may trigger trade compliance issues

### State of Chiplet Ecosystem (2026)
- **UCIe 3.0** (released August 2025): doubles peak link speed to 48–64 GT/s; backward compatible with 1.0/2.0
- Annual update cadence for UCIe standards since 2023 indicates fast-moving standardisation
- **Runtime recalibration** (UCIe 3.0): links can retune on-the-fly, improving operational efficiency
- **Continuous-transmission support** (Raw-Mode): enables SoC-to-DSP streaming for communications/signal-processing

### Industry Adoption of UCIe 3.0
- Companies across compute, EDA, analog, and optical interconnect aligning roadmaps to **64 GT/s** links
- Applications: GPU-class bandwidth for AI scale-up, rack-scale disaggregation
- UCIe 3.0 broadens 3D hybrid bonding support (1µm pitch, already in UCIe 2.0)

### CHIPS Act and Packaging
- CoWoS monthly production projected: 40K units/month (end-2025) → 60K (2026-H1) → 80K (end-2026)
- CHIPS Act funding directed at US-based advanced packaging capacity as strategic imperative
- Only 3% of global chip packaging happens in US → major strategic gap
