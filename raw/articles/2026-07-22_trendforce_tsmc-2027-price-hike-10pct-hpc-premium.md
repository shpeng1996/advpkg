# [News] TSMC Reportedly Plans Up to 10% Price Hikes in 2027, with Extra HPC Premiums: Apple, NVIDIA in Focus

Source: Nikkei (via TrendForce) | Date: 2026-07-22

While TSMC said at last week's earnings call that it would avoid drastic price hikes like the 4–5x increases seen among memory players, Nikkei reports that the foundry giant plans to delay price increases until 2027, giving customers more time to prepare after negotiations. Prices for both advanced and mature nodes could rise by up to 10% next year.

## Key Price Hike Structure

- **Base hike**: 5–10% across both advanced and mature nodes
- **HPC surcharge**: Additional 10–15% on top of the base adjustment for HPC orders exceeding customers' original volume commitments
- **Timing**: Delayed to 2027 (not 2026), giving customers more time to prepare
- **Mature nodes** (12nm, 16nm, 28nm, legacy): up to 10%, some products smaller increases

## Business Context

- HPC segment accounted for **66% of TSMC's Q2 2026 total sales**, with revenue rising 20% quarter-over-quarter (strongest gain across all segments)
- Advanced nodes (7nm+) = ~77% of TSMC Q2 revenue; mature nodes ~23%
- J.P. Morgan expects 8–10% advanced-node price hike in 2027 to protect margins amid costly overseas expansion
- Analyst note: Intel Foundry unlikely to gain significantly from TSMC pricing due to TSMC's ongoing technology lead (A16, A14)

## Customer Impact

Key affected customers: **NVIDIA, Apple, Google, Amazon, Qualcomm, Arm, MediaTek**

- **Apple**: A20 Pro (2nm) could fall under HPC category → additional HPC premium. Combined impact: foundry cost per processor could rise US$10–20. May coincide with iPhone 18 debut.
- **NVIDIA**: Set to overtake Apple as TSMC's largest customer in 2026 (~US$33B / ~22% of revenue). Blackwell on 4nm, next-gen Rubin on 3nm.
- Intel Foundry: JPM says Intel unlikely to gain significantly from TSMC's price hikes despite positioning as alternative.
