## HBM4 Production Status
- Micron began mass production and shipments of HBM4 (6th-generation HBM)
- Based on 1β (1-beta, 10nm-class 5th-gen) DRAM process
- Supplying to NVIDIA Vera Rubin AI computing platform (H2 2026 launch)
- Shipments began March 2026
- Generated >$1 billion in revenue within three months
- 12-high HBM4 ramp progressing at ~2× the pace of 12-high HBM3E
- Yield stabilization expected more quickly than HBM3E

## HBM4E Development
- Based on 1γ (1-gamma, 10nm-class 6th-gen) DRAM
- Mass production targeted for 2027

## Other Product Updates
- 256GB DDR5 RDIMM (3D stacked architecture, 1γ DRAM) samples shipped to major server customers
- PCIe Gen6 SSDs based on G9 NAND platform — in mass production
- 245TB QLC SSDs — shipments started
- 16Gb LPDDR5X (1γ) for leading smartphones — in mass production ramp

## Strategic Customer Agreements (SCAs)
- 16 SCAs signed across data center, mobile, automotive markets
- Multi-year contracts from 2026 through 2030
- SCA backlog (remaining performance obligations): ~$100 billion
- ~$22 billion in customer prepayments and related financial commitments

## Q4 FY2026 Guidance
- Revenue: $50B ± $1B
- Gross Margin: ~86%
- GAAP Diluted EPS: $30.73 ± $1.00
- Q4 CapEx: ~$10B
- FY2026 total CapEx: ~$27B
