SK hynix has announced a massive KRW 100 trillion investment plan focused on its Cheongju complex, consisting of two major components:

## SK hynix KRW 100T Cheongju Investment

**KRW 80T — M17 NAND Fab (New Construction)**
- Construction start: 2027
- Target operations: 1H 2029
- Purpose: Next-generation NAND flash production

**KRW 20T — P&T7 Advanced Packaging Hub (Completion)**
- Target completion: 2027
- Purpose: Advanced packaging hub for HBM and memory integration

Additionally, SK Group (SK hynix's parent) announced a 1 GW AI data center investment in the Chungcheong region, signaling vertical integration ambitions across semiconductor manufacturing and AI compute infrastructure.

## Samsung KRW 140T Parallel Investment

Separately, Samsung Electronics announced a KRW 140 trillion investment across four subsidiary segments:

| Subsidiary | Investment | Purpose |
|-----------|-----------|---------|
| Samsung Display | KRW 67T | OLED panel production |
| Samsung Semiconductor | KRW 56T | HBM fabs in Onyang + Cheonan |
| Samsung SDI | KRW 9T | Battery production |
| Samsung Electro-Mechanics | KRW 8T | AI server package substrates in Sejong |

**Key AP observation**: Samsung Electro-Mechanics' KRW 8T allocation specifically targets AI server package substrates at its Sejong facility, reflecting the growing importance of advanced substrate technology in AI server deployments.

## Industry Implications

- The combined SK hynix + Samsung investment announcement (totaling KRW 240T across both companies) underscores continued aggressive capacity expansion despite near-term market uncertainties
- SK hynix's P&T7 packaging hub completion by 2027 aligns with HBM4 volume ramp requirements
- Samsung's simultaneous HBM fab investment in Onyang + Cheonan suggests confidence in closing the gap with SK hynix in HBM market share
- Package substrate investment by Samsung Electro-Mechanics positions the subsidiary as a key enabler for AI server deployments alongside its semiconductor siblings
