# Breaking Through the Limits of Process Shrinkage: Advanced Packaging Reshapes the Semiconductor Competitive Landscape as TSMC-Led Backend Ecosystem Reorganization Accelerates

**Source:** The Economy (economy.ac) | **Date:** 2026-07-10 | **Author:** Aoife Brennan

## Key Market Data

- **Yole Group projection:** Advanced packaging market: $37.8B (2023) → $69.5B (2029); **CAGR 10.7%**
- **Advanced packaging revenue share at TSMC:** ~8% in 2025 → expected >10% in 2026

## TSMC Packaging Expansion Status

**Taiwan facilities:**
- Longtan (Taoyuan), Hsinchu Science Park, Zhunan (Miaoli), Central Taiwan Science Park, Southern Taiwan Science Park
- **Chiayi Science Park (AP7):** Next-gen backend hub; 2 CoWoS facilities planned, first due completion later this year

**Arizona:**
- 2 advanced packaging plants planned; currently in permitting process for first facility
- 10-year strategic agreement with **Amkor Technology** for Arizona packaging capacity

**CoPoS timing (new data):**
- **Morgan Stanley Asia-Pacific Technology Industry report:** Mass production of CoPoS could begin as early as **2028** (ahead of previous expectations)
- Drivers: increasing AI chip size + growing Intel EMIB-T traction as competing technology
- Initial adopters: sub-2nm GPU/AI ASIC applications

## Taiwan Supply Chain Expansion (New Entrants)

### Asahi Kasei — Sunfort Photosensitive Film Expansion 🆕
- **Product:** Sunfort, photosensitive film used in semiconductor package substrates
- **Action:** Expanding processing capacity in **Taiwan** (new manufacturing facility)
- **Investment:** ~**USD 13.7 million**
- **Reason:** Booming AI demand drives larger, more complex multilayer package substrates; major customers include TSMC
- **First time this entity documented in wiki**

### Kinsus — Long-Term Substrate Expansion Strategy 🆕
- Taiwan-based package substrate supplier
- Long-term expansion strategy: **new manufacturing facilities every 2-3 years**
- Following TSMC-centered ecosystem demand

### ASE — CoWoS Capacity Expansion
- Increasing CoWoS production capacity to **20,000–25,000 wafers per month** by end of 2026
- TSMC outsourcing less complex RDL products to OSATs (ASE, Powertech)

## TSMC 3D IC Advanced Manufacturing Alliance — 37 Companies 🆕

TSMC and ASE jointly established the **3D IC Advanced Manufacturing Alliance** to build a localized advanced packaging equipment supply chain.

**Mission:** Bring together backend process, equipment, materials, and inspection companies for 3D IC and advanced packaging mass production. Align bonding, cleaning, inspection, heat treatment, and other processes to TSMC manufacturing standards; improve yields; ensure stable equipment supply.

**Members (37 total):**
- Chroma ATE
- Grand Process Technology
- Scientech Manufacturing
- ...and 34 others

**Note:** Previous wiki records mentioned 12 companies; this article confirms the expanded 37-member roster.

## Competitor Strategies Mentioned

- **SK hynix:** P&T7 packaging facility, Cheongju Technopolis, investment ~USD 13.8B, completion target end of 2027
- **Samsung:** Turnkey strategy (foundry + memory + packaging); I-Cube, H-Cube, X-Cube platforms
- **Intel:** EMIB + Foveros for chiplet architectures
