Corning has highlighted a next-generation glass-based optical interconnect technology designed to connect optical semiconductors and optical fibers, targeting emerging co-packaged optics (CPO) and glass-core semiconductor packaging architectures.

On June 24, Corning introduced GlassWorks AI Solutions at the AI Data Center Optical Communications & Interconnect Tech Conference held at POSCO Tower Yeoksam in Seoul.

## Corning Glass Bridge

Glass Bridge is a glass optical connector that directly links photonic integrated circuits (PICs) and optical fibers.

**Technical challenge addressed**: On-chip optical waveguides measure hundreds of nanometers in width; optical fiber cores are several micrometers wide — a size mismatch of several dozen times.

**Solution**: Glass Bridge uses waveguides formed inside glass (via wafer-based ion-exchange waveguide technology) to precisely connect the two structures.
- Light transmitted through optical fibers is guided through the glass waveguide and delivered to photonic chips
- Enables high-density optical I/O interfaces at the front end of PICs
- Simplifies alignment and assembly between optical fibers and photonic devices

**Specifications (initial product)**:
- Supports photonic chip core pitches of 30 micrometers or greater
- Target coupling loss: <2 dB between optical fibers and photonic chips

**Development status**:
- Currently co-developing with multiple partners
- Announced collaboration with GlobalFoundries last year on optical interconnect for AI data centers

## CPO Solutions
Corning provides CPO passive optical solutions including fiber harnesses with Fiber Array Units (FAU) integrated to CPO optical engines. Key components: CPO-optimized optical fibers, customized FAU, pre-assembled fiber harness, connectors, fiber management.

## Glass-Core Semiconductor Packaging Architecture
Corning also showcased a next-generation CPO architecture combining glass substrates with optical interconnects:
- Optical waveguides formed on glass substrates equipped with through-glass vias (TGVs)
- Connects flip-chip-mounted photonic devices
- Addresses future demand for glass-substrate-based semiconductor packaging

## GlassWorks AI Platform
Integrated optical connectivity infrastructure for AI data centers: optical fibers, cables, connectors, FAUs, alignment components. Covers connectivity within racks, between racks, and across data center campuses.

## Business Context
- Corning expanded optical communications manufacturing in North Carolina, Texas (US), and Poland
- Secured multi-billion-dollar long-term supply agreements with Meta, Nvidia, and Amazon

## Significance
This is the first detailed public disclosure of Corning's Glass Bridge product specifications and its glass-substrate CPO architecture. It positions Corning as a vertically integrated provider from optical fiber through glass substrates to CPO components for AI data centers.
