# TSMC Technology Symposium 2026 Overview

**Source**: SemiWiki — published 2026-04-22
**Event**: TSMC 2026 North America Technology Symposium (April 22, 2026)

## Key Advanced Packaging Announcements

### CoWoS Scaling Roadmap
- TSMC is currently producing **5.5-reticle size CoWoS** (as of April 2026)
- A **14-reticle size CoWoS** is planned for production in **2028**, capable of integrating approximately **10 large compute dies and 20 HBM stacks** in a single package
- Further expansion **beyond 14 reticles** is planned for **2029**
- The number of compute transistors within a single CoWoS package is projected to increase by **up to 48×** between 2024 and 2029
- Memory bandwidth is expected to scale by **34×** from 2024 to 2029, driven by HBM advancements

### Co-Packaged Optics (CPO): TSMC-COUPE™
- TSMC's Compact Universal Photonic Engine (**TSMC-COUPE™**) is reaching a key milestone in 2026
- True co-packaged optics solution using COUPE on substrate **begins production in 2026**
- Achieves **2× power efficiency** and **10× latency reduction** vs. pluggable versions on circuit boards
- COUPE integrates the optical engine directly into the CoWoS package

### SoIC 3D Stacking Roadmap
- **A14-to-A14 SoIC** expected in **2029**
- Offers **1.8× the die-to-die I/O density** of N2-to-N2 SoIC technology
- Supports higher data-transfer bandwidth for future AI compute systems

### Process Technology (Context)
- TSMC unveiled **A13** process technology at the Symposium, targeting **2029 production**
- A12 also in roadmap alongside A13

## Strategic Significance
- Advanced packaging accounted for ~8% of TSMC revenue in 2025; expected to surpass **10% in 2026**
- CoWoS capacity ramping to **90,000 wafers/month by end-2026** (up from ~75,000–80,000)
- Scale targets: **120,000–130,000 wafers/month** possible by end-2026 per some estimates
- NVIDIA and AMD are the primary demand drivers for CoWoS
