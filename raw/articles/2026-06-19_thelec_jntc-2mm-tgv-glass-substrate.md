JNTC announced on June 19, 2026 that it has completed development of a through-glass via (TGV) substrate with a thickness of 2 millimeters, becoming the first company worldwide to achieve this milestone.

## Key Facts

**TGV portfolio expansion**: 0.3mm → 2.0mm (with 3.0mm version in development)

**Validation status**:
- Completed validation with substrate manufacturers in Taiwan and South Korea
- Qualification testing underway with Japanese materials supplier
- Passed reliability evaluations for glass micro-cracks (one of the most significant technical challenges in glass substrate development)

**Mass production target**: 2027

**Customer engagements**:
- Participating in new projects with two global semiconductor companies (unnamed)
- MOU signed with major South Korean corporation (May 2026)
- Expected to sign additional agreement with Japanese global substrate manufacturer (July 2026)

## Company Background

JNTC entered the semiconductor glass substrate business in 2024. In October 2025, it completed a TGV mass-production line, establishing a vertically integrated manufacturing system covering the entire production process.

In 2025, JNTC strengthened manufacturing by absorbing Comet (subsidiary specializing in plating and etching), and improved quality/cost via equipment from affiliate Jinwoo Engineering.

**Additional technology**: Also secured unit cell-cut processing technology after customer requests.

## Significance

This is the first reported development of a 2.0mm-thick TGV glass substrate. Thicker glass substrates are needed for structural integrity in large package sizes (AI accelerators), as the industry's current focus is on 0.3–1.0mm range for semiconductor packages. JNTC's ability to cover 0.3mm–2.0mm (and developing 3.0mm) differentiates it from competitors and addresses emerging requirements for mechanically robust glass core substrates in high-power packages.

JNTC has previously participated in glass substrate ecosystem discussions at events in Korea alongside BOE and other players. Its entry into projects with two global semiconductor firms suggests it may be supplying to the same evaluation ecosystem as Absolics (AMD/AWS testing) and Intel (Rio Rancho glass substrate program).
