# Samsung's Custom HBM4E Design Reportedly Aimed for Mid-2026, Parallels SK Hynix and Micron

**Source**: TrendForce — published 2026-01-23

## Key Facts

### HBM4E Development Timeline
- **Samsung Electronics**: Custom HBM4E design aimed for completion **mid-2026**
- **SK Hynix**: Also targeting HBM4E development completion **1H 2026**
- **Micron**: HBM4E slated for **2027**, will come in standard and customized versions (partnership with TSMC)
- All three major HBM suppliers developing HBM4E on roughly parallel timelines

### HBM4E Specifications
- **Data rate**: 10 Gbps (vs. HBM4's 6.4 GT/s)
- **Bandwidth per stack**: 2.5 TB/s
- **Per-package power**: up to 80 W
- 16-Hi stacking may be branded as HBM4E depending on performance characteristics

### Market Projections
- HBM4E estimated to reach **~40% of total HBM market demand in 2027**
- AI accelerators featuring HBM4E planned for launch in **2027**

### HBM4 Context (current generation, 2026)
- HBM4 specification: **2048-bit interface**, 24 Gb and 32 Gb DRAM layers, up to 6.40 GT/s
- Samsung mass production at Pyeongtaek started **February 2026**
- SK Hynix: full-scale HBM4 production at M16 (Icheon) and M15X (Cheongju)
- SK Hynix, Samsung, Micron competing for NVIDIA 16-Hi HBM4 supply contracts

### Process Technology
- Micron: using 1β (5th Gen, 10nm-class) process for HBM4
- Samsung: transitioning to 1γ (6th Gen, 10nm-class) for 24 Gb DRAM dies in HBM4

### HBM Roadmap Beyond HBM4E
- **HBM5**: industry expectations place launch in **2029**
