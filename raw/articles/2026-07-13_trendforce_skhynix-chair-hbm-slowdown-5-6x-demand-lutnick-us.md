SK hynix has taken its first major step into global capital markets, with The Wall Street Journal reporting that its ADRs surged 13% last Friday following the memory chipmaker's record $26.5 billion U.S. share offering—the largest ever by a foreign company. The stock closed its debut trading day at a $1.2 trillion market value, surpassing U.S. chip giants Micron and AMD.

## Supply Gap to Persist Beyond 2030

Citing SK Group Chair Chey Tae-won (Hankyung), customers are urging SK hynix to boost supply by **"five to six times."** The remarks underscore the widening gap between surging AI-driven demand and available memory capacity. Chey previously stated SK hynix aims to triple wafer output by 2034.

SK hynix CEO Kwak Noh-jung (Reuters) stated the global memory industry is heading toward its **most severe supply shortage ever in 2027**. Kwak added that SK hynix expects demand to remain above its supply capabilities even beyond 2030.

## HBM Demand Shows No Signs of Slowing

Despite concerns that rising HBM costs could prompt tech giants, including NVIDIA and Google, to reduce HBM adoption, SK hynix pushed back:
- "We haven't seen any signals that HBM demand is declining" — Chair Chey
- "Our partners are actually asking us to further increase supply capacity for both this year and next"

## Qualcomm HBC: Emerging Alternative to HBM

As the AI industry seeks alternatives to ease reliance on HBM, Qualcomm's **HBC (High-Bandwidth Compute)** is emerging:
- Stacks LPDDR memory chips directly on top of a logic die
- Reduces data movement to lower power consumption while boosting memory capacity
- Claims up to **6x higher bandwidth per watt than HBM** (Nikkei Asia)
- Positions as energy-efficient alternative for AI accelerators
- Directly challenges NVIDIA's HBM-based GPU dominance

## U.S. Expansion Plans

Commerce Secretary Howard Lutnick (Bloomberg) urged Samsung and SK hynix to build memory fabs on American soil. Against this backdrop:
- Chair Chey signaled openness to further U.S. manufacturing expansion
- Conditions required: reliable power, clean water, suitable sites, skilled talent, semiconductor ecosystem
- SK Group: committing **US$10 billion to establish an AI solutions business in the U.S.**
- Indiana advanced packaging facility ($4B) ongoing
