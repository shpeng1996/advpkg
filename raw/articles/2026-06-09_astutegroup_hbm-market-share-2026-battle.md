# SK hynix holds 62% of HBM, Micron overtakes Samsung, 2026 battle pivots to HBM4

Published 2025-09-24; republished/relevant context for the 2026 HBM4 competitive landscape.

## Key claims and data points

- Q2 2025 HBM market share (per Chosun Biz): SK hynix 62%, Micron 21%, Samsung 17% (Samsung fell to third place).
- Counterpoint Research forecasts Samsung's HBM share will recover to above 30% in 2026 as its HBM3E parts gain qualification and HBM4 reaches full-scale supply.
- SK hynix announced completion of HBM4 development: claims 40% improvement in power efficiency and data rates of 10 Gbps; mass production to follow qualification.
- Micron has begun shipping HBM4 samples rated up to 11 Gbps, is collaborating with foundry partners on HBM4E, expects to sell out 2026 HBM capacity, and forecasts an HBM annualized revenue run-rate of ~US$8 billion.
- Samsung resumed construction of its Pyeongtaek P5 fab and is positioning a 6th-generation 10nm-class DRAM process as a stepping stone to volume HBM4 production.
- SK hynix remains NVIDIA's primary HBM supplier, with its HBM4 slated for the Rubin GPU platform.
- Counterpoint expects Korean suppliers (SK hynix, Samsung) to retain collective dominance; Chinese suppliers reportedly still struggle with thermal management and operating-speed hurdles.

- Concrete Q2 2025 HBM market-share baseline (62/21/17 split among SK hynix/Micron/Samsung) — useful historical anchor for tracking the 2026 share shifts already noted in wiki/technologies/hbm4.md (e.g., Samsung's HBM4E "ships first" claim from 2026-05-31).
