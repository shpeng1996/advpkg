# AMD's Lisa Su Reportedly to Visit TSMC for 2nm Capacity; Unveils US$10B Taiwan AI Packaging Investment

**Published:** 2026-05-21 | **Source:** TrendForce

Ahead of COMPUTEX 2026, AMD Chair and CEO Lisa Su arrived in Taipei on May 20. She is set to attend the AI summit on May 22 and may personally meet with TSMC Chairman C.C. Wei to secure capacity for 2nm process technology and advanced packaging.

AMD announced that its next-generation EPYC processor, codenamed "Venice," has entered production in Taiwan using TSMC's 2nm process, with future production planned at TSMC's Arizona fab. Venice is also said to be the industry's first high-performance computing (HPC) product to enter production on TSMC's 2nm technology.

AMD also plans to extend TSMC's 2nm process across its data center CPU roadmap with "Verano," a 6th Gen EPYC processor. Built for cloud and AI workloads, Verano is expected to advance the EPYC platform with memory innovations including LPDDR, improving CPU performance, bandwidth, and efficiency for increasingly power-constrained applications.

## US$10B Taiwan Investment: Advanced Packaging Focus

AMD announced more than US$10 billion in investments across Taiwan's ecosystem to deepen strategic partnerships and expand advanced packaging capacity for next-generation AI infrastructure. The company is working with Taiwan-based ASE and SPIL, along with other industry partners, to develop and qualify next-generation wafer-based **2.5D bridge interconnect technology (EFB architecture)**. The EFB architecture is designed to increase interconnect bandwidth and improve power efficiency, supporting "Venice" CPUs.

**Key milestone:** AMD has achieved a milestone with PTI by qualifying the **industry's first 2.5D panel-based EFB interconnect**.

Rising AI server demand is pushing AMD to accelerate efforts to secure advanced process and CoWoS packaging capacity to avoid future AI chip supply constraints. Institutional investors say that beyond TSMC, OSAT firms including ASE and Powertech, as well as server ODMs and suppliers of cooling, power, connectors, and high-speed interconnects, are expected to benefit.

## CPU-to-GPU Ratio Shift

Lisa Su said data centers previously maintained a CPU-to-GPU ratio of roughly 1:4, but growing adoption of AI agents could push that ratio closer to 1:1. AMD has sharply raised its CPU market outlook: Su now expects the server CPU addressable market to grow more than 35% annually, exceeding US$120 billion by 2030.

AMD also showcased a palm-sized device powered by a Ryzen AI Max processor that can run a 200-billion-parameter large language model offline, adopting a unified RAM architecture where CPU, GPU, and NPU share 128GB of memory.
