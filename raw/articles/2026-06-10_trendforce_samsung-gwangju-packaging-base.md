# Samsung May Announce New Gwangju Advanced Packaging Base by End-June Amid Capacity Expansion

**Published**: 2026-06-10 | **Source**: TrendForce

---

Samsung Electronics is reportedly looking to expand its advanced packaging capacity. According to Hankyung, sources say the company is pursuing plans to build an advanced packaging plant in **Gwangju** (Honam region). Samsung Electronics is expected to announce the investment as early as the **end of June 2026** at a meeting of major conglomerate leaders hosted by President Lee Jae-myung.

The new facility would mark:
- Samsung Electronics' **first packaging-base construction since Onyang Campus (35 years ago)**
- The company's **first major semiconductor production site groundbreaking since the Pyeongtaek Campus launch (11 years ago)**

The decision to locate in Gwangju reflects infrastructure concerns: electricity and water supplies in the Seoul metropolitan area are approaching their limits.

## Samsung's HBM Packaging Capacity Targets

According to New Daily (2026-03-17), Samsung is scaling HBM back-end processing capacity at its Cheonan facility, targeting by end-2026:
- **Thermal Compression Bonding (TCB)**: 231,000 units/month
- **Hybrid Copper Bonding (HCB)**: 19,500 units/month

Samsung plans to **transition its HBM stacking roadmap from TCB to HCB by 2029**.

## Overseas Expansion: Vietnam Testing Facility

Per Reuters, Samsung plans to invest **39 trillion dong (~US$1.5 billion)** in Vietnam to build a **semiconductor testing facility**, approximately 60km north of Hanoi. Operations expected: **November 2027**. Construction has already begun.

## SK Hynix Honam Region Consideration

SK hynix is also reportedly considering establishing a semiconductor packaging production base in the Honam region (South Jeolla Province), though SK hynix has already announced the 19 trillion won P&T7 plant in Cheongju, North Chungcheong Province (construction began April 2026).
