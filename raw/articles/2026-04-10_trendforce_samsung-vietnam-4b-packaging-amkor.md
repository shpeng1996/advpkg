# [News] Samsung Reportedly Bets on Vietnam with $4B Chip Packaging Push; Amkor Expands Presence

**Published**: 2026-04-10 | **Source**: TrendForce (citing Bloomberg, Reuters, Global Economic News)

Semiconductor giants are accelerating global advanced packaging expansion as demand for AI chips surges, with Southeast Asia emerging as one of the most sought-after hubs. While Intel is set to bring its advanced packaging facility in Malaysia online later this year, Bloomberg reports that Samsung is also preparing a major push in Vietnam, planning a US$4 billion investment to build a chip packaging plant in the country's north.

## Samsung Mulls Multi-Phase Expansion Plan

According to Bloomberg, Samsung's project will roll out in multiple phases in Thai Nguyen province, starting with an initial US$2 billion commitment.

Reuters adds that Vietnam's Finance Ministry is working in tandem with agencies to finalize and submit to the Prime Minister a memorandum of understanding (MoU) with Samsung covering the group's proposed semiconductor manufacturing project in the country.

Samsung first established operations in Bac Ninh in 2008, laying the groundwork for its manufacturing footprint. Since then, Vietnam has grown into Samsung's largest global smartphone production hub, further reinforced by a major handset plant in Thai Nguyen launched in 2013.

## Amkor Accelerates Vietnam Expansion, Raises Three Key Policy Requests

Amkor is speeding up the expansion of its production lines in Vietnam, leveraging what it describes as the world's largest advanced semiconductor packaging facility in Bac Ninh province. The U.S.-headquartered company has invested a total of US$1.6 billion since 2021 to build a state-of-the-art OSAT facility at the Yen Phong 2C Industrial Park in Bac Ninh.

Kim Sung-hoon, general director of Amkor Technology Vietnam, recently met with the Secretary of the Bac Ninh Provincial Party Committee, where he outlined three key policy proposals:
1. Establish concrete measures prioritizing LPG supply to semiconductor facilities (amid global energy disruptions and geopolitical tensions)
2. Introduce a mechanism to directly subsidize ~3% of investment capital to enhance global competitiveness
3. Stronger practical support measures—housing and welfare initiatives—to attract and retain highly skilled engineers

## Strategic Significance

Vietnam, benefiting from competitive labor costs, strategic location, and an established industrial base, is increasingly emerging as a key destination for packaging and testing capacity relocation. The expansion of Korean semiconductor back-end equipment and materials players into Vietnam is expected to accelerate.

Key facts:
- Samsung: US$4B total; US$2B initial phase; Thai Nguyen province; multi-phase rollout
- Amkor: US$1.6B invested since 2021; Yen Phong 2C Industrial Park, Bac Ninh
- Intel Malaysia advanced packaging facility also expected operational in 2026
