# SK hynix Confirms Nasdaq Listing, Seeking Up to $29 Billion in a Record ADR Offering

Every won is earmarked for fabs and HBM packaging, a capacity bet placed at peak valuation

By Allen Lee | Published: Jun 25 2026

SK hynix has formally decided to list American depositary receipts on the U.S. Nasdaq, aiming to raise up to 45.45 trillion won (about $29 billion) — a deal that, at the top of its range, would rank as the largest ADR offering ever, surpassing Alibaba's $21.8 billion New York debut in 2014.

What turns a long-rumored plan into news is the part that's now fixed: the board has approved it, the size is set, and the use of the money is unusually specific. SK hynix isn't raising dollars to pad its balance sheet. It is converting a record stock valuation — built on an AI-memory boom that has its order books sold out — into capital earmarked entirely for the fabs and packaging that the next wave of demand will need.

## What the Board Approved

The South Korean memory giant said on June 24 that its board — with all outside directors present — approved issuing new shares through a third-party allotment, to be deposited with an overseas institution and converted into ADRs for sale to international investors. It will issue up to 17.79 million new shares, about 2.5% of its stock; based on the June 23 closing price of 2.555 million won, that implies a maximum of roughly 45.45 trillion won (~$29 billion), though the final size depends on bookbuilding. Each common share will be represented by 10 ADRs. The company plans to list on the Nasdaq Global Select Market on July 10, with subscription and payment on July 14 and the new Korean shares listing on July 29.

## Proceeds Allocation (100% to Capacity)

All of the proceeds are earmarked for manufacturing capacity:
- Building the first fab (Y1) at the Yongin semiconductor cluster
- Constructing and equipping the Cheongju P&T7 advanced-packaging fab for AI memory (HBM)
- Purchasing chipmaking equipment including EUV lithography scanners

## ADR Structure

- Ticker: SKHY (Nasdaq Global Select Market)
- Ratio: 10 ADRs per common share
- Target listing date: July 10, 2026
- Underwriters: BofA Securities, Citigroup, Goldman Sachs, JPMorgan
- Dilution: ~2.5% of existing shares

## Scale Context

The ~$29 billion would be the largest ADR offering ever recorded, surpassing:
- Alibaba (2014): $21.8 billion NYSE IPO
- TSMC and ASML already trade as ADRs in the U.S.

## Company Context

SK hynix became South Korea's most valuable company this week (June 2026), overtaking Samsung Electronics, after shares rose >300% YTD. Market cap first topped $1 trillion in May 2026. The company holds ~60% of the HBM market with HBM capacity for 2026 sold out, shortages forecast into 2027.

## Risk Factors

- Final size subject to SEC review and bookbuilding
- Memory industry cyclicality — capacity being built ahead of demand
- Currency swings and market volatility
