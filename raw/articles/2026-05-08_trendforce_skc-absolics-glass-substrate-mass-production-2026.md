# SKC Said to Speed Glass Substrate Mass Production by Year-End; Advances New Non-Embedding Tech for U.S. Client

Published: 2026-05-08 | Source: TrendForce

As advanced packaging becomes increasingly important, glass substrates are drawing growing industry attention. According to Business Post, SKC and its subsidiary Absolics are expected to begin what could become the world's first commercial production of glass substrates by the end of 2026.

Global Economic, citing sources, indicates that the company has already completed electrical and signal performance verification and has now entered the yield stabilization stage. Prototypes produced by Absolics are reportedly undergoing performance testing by companies including AMD and Amazon Web Services (AWS).

## Non-Embedding Glass Substrate Project

ETNews, citing sources, reports that SKC's Absolics has launched a new project with a U.S. semiconductor company, supplying prototype non-embedding glass substrates for next-generation communications semiconductors. The project marks a shift beyond Absolics' previous focus on embedding glass substrates, with commercialization preparations potentially beginning as early as 2026 if reliability testing is successfully completed.

Non-embedding glass substrates are generally considered to face lower technological barriers than embedding types, a move that could help accelerate glass substrate commercialization while addressing increasingly diverse market demands.

## Apple Foundry Talks Fuel Glass Substrate Speculation

As Apple is reportedly considering Intel and Samsung for future foundry cooperation, industry sources suggest glass substrates could potentially be adopted in future Apple chips. Intel's advanced packaging technologies, including EMIB and glass substrates, are among the factors drawing attention, with some market watchers speculating the technology could eventually appear in future chips such as the M5 and M6.

Samsung's foundry division is reportedly targeting glass substrate adoption for advanced packaging beginning in 2028.

## Key Technical Challenge: SeWaRe

The biggest mass-production challenge for glass substrates is "SeWaRe," which refers to micro-cracks that form during processing, especially drilling and dicing.

## Supply Chain / Equipment Ecosystem

Key glass substrate equipment and materials are currently dominated by leading companies from Europe, the U.S., and Japan:
- LPKF: TGV equipment
- SCHOTT, Corning, AGC, NEG: low-CTE glass supply
- Lam Research: etching and plating systems
- DISCO: dicing equipment
- Onto, KLA: inspection tools
- SUSS, EVG: temporary bonding and debonding
