As leading foundries now target 2028–29 for 1.4nm-class mass production, they are accelerating enhanced 2nm variants to bridge the gap.

## Samsung Foundry 2nm Roadmap Expansion

According to The Elec (citing VP Shin Jong-shin, Samsung Foundry Design Platform Development Team), the Samsung Foundry 2nm platform roadmap is:

| Node | Mass Production Target | Key Features |
|------|----------------------|--------------|
| SF2  | Launched 2026 | First 2nm GAA nanosheet |
| SF2P | 2026-2027 | -26% power, +15% freq vs SF2 (>50% from DTCO) |
| SF2P+| **2027–2028** | Performance-enhanced 2nm variant |
| SF2X | 2028+ | AI/HPC optimized |
| SF1.4| **2029** | 1.4nm mass production |
| SF1.4+| 2030 | Enhanced yield + performance |

## TSMC 2nm-Class Node Portfolio (Comparison)

TSMC's N2 platform roadmap:

| Node | Mass Production | Key Features |
|------|----------------|--------------|
| N2   | 2025 (launched) | First-gen nanosheet |
| N2P  | **2H 2026** (HVM) | Performance enhanced |
| N2X  | 2027 | Further enhancement |
| N2U  | 2028 | ~3-4% higher perf, 8-10% lower power vs N2P; full IP compat with N2P |
| A16  | 2027 (official) | Super Power Rail (SPR); production-ready 2H 2026 |

## Strategic Implications for Advanced Packaging

- SF2P+ and SF2X are optimized for AI/HPC — key advanced packaging compute die nodes
- The SF1.4 3D stacking plan (stacking SF1.4 on top of SF2P, starting 2027) directly intersects with Samsung's advanced packaging roadmap
- TSMC N2U's N2P compatibility reduces redesign costs, easing customer transition

## Context: Competition

Both Samsung and TSMC are racing to offer enhanced 2nm variants to bridge the gap toward 1.4nm/A14-class nodes expected in 2028-2029. The SF2P+ vs N2P/N2U competition will heavily influence which advanced packaging customers (NVIDIA, AMD, Google, etc.) choose as their foundry for next-generation AI chips.
