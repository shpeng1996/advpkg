# Chip Industry Week In Review #144

**Published:** June 26, 2026 | **Source:** semiengineering.com

Onsemi to buy Synaptics; IBM's 7Å chip w/40% more SRAM area; 1nm MoS2 nanotubes; AI pressure points; memory updates; $250M CHIPS Act award; trade secret theft; new advanced packaging site; MEMS capacity; humanoids; earnings.

## Equipment and Advanced Packaging

- **Applied Materials** introduced an epitaxy system for DRAM fabs, and CMP, deposition, and e-beam inspection systems for advanced packaging.
- **JCET** is reportedly planning a ~$1.15B Shanghai advanced packaging and testing site (a new facility, distinct from its existing Jiangyin 3D packaging plant).
- **AlixLabs** launched the first commercially available equipment for its ALE Pitch Splitting etching technology to increase pattern density.
- **GlobalFoundries (GF)** now offers wafer-to-wafer bonding for its 9SW RF-SOI platform for front-end modules.

## Memory and Storage

- **Micron** (Q3 earnings): AI demand and structural capacity limits will keep memory supply tight beyond 2027. 16 customers have signed multiyear take-or-pay agreements with price floors. Micron's 12-high HBM4 ramp is progressing twice as fast as HBM3E, and has already generated more than $1B in shipments.
- **SK hynix** plans to list ADRs on the Nasdaq (ticker: SKHY), potentially bringing in $29B.
- **Samsung** uncorked UFS 5.0 for mobile devices with a data transfer speed of up to 10.8 GB/s and up to 1TB capacity.
- **IDC** forecasts a continued supply/demand imbalance for memory through 2027.

## Dealmaking and Financials

- **Onsemi** plans to acquire **Synaptics** for ~$7B (edge AI compute, wireless connectivity, HMI, touch, display, biometric). Expected to close mid-2027.
- **Qualcomm** plans to acquire **Modular** (AI software stack across heterogeneous hardware).
- **OpenAI** and **Broadcom** designed an LLM inference chip.
- **Micron** inked a strategic agreement with **Anthropic** spanning memory/storage AI architecture design and supply.
- **Groq** raised $650M, pivoting to AI inference cloud after Nvidia licensed its LPU design.
- **Nearfield Instruments** raised $380M for 3D metrology/inspection/process control equipment.
- U.S. Commerce Department signed a $250M CHIPS R&D award with **I-Pulse** for SiC power semiconductors.

## Semiconductor Scaling and Research

- **IBM** unveiled a 7Å transistor architecture using staggered nanosheet transistors (tiles on a roof). Claims: 50% performance boost, 70% power efficiency improvement, 40% SRAM scaling improvement. Mass production ~2031.
- **U. of Tokyo** created 1nm MoS₂ nanotubes for ultra-scaled GAA transistors.
- **TNO** and **ASML** collaborating to scale European photonic chip manufacturing (6-inch InP chips).

## Global

- **South Korea** discussing a second major semiconductor cluster with Samsung and SK hynix.
- China's **LineShine** supercomputer topped the TOP500 list at 2.198 exaflop/s.
- **X-FAB** received ~$145M German government grant for MEMS foundry in Erfurt.
- **Teledyne MEMS** expanding MEMS manufacturing capacity in Edmonton, Canada.

## AI Pressures and Data Centers

- **Gartner** warned AI coding-agent costs could exceed average developer salary by 2028.
- California paused its largest planned data center (1M sq ft, 260M gallons water/year from Colorado River).
- Hyperscalers under pressure on data center water usage (Amazon +52% improvement, Meta using desalinated seawater in India, Google recycled water).

## Financial Releases

- Cerebras (Q1 2026), Micron (Q3 2026 record results), Qualcomm (long-term projections).
