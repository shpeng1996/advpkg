# TSMC Speeds Advanced Packaging: AP7 Targets 2026 Output; Arizona P6 Eyed for U.S. Packaging Hub

**Publisher**: TrendForce | **Date**: December 4, 2025

## Summary

TSMC is accelerating its advanced packaging capacity buildout on two fronts: a major new AP7 facility in Chiayi, Taiwan, and plans to repurpose its Arizona P6 site as a U.S. advanced packaging hub — a strategic move driven by CHIPS Act incentive requirements and customer onshoring pressure.

## Key Findings

### AP7 Chiayi — Taiwan's Next-Generation Packaging Mega-Site
- TSMC held the **opening ceremony for AP7** in Chiayi, Taiwan in late 2025
- **Phase 1**: Equipment move-in scheduled for 2026; mass production in 2027
- **Phase 2**: Equipment installation and testing begun; production start 2026
- AP7 is TSMC's most technologically advanced packaging site, hosting:
  - **WMCM** (Wafer-Level Multi-Chip Module) — Apple mobile chip packaging
  - **CoPoS** (Chip-on-Panel-on-Substrate) — panel-level packaging 2028+ ramp
  - **SoIC** (System on Integrated Chips) — 3D stacking for AI chips
- Site spans 15,000 sq. meters planned across eight phases; scale comparable to TSMC's largest fabs

### Arizona P6 — U.S. Advanced Packaging Hub
- TSMC plans to **repurpose the P6 area** (originally slated for wafer fab Phase 6) as an **advanced packaging facility**
- Current challenge: TSMC Arizona wafers must be shipped to Taiwan for assembly and testing → adds cost and time
- U.S. packaging hub strategy: combination of building own facilities + forming strategic partnerships with U.S. OSATs
- Separate TrendForce report (July 2025) noted TSMC to **break ground on U.S. advanced packaging plants in 2028**, starting with SoIC and CoPoS
- Arizona P6 packaging: **SoIC-first** approach likely, followed by CoWoS capabilities

### CapEx Context
- TSMC advanced packaging CapEx projected to grow at **24% CAGR in 2025–27**
- AP7 (Chiayi) is the primary driver of this CapEx; Arizona P6 adds incremental U.S.-based investment
- Total 2026 TSMC CapEx guidance: $52–56 billion, with 10–20% allocated to packaging/testing/masks

## Geopolitical Context

TSMC's Arizona packaging investment is partly driven by CHIPS Act compliance requirements — recipients of U.S. government subsidies must demonstrate meaningful U.S. manufacturing footprint. The packaging gap (U.S. has <3% global advanced packaging share) is a critical vulnerability that Arizona P6 is intended to partially address.

## Implications for Supply Chain

- U.S. fabless customers (AMD, NVIDIA, Qualcomm, Apple) currently rely on Taiwan-based packaging; Arizona P6 provides geographic diversification
- 2028 groundbreaking → 2030+ meaningful U.S. advanced packaging output
- Near-term (2026–2027): Taiwan AP7 remains the primary capacity addition
