# Packaging Technologies Redefine AI And HPC Scalability Limits At ECTC 2026

Advancements in EMIB-T, co-packaged optics, and glass substrates.

June 5th, 2026 — By: Lori Scott (Senior Director of Marketing, Intel Foundry)

SPONSOR BLOG — Intel Foundry's official summary of 20 ECTC 2026 technical papers.

The 2026 IEEE Electronic Components and Technology Conference (ECTC) showcased how advanced packaging can redefine the scalability limits of artificial intelligence (AI) and high-performance computing (HPC). Across 20 technical papers, Intel Foundry engineers and collaborators highlighted breakthrough innovations — from Embedded Multi-die Interconnect Bridge-T (EMIB-T) enabling large multi-die systems without significantly increasing cost or manufacturing risk, to co-packaged optics (CPO) delivering dramatically higher bandwidth at reduced power in the near term, to future glass substrates that unlock larger form factors and improved stability.

## Advancing EMIB-T Scaling Capabilities

EMIB-T advanced packaging technology enables ultra-large, high-performance chiplet systems that scale beyond silicon reticle limits and traditional packaging constraints. EMIB-T leverages embedded silicon bridges for high-density lateral 2.5D interconnect, combined with through-silicon via (TSV)-enabled vertical scaling to enhance power delivery. This approach allows Intel Foundry to construct package-scale systems that function as a single large device while being composed of many optimized chiplets.

**Key EMIB-T metrics demonstrated at ECTC 2026:**
- First Layer Interconnect (FLI) bump pitch: **25µm**
- Package form factor: up to **120 × 120 mm**
- Compute/memory Si content: more than **9× reticles** on single package
- HBM4e interface: **12+ Gb/s**
- UCIe interface: **64 Gb/s**

**Collaborations presented:**
- With Siliconware Precision Industries (SPIL): 3D integration of SRAM chiplet in fan-out embedded bridge
- Package architectures for hyper-large form factors for AI and HPC
- Challenges and solutions for package-level ultra-large die complexes

## Co-Packaged Optics for Improved Bandwidth

CPO addresses fundamental system-level problems when data center bandwidth scales faster than electrical interconnect technology can support.

**High-performance detachable edge optical connector:** Intel Foundry's edge-coupled optical interface uses **glass fan-out couplers** and expanded-beam edge connectors to deliver low-loss, highly reliable, and detachable optical interfaces compatible with high-volume manufacturing. Suitable for wafer-level and package-level assembly.

**V-groove based edge coupling glass coupler interface:** Intel Foundry engineers replaced the traditional fiber-only approach with a rigid optical glass coupler that attaches to the V-grooves in photonic integrated circuits (PICs). This method improves bandwidth and energy efficiency while reducing latency and simplifying manufacturing.

Additional CPO research: Die-to-die attach with low thermal input thermo-compression bonding.

## Next-Gen Glass Substrates for Larger Package Sizes

Traditional organic core substrates are reaching their scaling limit. Intel Foundry's research and development on glass core substrates focuses on removing this base material as a scaling bottleneck.

**Key glass substrate results:**
- Fully copper-filled TGVs can survive **very harsh thermal cycling without failure** (zero failure demonstrated)
- Glass stays flat and stable during processing, unlike organic substrates (warpage eliminated)
- Multiple TGV types demonstrated: low taper, mixed sizes, cavities for embedded devices
- Precise and repeatable TGV creation enables flexible package designs

## Hybrid Bonding Research at ECTC 2026

Intel presented and collaborated on several hybrid bonding topics:
- Collaboration with Fouriers Scientific: **Thermal behavior of hybrid bonding interfaces** in advanced packaging (first public thermal behavior data for HB interfaces)
- Collaboration with NIST: **Probing plasticity in hybrid bonding copper** via atomic force microscopy
- **Die distortion in die-to-wafer hybrid bonding** (critical challenge paper)
- **Ultra-low temperature hybrid bonding** for die-to-wafer scaling

## Additional Research Topics

- Collaboration with EV Group: Advanced metrology for heterogeneous chiplet integration with **100% chip-to-wafer bond overlay control at high speed**
- Assembly materials creep behaviors and their impact on package warpage
- AI headnodes socketed interconnect yield, warpage, and functionality
- Selective layer transfer for fine-grain mixed front-end devices
- Infrared laser debond process modeling
- Large form factor patch-on-interposer (PoINT) scaling
- **Fluxless thermo-compression bonding of 4× reticle die stack** (enables largest-ever reported TCB die stack configuration)
