Summary highlights from SemiEngineering Chip Industry Week in Review #147 (July 17, 2026):

## Advanced Manufacturing & Packaging
- **Intel Foundry** will invest €5B to expand Intel 3 capacity at its Leixlip, Ireland campus. Also entered high-volume manufacturing for a subset of Panther Lake processors on 18A using ASML's High-NA EUV.
- **UMC** delivered the first production wafers for **SILITH's** 1.6T silicon photonics platform from its 300mm Singapore fab, giving UMC a production base for its own silicon photonics platform planned for customer development in 2027.
- **Tower Semiconductor** plans a ~$4B expansion of silicon photonics, silicon-germanium, and advanced-packaging capacity in Japan.
- **TSMC** will invest an incremental $100B in advanced semiconductor manufacturing and packaging facilities in Arizona, potentially expanding its footprint to about 12 U.S. fabs and packaging facilities.
- **Samsung** plans to begin operations at its first Yongin semiconductor fab by 2029, speeding up the original schedule by one to two years.

## Government Interventions
- **European Commission** approved €659M in German subsidies for four new facilities (SiC epi-wafers, power MOSFETs, metrology equipment, detector chips).
- **U.S. Commerce** will provide **Bosch** CHIPS Act incentives worth up to $225 million for a $2B conversion of its Roseville, California facility into a SiC chip fab.
- **India's government** approved ~US$13.2B for **Semicon 2.0**, covering chip design, equipment and materials, new fabs, advanced packaging, R&D, and workforce development.

## Memory / Standards
- **JEDEC** finalized new memory standard **JESD330-4 (SPHBM4)**: uses a narrower, faster interface to connect HBM4-class memory through organic package substrates rather than silicon interposers.
- **Nanya Technology** plans to invest ~$16B in its new Taiwan DRAM fab (up from $9.4B originally); first phase expected to reach 30,000 wsm by 2028; longer-term goal 45,000 wsm.
- **CXMT** (China) $8.55B Shanghai IPO was 200x oversubscribed by retail investors. U.S. lawmakers urged Commerce to prohibit purchases of CXMT memory.

## Market Analysis
- Global semiconductor equipment sales will rise 23% to ~$166B in 2026 and reach a record ~$230B by 2028 (SEMI forecast).

## Innovations
- **Cadence** launched **AuraStack AI Super Agent for PCB and advanced packaging** design — up to 2X faster time to market and 15X higher productivity by coordinating domain-specific AI agents across planning, implementation, and multiphysics analysis.
- **Rapidus** and **Cadence** partnering to integrate Cadence's InnoStack AI Super Agent into Rapidus' Raads chip design environment.
- **imec** and **Diraq** demonstrated first coherent operation of an eight-qubit silicon MOS array fabricated in 300mm CMOS-compatible process.

## Funding
- 80 companies raised >$6B in Q2 2026; 18 companies ≥$100M.
- Quadric: Series C extended to $46M for programmable AI chip platform.
- TYLsemi: $43M for full-stack chiplet platform for custom AI silicon.
- Diodes Incorporated to acquire ElevATE Semiconductor for $250M (mixed-signal ICs for ATE).
