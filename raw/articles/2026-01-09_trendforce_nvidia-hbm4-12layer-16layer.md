# NVIDIA Fuels HBM4 Race: 12-Layer Ramps, 16-Layer Push by SK Hynix, Samsung, and Micron

**Publisher**: TrendForce | **Date**: January 9, 2026

## Summary

NVIDIA's insatiable demand for high-bandwidth memory is driving all three major HBM suppliers — SK Hynix, Samsung, and Micron — to accelerate HBM4 production and push toward next-generation 16-layer stacks in H2 2026.

## Key Findings

### HBM4 12-Layer — Current Ramp Phase
- **SK Hynix** is leading the HBM4 12-layer ramp, with M15X facility (Icheon) producing 12-layer HBM4 stacks since late 2025
- SK Hynix M15X came online **4 months ahead of schedule** (February 2026), at ~10,000 wafers/month initial rate
- **Samsung** began first batch HBM4 shipments in February 2026; passed NVIDIA's HBM4 prototype test (achieving ~30% HBM3E price discount strategy)
- **Micron** HBM4 achieving industry-leading speeds above **11 Gbps**, on track for high-yield ramp in Q2 2026

### HBM4 16-Layer — H2 2026 Push
- NVIDIA has formally requested **16-layer HBM4 supply** for H2 2026 GPU configurations
- 16-layer HBM4 enables greater capacity per stack, critical for next-generation AI training systems
- All three suppliers are accelerating 16-layer development timelines to meet NVIDIA's H2 2026 demand
- SK Hynix leads in 16-layer readiness; Samsung and Micron targeting similar H2 2026 delivery

### Production Capacity Scale-Up
- Both SK Hynix and Samsung are targeting **~250,000 HBM wafers/month** by end of 2026 (~50% increase from current levels)
- HBM3E remains the mainstream product in H1 2026; HBM4 becomes dominant in H2 2026
- HBM4E (10 GT/s, 2.5 TB/s) development completion targeted for H1 2026; volume in 2027

### Supplier Market Dynamics
- SK Hynix holds ~70% of NVIDIA's HBM supply (primarily HBM3E/HBM4)
- Samsung recovering after HBM3E qualification issues; now confirmed on HBM4 track
- Micron positioned as #3 but gaining ground; expects to remain third-ranked through 2026

## Packaging Implications
- HBM4 12-layer and 16-layer stacks require tighter TSV (Through-Silicon Via) tolerances
- All HBM4 supply to NVIDIA integrates with TSMC CoWoS-L packaging
- Increased HBM layers → larger CoWoS interposer requirements → further CoWoS capacity pressure
