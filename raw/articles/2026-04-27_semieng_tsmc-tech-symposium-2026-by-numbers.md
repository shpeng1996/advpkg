# TSMC Tech Symposium 2026, By The Numbers

**Published:** April 27, 2026 | **Author:** Barry Pangrle | **Source:** Semiconductor Engineering

## Key Announcements from TSMC North America Technology Symposium 2026 (April 22, 2026)

### Process Node Roadmap

| Node | Target Date | Notes |
|------|-------------|-------|
| A14 | ~2026–2027 | Numbers presented again without changes |
| A16 | 2027 | **Delayed from previously advertised late 2026** |
| N2U | Enhancement of N2P | Scheduled for production in 2028 |
| A13 | 2029 | New announcement; optical shrink of A14 (~3% linear reduction = ~6% density gain) |
| A12 | 2029 | New announcement; aggressive acceleration |

Key note: A12 for 2029 is described as "an aggressive speedup in delivering a new technology node." N2 is ramping better in terms of defect density reduction than N3 — impressive given N3 was the last finFET node and N2 is the first NanoFlex node.

### Advanced Packaging Roadmap

**CoWoS:**
- TSMC is manufacturing the world's largest 5.5-reticle size CoWoS with >98% yield in 2026
- Roadmap shows CoWoS and SoW support out to 2029
- SoW-X projected to accommodate **64 HBM stacks** by 2029
- If 64 GB HBM stacks in 2029: total of 4 TB HBM on an SoW-X system

**CoWoS/SoW Integration Roadmap Summary:**
- Current: 5.5-reticle CoWoS
- 2028: 14-reticle CoWoS (announced at symposium)
- 2029: SoW-X with 64 HBM stacks

**SoIC:**
- A14-on-A14 SoIC targeted for production in 2029
- Delivers 1.8× higher die-to-die I/O density compared to N2-on-N2 SoIC

**Co-Packaged Optics (COUPE):**
- TSMC's Compact Universal Photonics Engine (COUPE) roadmap presented
- CPO brings optical signaling closer to the chip
- Expected energy efficiency improvements and latency reductions
- Production in 2026 (previously announced)

### Revenue Impact

AI/HPC has dramatically impacted TSMC revenue:
- AI/HPC has displaced smartphones as TSMC's top revenue generator
- Advanced packaging accounted for ~8% of TSMC revenue in 2025, expected to surpass 10% in 2026
- N7, N5, and N3 revenue percentages have been consistent over the last 6 quarters
- N2 beginning to impact revenue percentages as finished wafers are delivered

### Significance

The announcement of A12 for 2029 alongside A13 indicates TSMC is compressing its angstrom-era roadmap, planning two new nodes for 2029. A16's delay to 2027 adjusts the previous timeline. TSMC explicitly **does not require High-NA EUV** for A13 and A12, continuing to optimize existing low-NA EUV infrastructure.
