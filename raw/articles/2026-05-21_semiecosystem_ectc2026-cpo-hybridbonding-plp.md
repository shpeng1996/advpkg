# CPO, Hybrid Bonding, PLP Featured At ECTC

**By Mark LaPedus — Semiecosystem, May 21, 2026**

ECTC will be held from May 26-29 in Orlando, Florida. Applied Materials, ASML, CEA-Leti, Corning, Kioxia, GlobalFoundries, Intel, Resonac, TSMC, Ushio and many others will present papers at ECTC.

## Hybrid Bonding Papers

**Applied Materials (with Besi)**
Paper title: "First Demonstration of 450nm Pitch Cu-Cu Hybrid Bonding with 98% Yield Across 20M Interconnects for Ultra-Dense 3D Integration."

Key details:
- 450nm pitch copper hybrid bonding achieves 98% yield across 20 million via links
- Used Electron Beam Absorbed Current (EBAC) analysis to identify open defects
- TEM-EELS analysis revealed a thin carbon-rich layer at the Cu-Cu bond interface coinciding with large (111)-oriented Cu grains on top and bottom pads
- Key process optimizations: metallization, CMP, plasma treatments, post-bond annealing — enabled precise engineering of grain size and crystal orientation
- Results foundational for further scaling below 300nm pitch

AMAT recently entered the hybrid bonding market with an integrated system co-developed with BE Semiconductor Industries (Besi): the Kinex Integrated Die-to-Wafer Hybrid Bonding System.

**ASML**
Paper title: "Enabling Scalable Die-to-Wafer Hybrid Bonding Through Die Distortion Correction and Grid Measurement."

Key details:
- Systematic approach to optimize hybrid bonding overlay in D2W bonding
- High-precision grid measurements at key process steps
- Characterizes die distortion from: stress relaxation due to singulation, die placement, and bonding
- Less than 80nm bonding overlay performance (simulation) across heterogeneous dies
- Achieved through improved die placement accuracy and symmetric magnification correction (reticle writing correction, scanner-based precorrection, optimized film stress compensation)

## Panel-Level Packaging (PLP) Papers

**Resonac (Japan)**
Paper title: "Panel CMP Co-planarization of Heterogeneous Interfaces for Damascene Organic Interposers (L/S = 2/2μm)."

Key details:
- L/S = 2/2µm organic polymer damascene wiring on 320×320mm glass panels using panel-level CMP
- Two-step CMP (bulk Cu removal + barrier-metal CMP)
- Post-CMP step height <100nm at L/S = 2/2µm
- Uses grayscale laser direct imaging (LDI) lithography for simultaneous single-exposure via-trench formation
- Practical solution for HBM4-class high-density RDLs on panel-level organic interposers

**Ushio (Japan)**
Paper title: "First Demonstration of Stitching-Free Exposure over an Ultra-Large 18-Reticle Area with High-Resolution 1.5μm Line/Space on Glass Substrates."

Key details:
- Stitching-free exposure of 18-reticle area on 510mm × 515mm glass substrates
- Large-area projector lens enables precise 1.5µm L/S patterning
- Process margin of 22µm (ideal) for organic and glass substrate thickness variations
- 18-reticle area is more than 4× larger than conventional substrate stepper tools
- Crucial technological foundation for advancing high-performance AI packages on large glass panels

## Co-Packaged Optics (CPO) Papers

**GlobalFoundries + Corning**
Paper title: "Detachable Glass Waveguide Connector for Co-Packaged Optics on Silicon Photonics Platform with <1.5dB/Facet Passive Coupling and 280mW Power Handling."

Key details:
- Combines GF's Fotonix CPO technology with Corning's GLASSBRIDGE Connector architecture
- Passively-integrated detachable connector leveraging silicon and glass materials
- Coupling loss <1.5dB/facet passive
- 280mW power handling capability
- Establishes manufacturable pathway for next-gen co-packaged optics

**Intel**
Paper title: "Multi-channel and Multi-scale Optical Performance for a Detachable Edge-Coupling Connector with a Glass Coupler and Expanded Beam in CPO."

Key details:
- Low-loss and high-reliability edge-coupling architecture
- Fan-out glass coupler + detachable expanded beam connector
- Compatible with wafer-level and package-level assembly/test flows
- Coupling loss from fiber to PIC: approximately -1.55dB across all channels
- >100 plug/unplug cycles with <0.01dB performance variation, no failures
