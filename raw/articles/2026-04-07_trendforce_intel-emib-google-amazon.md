# Intel Advanced Packaging Reportedly Gains Traction vs. TSMC as Google, Amazon Weigh EMIB Adoption

**Publisher**: TrendForce | **Date**: April 7, 2026

## Summary

Intel's EMIB (Embedded Multi-die Interconnect Bridge) advanced packaging technology is reportedly gaining significant traction with major cloud service providers (CSPs) as an alternative to TSMC's CoWoS. Google and Amazon are both evaluating EMIB adoption for their custom AI accelerator chips.

## Key Findings

### Google — TPU v9 Targeting EMIB (2027)
- **Google** is planning to implement Intel EMIB in its **2027 TPU v9** accelerator design
- This would represent a major win for Intel's packaging services business
- Google's current TPUs (v7e, v8e) use TSMC CoWoS, making this a potential defection

### Amazon — AWS Custom Chip Consideration
- **Amazon Web Services (AWS)** is among the cloud giants weighing EMIB adoption for its custom AI chips (Trainium, Inferentia series)
- Intel's EMIB offers competitive bandwidth and potentially better pricing leverage vs. TSMC's fully-booked CoWoS

### Meta — MTIA Accelerators
- **Meta** is also considering EMIB for its MTIA (Meta Training and Inference Accelerator) chips
- The Meta consideration extends Intel's EMIB customer pipeline beyond traditional enterprise compute

### Intel's Value Proposition vs. TSMC
- Intel EMIB packages targeting **120×120 mm** (vs. industry-standard 100×100 mm), supporting **≥12 HBM stacks**
- EMIB-T (with TSV integration) positioned for HBM4 support
- Intel can offer EMIB on **non-Intel silicon** — packaging TSMC-fabbed dies in Intel's EMIB
- Pricing leverage: TSMC CoWoS fully booked through 2026, creating capacity/pricing pressure

## Revenue Outlook

- Intel CFO projected EMIB could generate **billions of dollars** in revenue as early as H2 2026
- Customer commitment timeline set for H2 2026
- Foveros-R and Foveros-B to be production-ready in 2026

## Competitive Implications

This development marks a significant shift in the advanced packaging competitive landscape. While TSMC has dominated AI packaging through CoWoS, Intel's EMIB — capable of packaging dies from any foundry — represents a credible alternative that CSPs are now seriously evaluating. The ability to package TSMC-fabbed dies using Intel's EMIB infrastructure adds a "foundry-agnostic" angle to Intel's packaging pitch.
