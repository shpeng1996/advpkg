## Summary

Phil Garrou's IFTLE column (a respected industry voice in heterogeneous integration) analyses advanced IC packaging as an emerging front in US-China trade/technology competition, examining the strategic significance of packaging for both sides.

## Key Claims

### Advanced Packaging as Geopolitical Chokepoint
- Advanced packaging is "the next front in the chip wars"
- US Commerce Department data: only **3% of global IC packaging occurs in the US**, and of that, **very little is advanced packaging**
- US Commerce Secretary: "One of the fundamental goals of the CHIPS and Science Act is creating an advanced packaging ecosystem in the US to ensure full start-to-finish chip production occurs domestically"
- **Chiplets as geopolitical chokepoint** (Deloitte prediction): "the complexity involved in sourcing and packaging multiple dies and components from diverse vendors from different regions will likely make chiplets a major geopolitical chokepoint in 2026"

### China's Packaging Strategy
- Chinese government views **advanced IC packaging** and **chiplets** as key to overcoming US export control sanctions
- Chiplets enable China to potentially circumvent restrictions on advanced semiconductor production equipment (which have so far blocked progression beyond ~5nm wafer processes)
- Chinese companies are investing heavily in OSAT, heterogeneous integration, and chiplet assembly
- China's OSAT industry (JCET, Tongfu Microelectronics, CARSEM) is scaling up advanced packaging capabilities

### Asia's Dominance in Packaging
- Asia (Taiwan, South Korea, China) dominates global advanced packaging capacity
- Taiwan (TSMC, ASE) + South Korea (Samsung, SK Hynix) together represent the vast majority of globally-strategic advanced packaging
- US dependency on Asian packaging infrastructure is a strategic vulnerability

## Policy Implications
- CHIPS Act R&D investment includes advanced packaging ecosystem development
- National Advanced Packaging Manufacturing Program (NAPMP): US government funding for domestic packaging R&D
- Goal: ensure US can package chips domestically without dependence on Taiwan/Korea/China
