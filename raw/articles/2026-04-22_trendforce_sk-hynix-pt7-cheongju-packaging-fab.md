SK hynix announced another major investment amid the AI memory boom, holding a groundbreaking ceremony for its P&T7 facility in Cheongju on April 22, 2026. The KRW 19 trillion (US$12.85 billion) project will be built in phases, with the WT (wafer testing) line targeted for October 2027, followed by the WLP (wafer-level packaging) line in February 2028.

SK hynix said P&T7 is a dedicated advanced packaging facility for AI memory, including HBM. It will mark the company's fifth production site in Cheongju, following M11, M12, M15, and M15X. The new fab is expected to create strong synergies with existing nearby operations, further reinforcing Cheongju as a key hub for advanced memory production.

The facility will include roughly 60,000 sqm of WLP process space across three floors and about 90,000 sqm of WT (wafer test) lines across seven floors, bringing total cleanroom area to around 150,000 sqm.

SK hynix is doubling down on HBM advanced packaging beyond Korea. The company has broken ground on its first U.S. semiconductor facility in Indiana, about two years after unveiling its 2024 investment plan. The Indiana fab is slated for full-scale operations in 2H28, with next-generation HBM—HBM4E and HBM5—set to anchor production.

Beyond the U.S. expansion in Indiana (approximately $3.87 billion / KRW 5.2 trillion), the company is investing KRW 19 trillion in Cheongju for P&T7.

## Earnings Preview: Record Margins in Sight (Q1 2026)

SK hynix is estimated to have posted KRW 53.89 trillion in revenue and KRW 38.49 trillion in operating profit for Q1 2026 on a consolidated basis—surging 205.5% and 417.3% year over year, respectively. ASP expansion is the key driver, with DRAM and NAND prices estimated to have jumped by up to 70% QoQ. The ramp-up of high-value products such as HBM3E is expected to lift DRAM operating margins to as high as 70–80%. SK hynix's first-quarter operating profit is projected to reach around KRW 40 trillion, implying an operating margin above 70%—a record high.
