TSMC announced on May 15 that it plans to sell shares in affiliate company Vanguard International Semiconductor Corporation (VIS). The foundry giant intends to sell up to 152 million common shares of VIS, equivalent to around 8.1% of Vanguard's outstanding shares. The transaction is expected to be carried out through block trades, with financial institutions as the primary buyers.

TSMC emphasized that the share sale is based on resource allocation considerations, aiming to focus resources more closely on its core businesses. Following the sale, TSMC's stake in Vanguard is expected to decline from around 27.1% to approximately 19%, with no further plans to reduce its holdings in the foreseeable future.

TSMC noted that since June 2024, it has no longer held a board seat at Vanguard, and the relationship between the two companies has gradually shifted from equity ties toward business cooperation.

## Strategic Significance for Advanced Packaging

TSMC stated that the disposal of Vanguard shares will not affect the existing strategic partnership between the two companies. TSMC and Vanguard are expected to continue cooperating in:
- Specialty process technologies
- Advanced packaging supply chains
- Outsourcing silicon interposer production to Vanguard
- Licensing gallium nitride (GaN) process technology to Vanguard

Industry sources say TSMC's rising capital spending on advanced nodes, AI HPC, packaging, and global expansion is driving a sharper focus on core priorities. The planned partial sale of Vanguard shares is seen as part of efforts to concentrate resources on:
- 2nm and A16 process nodes
- Advanced packaging including CoWoS and SoIC
- Capacity expansion in the U.S., Japan, and Germany

Note: VIS/VSMC (Singapore fab) was reported in May 2026 to be entering silicon interposer production for CoWoS supply chain (2027 mass production target), reinforcing the strategic value of the TSMC–Vanguard partnership for advanced packaging even as equity ties are reduced.
