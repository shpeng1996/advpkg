## Summary

Apple is set to upgrade packaging technology from the current InFO (Integrated Fan-Out) process to WMCM (Wafer-Level Multi-Chip Module) packaging for the A20-series chip used in the iPhone 18, alongside its transition to a 2nm process.

## Key Technical Points

**WMCM vs. InFO**
Compared with InFO, WMCM's defining advantage lies in its ability to integrate multiple chips with different functions in parallel on the redistribution layer (RDL), including the application processor, memory, and high-speed I/O dies, driving:
- Higher interconnect density
- Improved packaging yield
- More advanced thermal management

This thermal efficiency improvement is a core motivation for Apple's transition to WMCM for the A20, helping manage the higher heat output of the 2nm node.

## Production Context

- **Longtan AP3**: TSMC is upgrading existing InFO equipment to support WMCM production
- **Chiayi AP7**: New dedicated WMCM production line under construction; Phase 2 to be Apple-dedicated WMCM base
- **Target capacity**: ~60,000 wafers/month by end of 2026

## Significance for InFO Technology

This article marks a critical inflection point: while InFO (commercialised 2016 for Apple A10) remains in production for current devices, WMCM represents its successor for flagship SoC packaging. InFO's commercial lifecycle may be transitioning from lead product to secondary/mature status.
