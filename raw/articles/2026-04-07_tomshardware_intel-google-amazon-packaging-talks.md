# Intel reportedly in talks with Google and Amazon over advanced packaging

By Luke James, published 7 April 2026 (Tom's Hardware)

Citing a WIRED report, Tom's Hardware reports that Intel is in active talks with Google and Amazon to provide advanced chip packaging services for their custom AI processors ("Multiple sources say that Intel has been in ongoing talks with at least two large customers for its advanced packaging services: Google and Amazon"). Google, Amazon, and Intel all declined to comment on specific customer relationships.

## Financial context
Intel Foundry CFO Dave Zinsner said at the Morgan Stanley TMT conference that Intel is "close to closing some deals that are in the billions of dollars per year, in terms of revenue on packaging." At the January Q4 2025 earnings call, Zinsner said he had revised packaging revenue projections over the prior 12–18 months from "hundreds of millions of dollars" to "well north of $1 billion," and suggested packaging could achieve the same ~40% gross margins as Intel's core product business.

This contrasts with current financials: Intel Foundry posted $4.5 billion revenue in Q4 2025 with a $2.5 billion operating loss; external foundry revenue for full-year 2025 totaled just $307 million (mostly U.S. government contracts and residual Altera work). The Foundry division lost $10.3 billion on $17.8 billion revenue for all of 2025, driven largely by 18A ramp costs.

## Technology: EMIB-T rollout
Intel's advanced packaging portfolio centers on **EMIB** (2.5D, silicon bridges embedded in package substrate connecting chiplets) and **Foveros** (3D die-stacking). The next-generation **EMIB-T** adds through-silicon vias (TSVs) to the bridge for improved power delivery and signal integrity, and is set to roll out in production fabs **this year (2026)**. EMIB-T supports packages **up to 120x180mm** and can accommodate **more than 38 bridges and over 12 reticle-sized dies**.

Naga Chandrasekaran, head of Intel Foundry, told WIRED: "Even more so than the silicon itself, chip packaging is going to transform how this AI revolution comes to fruition over the next decade."

## Capacity buildout
- **Fab 9** (Rio Rancho, New Mexico): received $500 million from the CHIPS Act; operational since January 2024; stacks chips via Foveros, interconnects via EMIB.
- **Penang, Malaysia**: advanced packaging complex 99% complete; first-phase assembly/test operations to begin later in 2026 (per Malaysian PM Anwar Ibrahim, after briefing with Intel CEO Lip-Bu Tan in March 2026).
- **Outsourcing**: Intel has outsourced EMIB production for the first time to **Amkor's Songdo K5 facility** (South Korea), with additional sites planned in **Portugal and Arizona**.
