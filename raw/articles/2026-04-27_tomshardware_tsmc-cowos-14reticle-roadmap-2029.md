At the North American Technology Symposium 2026, TSMC revealed its updated CoWoS packaging roadmap with major enhancements. Within chipmaking, the reticle limit is the largest size that a chip can be printed within a single step of the manufacturing process. TSMC's previous CoWoS-based system-in-packages (SiPs) roadmaps topped out at a 9.5-reticle size. Now the company expects to produce 14-reticle and over 14-reticle-sized System-in-Packages (SiPs) with up to 24 HBM5E stacks by 2029.

"AI compute scaling is driven by the combination of advanced logic, SoIC 3D stacking, and CoWoS technologies," a statement by TSMC reads.

## CoWoS Roadmap 2026–2029

**Current (2026):** TSMC has begun mass production of 5.5-reticle-sized CoWoS SiPs, supporting up to 12 HBM3E/HBM4 stacks and has achieved yields over 98%.

**2027:** 9.5-reticle-sized interposer supporting 12 HBM5 stacks, requiring a 120 mm × 150 mm substrate.

**2028:** 14-reticle-sized interposer capable of carrying 20 3D-stacked compute chiplets and 20 HBM5 modules.

**2029:** Over 14-reticle sizes, with up to 24 HBM5E stacks. TSMC's A14 will be in mass production by 2029.

One standard reticle measures 26 mm × 33 mm (858 mm²), so a 14-reticle-sized interposer measures 12,020 mm² — about the size of a small plate, and slightly larger than a CD.

## Transistor and Bandwidth Scaling

Based on TSMC's expectations, customers will be able to put at least 24 3D-stacked compute chiplets on one 14-reticle-sized CoWoS interposer by 2029. This translates to:

- **48x more compute transistors** vs current generation by 2029
- **34x more memory bandwidth** by 2029

Such gargantuan multi-chiplet processors confirm that advanced packaging is now the de facto scaling engine for the industry. TSMC's lateral CoWoS and vertical SoIC technologies enable faster growth of transistor budgets than traditional Moore's Law scaling.

## Cooling and Physical Implications

An SiP using a 14-reticle-sized interposer (12,020 mm²) will consume enormous power and require exotic cooling solutions — such as cold plates capable of handling up to 4.4 kW TDPs, immersion cooling, or a combination of both. The dimensions alone will redefine how AI servers are built.

## Context

Such high integration is designed to meet insatiable AI accelerator demand for both compute and memory bandwidth, and signals that packaging, not lithography, acts as a primary driver for semiconductor technologies.
