# Samsung Reportedly Plans 50% HBM Capacity Surge in 2026, Spotlight on HBM4

**Publisher**: TrendForce | **Date**: December 30, 2025

## Summary

Samsung Electronics is executing an aggressive HBM capacity expansion in 2026, planning a 50% surge in monthly HBM production wafers, targeting approximately 250,000 wafers per month by year-end with focus on HBM4 and recovery from HBM3E qualification setbacks.

## Key Findings

### Samsung HBM Capacity Target
- Samsung plans to boost HBM production to **~250,000 wafers/month** by end of 2026
- This represents a ~47% increase from the 2025 level of ~170,000 wafers/month
- The expansion is concentrated in **HBM4** rather than HBM3E, reflecting the product generation transition

### Strategic Context
- Samsung suffered significant HBM3E qualification delays with NVIDIA in 2024–2025, ceding market share to SK Hynix
- Samsung eventually **passed NVIDIA HBM4 prototype testing** (reported August 2025)
- The 50% capacity surge is partly a recovery strategy to regain NVIDIA HBM share lost during HBM3E issues
- Samsung offered ~30% HBM3E price discount to challenge SK Hynix during the transition period

### HBM4 Technical Specifications (Samsung)
- Samsung's custom HBM4 design targets 12-layer initial, with 16-layer push in H2 2026
- Samsung and SK Hynix both began delivering **paid HBM4 samples to NVIDIA** ahead of Q1 2026 contract finalization
- Samsung's HBM4 custom design completion (alongside custom HBM4E design): targeted May–June 2026

### Comparison with SK Hynix
- SK Hynix: similarly targeting ~250,000 HBM wafers/month by end 2026 (50% increase)
- SK Hynix holds structural advantage: M15X facility came online 4 months early (February 2026)
- Market share expectation: SK Hynix ~55–60%, Samsung ~30–35%, Micron ~10% for HBM4 in 2026

### Broader HBM Market Dynamics
- Total industry HBM capacity expected to grow 80–100% by end of 2026 vs. 2024 baseline
- HBM4E (10 GT/s, 2.5 TB/s, 80W) expected to reach 40% of 2027 HBM market
- HBM5 development targeted for 2029
