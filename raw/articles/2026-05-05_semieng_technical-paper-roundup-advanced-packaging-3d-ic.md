# Chip Industry Technical Paper Roundup: May 5, 2026

**Source**: Semiconductor Engineering, Linda Christensen, May 5th, 2026

New technical papers recently added to Semiconductor Engineering's library covering advanced packaging and heterogeneous integration topics:

## Advanced Packaging Related Papers

### 1. Rethinking Compute Substrates for 3D-Stacked Near-Memory LLM Decoding: Microarchitecture-Scheduling Co-Design
**Institutions**: University of Edinburgh, Peking University, Cambridge, CAS, HKUST  
**Relevance**: Architecture research for 3D-stacked near-memory computing — directly relevant to future HBM/CoWoS design choices and in-memory compute for AI LLM inference.

### 2. In-SoIC ESD Protection for Chiplet-Based 3D Microsystems: Future Research Directions
**Institution**: UC Riverside  
**Relevance**: Electrostatic discharge (ESD) protection within TSMC SoIC 3D stacked structures — a critical reliability challenge for chiplet-based systems. Identifies future research directions for SoIC-integrated ESD.

### 3. Epoxy Composites Reinforced with Long Al₂O₃ Nanowires for Enhanced Thermal Management in Advanced Semiconductor Packaging
**Institutions**: Georgia Tech, NCKU (National Cheng Kung University)  
**Relevance**: New thermal interface material (TIM) using alumina nanowire-reinforced epoxy composites. Directly addresses the thermal management bottleneck in advanced 2.5D/3D packaging (CoWoS, SoIC stacks).

### 4. Chipmunq: A Fault-Tolerant Compiler for Chiplet Quantum Architectures
**Institution**: TU Munich  
**Relevance**: Quantum computing on chiplet architectures — emerging application domain for heterogeneous integration. Signals early-stage research into chiplet-based quantum systems.

### 5. Highly Energy-Efficient Manifold Microchannel for Cooling Electronics with a Coefficient of Performance Over 100,000
**Institution**: KAIST  
**Relevance**: Breakthrough liquid cooling efficiency (COP >100,000) for high-power electronics — highly relevant to advanced packaging thermal challenges (CoWoS + SoIC stacks with AI GPU powers exceeding 1 kW).

### 6. Nonvolatile Photonic Field-Programmable Coupler Array
**Institution**: University of Oxford  
**Relevance**: Photonic integrated circuit (PIC) technology for optical computing/interconnect — relevant to co-packaged optics (CPO) ecosystem.

## Other Notable Papers (May 5-6, 2026)
- **High-NA EUV Lithography: Extending The STCC Formula** (Science Tokyo) — lithography technology relevant to advanced node packaging requirements
- **A Detailed Evaluation of a Production Server With High-End MRDIMM Main Memory** (BSC, Micron, Intel, UPC) — MRDIMM packaging evaluation relevant to next-gen server memory packaging

## Significance for Advanced Packaging Wiki

These papers confirm several emerging research themes:
1. **Thermal management gap**: Multiple institutions targeting TIM and liquid cooling solutions for advanced 3D packages — validating the thermal management knowledge gap identified in wiki/concepts/thermal-management.md
2. **SoIC reliability**: ESD protection within SoIC 3D structures is an active and unresolved research challenge
3. **3D near-memory computing**: Growing academic interest in co-designing 3D-stacked memory+compute architecture (HBM + processing elements)
4. **Quantum chiplets**: Early research into chiplet-based quantum systems — watch this space for 2027+ heterogeneous integration applications
