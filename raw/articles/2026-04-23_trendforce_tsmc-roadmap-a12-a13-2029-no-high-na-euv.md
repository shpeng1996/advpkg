# TSMC Latest Roadmap: A12, A13 for 2029 Without High-NA EUV; A16 Volume Production Delayed to 2027

**Published:** April 23, 2026 | **Source:** TrendForce

## Key Announcements from TSMC North America Technology Symposium 2026

### New Process Nodes: A13 and A12 for 2029

At its 2026 North America Technology Symposium (April 22, 2026), TSMC announced two new process nodes:

**A13 (1.3nm-class):**
- An **optical shrink of A14**, reducing linear dimensions by ~3% (to ~97% scale)
- ~6% increase in transistor density over A14
- Targeted for production in **2029**
- Does NOT require High-NA EUV lithography

**A12 (1.2nm-class):**
- An "aggressive speedup" — not merely an incremental node
- Targeted for production in **2029** (same year as A13)
- Also does NOT require High-NA EUV lithography
- Compresses TSMC's angstrom-era roadmap significantly

### A16 Delay: 2027 Instead of Late 2026

A16 volume production is now slated for **2027**, marking a delay from its previously expected late-2026 timeline. Previous symposium (2025) had shown A16 for late 2026.

### Full Process Roadmap as of April 2026

| Node | Timeline | High-NA EUV? |
|------|----------|--------------|
| N2 | Ramping (2025–2026) | No |
| N2P | 2026–2027 | No |
| N2U | 2028 | No |
| A16 | **2027** (revised from late 2026) | Yes (Intel's version) / No (TSMC's approach) |
| A14 | 2026–2027 (volume) | No |

| A12 | **2029** (new) | No |

## TSMC's High-NA EUV Position

TSMC explicitly does NOT plan to adopt High-NA EUV systems for A13 and A12. This is a significant statement, because:
- ASML had expected High-NA EUV to enter large-scale mass production in 2027–2028
- ASML projected up to €60 billion in revenue from High-NA by 2030
- TSMC's decision forces ASML to "quietly pivot" — focusing High-NA sales on Intel and Samsung while shifting TSMC revenue model toward more Low-NA EUV systems

**Impact on ASML:**
- Low-NA EUV remains strong for TSMC through at least 2029
- ASML is raising its 2026 sales outlook to €36–40B on memory and logic demand
- South Korea (Samsung, SK Hynix) share of ASML sales hits 45% (up from earlier levels)

## Advanced Packaging Highlights at the Symposium

Also announced at the symposium (overlapping with semiengineering.com coverage):
- CoWoS 5.5-reticle size in volume production with >98% yield (2026)
- 14-reticle CoWoS planned for 2028
- SoW-X (System on Wafer eXtended) capable of 64 HBM stacks by 2029
- A14-on-A14 SoIC targeted for 2029: 1.8× higher die-to-die I/O density vs N2-on-N2

## Industry Implications

The dual announcement of A12 and A13 for 2029 without High-NA EUV represents a bold strategy by TSMC:
1. Maintains cost efficiency by avoiding the very expensive High-NA EUV transition
2. Compresses the angstrom-era roadmap by putting two nodes in the same year
3. Forces Intel (which plans to use High-NA EUV for 18A) into a differentiated but potentially more expensive position
4. Creates uncertainty for ASML's High-NA EUV revenue projections
