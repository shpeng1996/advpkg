# Korea's POSTECH Develops Tech for Stable 10+ Layer Chip Stacking, Achieving 4× the Density of 12-Hi HBM

**Source:** TrendForce (citing POSTECH, ETNews, Results in Engineering) | **Date:** 2026-07-10

South Korea's **Pohang University of Science and Technology (POSTECH)** has developed a new chip stacking technology that can stably stack more than 10 layers of ultra-thin semiconductor chips, each just one-fifth the thickness of a human hair (~14 µm silicon). Research team led by **Professor Kim Seok** developed a process that integrates chip transfer and metal bonding into a single step.

The technology achieved an integration density approximately **4× higher than conventional 12-layer HBM**, allowing significantly more chips to be stacked within the same package height. The study was published in the international engineering journal **Results in Engineering** (published online via ScienceDirect).

## Technical Details

### Problem Addressed
As chips become thinner (below ~tens of micrometers), they become increasingly susceptible to bending and cracking—a challenge that grows more severe with additional stacking layers. Conventional HBM stacking uses TC-NCF or MR-MUF processes that are approaching their limit at 12-16 high stacks.

### Solution: Combined Transfer Printing + In-Situ Bonding
The researchers combined two technologies into a **single simultaneous process**:
1. **Transfer printing**: Precisely positions chips
2. **In-situ bonding**: Forms metal interconnections during transfer

The process completes chip transfer, bonding, and electrical connection in a single step—eliminating the sequential multi-step process of conventional stacking.

## Key Specifications

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Die thickness | ~14 µm (one-fifth of human hair) |
| Maximum stable stack | 10+ layers |
| Density vs. 12-Hi HBM | ~4× |
| Process temperature | <180°C |
| Process pressure | <20 kPa |
| Chip type validated | Silicon with vertical TSV interconnects + lateral wiring |
| Interlayer alignment error | Minimal (after repeated stacking) |
| Warpage | Significantly reduced vs. conventional |

## Applications
- **HBM beyond 12-high**: Enable >12-layer stacks within JEDEC height constraints
- **Chiplet packaging**: Integrate multiple functional chips in single package at 4× current density
- **Micro LED displays**: Next-generation display chiplet integration
- **AI semiconductor performance**: Substantially improving compute density within same footprint

This development is particularly relevant given:

- Single-step transfer+bond process could reduce per-layer cost vs. hybrid bonding's complex process requirements
