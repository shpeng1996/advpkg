# Samsung Files New HBM Dummy Die Patent to Enhance High-Stack Reliability; Seen as Aimed at 16-Layer HBM5

Source: TrendForce (citing ETNews) | 2026-07-02

As the industry advances toward higher-layer HBM, Samsung is reportedly developing a new structural design to improve package reliability. According to ETNews, Samsung Electronics has filed a patent targeting reliability issues in high-bandwidth memory (HBM) packaging. With the industry transitioning to higher-layer HBM such as HBM4E and HBM5, the company is redesigning the dummy die that protects the memory stack to improve structural integrity and manufacturing yield.

## Patent Description

The HBM packaging patent describes a technology that shapes the sidewalls of the topmost dummy die into a **3-step terraced structure with a curved profile**. Samsung is expected to combine the technology with its existing HBM packaging technologies:
- Hybrid bonding
- HPB (Heat Path Block)

Industry sources note that in HBM stacks with 12 layers or more, warpage of the topmost dummy die is a key factor affecting manufacturing yield, suggesting the technology is intended for HBM5 products with 16 layers or more.

## Why the Dummy Die Matters

The dummy die sits at the top of the HBM stack. Its functions:
- Maintains required package height
- Protects the memory stack
- Assists with heat dissipation

**Yield context:**
- 8-layer → 12-layer HBM: yield falls 10–20 percentage points
- 16-layer HBM5: yields fall to ~40–60%

Improving the dummy die structure helps mitigate warpage and thermal expansion mismatch — two major contributors to yield loss.

## Key Design Innovations

1. **Deep Groove Sawing Process**: High-precision laser dicing (vs. conventional mechanical blade sawing) — enables deeper, more precise cuts while reducing crystal structure damage
2. **Inverted pyramid profile**: Narrower bonding interface at bottom, wider top surface → improved mechanical strength over straight sidewalls
3. **Trenches (Tr) in non-bonding region (NBR)**: Prevent dicing debris from contaminating the bonding interface → improves fusion bonding reliability
4. **Thermal management**: 
   - Defines 1–10 µm vertical distance between underside of bonding insulating layer and horizontal extension plane → preserves existing heat-transfer efficiency
   - Protruding surface reduces EMC (epoxy molding compound) volume → further improves heat dissipation

## Target Application

The patent is seen as preparation for **HBM5 with 16-layer stacks**, where dummy die warpage is the primary yield limiter. Combined with existing HPB heat management and hybrid bonding, this patent represents Samsung's reliability engineering roadmap for high-stack HBM.
