# GlobalFoundries Reportedly Sees Silicon Photonics Revenue Doubling in 2026, Passing $1B by 2028

**Source**: TrendForce News, 2026-05-07

As major foundries including TSMC and Samsung accelerate their push into silicon photonics, specialty chipmaker GlobalFoundries continues to build steady momentum. According to the Investing.com transcript, management increasingly views silicon photonics as a core growth engine, expecting revenue to roughly double in 2026 and potentially exceed US$1 billion by the end of 2028.

For reference, during the Q&A session at the May 5 earnings call, analysts pointed to expectations of roughly US$400 million in revenue contribution in 2026.

## SCALE Platform for CPO

Notably, GlobalFoundries said in its press release that it has launched its SCALE (Silicon Photonics Co-packaged Advanced Light Engine) optical module solution for CPO in May. The company described SCALE as the industry's first Optical Compute Interconnect (OCI) MSA–compliant platform purpose-built for modern AI scale-up architectures. Through SCALE, GF aims to enable high-bandwidth, energy-efficient connectivity at scale for its customers.

GlobalFoundries' silicon photonics portfolio is gaining fast traction, driven by the industry's shift toward pluggable and co-packaged optics as AI data center networking demands continue to scale rapidly. CEO Tim Breen said that GF is now designed in at three of the top four pluggable optical transceiver companies, with customers continuing to report strong feedback on its pluggable portfolio supporting 1.6T solutions and a clear roadmap toward 3.2T and beyond.

## Financial Highlights (Q1 2026)

- Q1 2026 revenue: US$1.63 billion (in line with market expectations)
- Q2 2026 guidance: ~US$1.76 billion ±$25 million (vs. LSEG estimate $1.74B)
- Target: exit 2026 with gross margins at or above 30%
- Silicon photonics 2026 revenue estimate: ~US$400 million (analyst consensus)
- Silicon photonics 2028 target: exceed US$1 billion

## Pricing Outlook

As mature-node foundries such as UMC and TSMC's VIS move to raise prices, GlobalFoundries also noted its pricing outlook. CEO Tim Breen said a large portion of GF's portfolio is governed by long-term agreements that have remained stable for years. However, a smaller segment tied to short-term pricing is seeing tightening supply-demand dynamics. GF plans to implement price increases in this segment beginning H2 2026 and extending into 2027.

For capacity-constrained areas, GF is also engaging customers on advance payments to secure supply as CapEx is ramped in constrained technologies such as FDX (Fully Depleted Silicon-On-Insulator).

## Key Significance for Advanced Packaging

- GF's SCALE platform is OCI MSA-compliant — directly competes with TSMC COUPE™ in the CPO ecosystem
- GF positions itself as a foundry-neutral CPO supplier vs. TSMC's vertically integrated COUPE approach
- 1.6T→3.2T roadmap aligns with AI datacenter bandwidth scaling (NVIDIA Rubin, next-gen switches)
