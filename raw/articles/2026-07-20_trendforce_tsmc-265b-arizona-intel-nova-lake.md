# [News] TSMC's $265B U.S. Bet: "No Food Left on the Table" Despite 4-5x Higher Fab Construction Costs

Source: CNBC, Reuters, City of Phoenix (via TrendForce) | Date: 2026-07-20

## Key Findings

### TSMC Arizona Expansion Details

- Total U.S. commitment: **US$265 billion** — largest foreign direct investment in U.S. history
- U.S. fab construction costs: **4–5× higher than Taiwan**, per CFO Wendell Huang
- Gross margin dilution: 2–3% early stage, widening to 3–4% in later stages

### Arizona Fab Status (per Reuters/City of Phoenix)

| Fab | Node | Status |
|-----|------|--------|
| 1st fab | 4nm | **Volume production since late 2024**; yields now matching Taiwan operations |
| 2nd fab | 3nm | **Construction complete**; volume manufacturing scheduled 2027 |
| 3rd fab | — | **Under construction** |
| 4th fab | — | **Preparatory work begun** |
| AP facility | Advanced Packaging | **Site prep started** |

- When complete: Arizona will host **~30% of TSMC's 2nm-and-beyond capacity**
- Final footprint: **12 wafer fabs + AP facilities + R&D center** in Arizona

### Intel Nova Lake Shift — Opportunities for TSMC

- Intel reportedly moving **80–90% of Nova Lake compute tile production back in-house** on 18A (not TSMC 2nm)
- Intel 18A yields reportedly reached **85%** (up from 65%), with Clearwater Forest (288-core Xeon 6+) in early production
- The in-house shift **frees up TSMC 2nm capacity** for other leading-edge customers
- **AMD** is a key beneficiary: Venice (2nm TSMC) and Instinct MI450/MI455/MI550 are key products

### AMD Upcoming Products (week of 2026-07-20)

- AMD "Venice" EPYC server CPUs: among first enterprise CPUs on TSMC 2nm
- Instinct AI accelerators (MI450, MI455, MI550): key AI data center push
