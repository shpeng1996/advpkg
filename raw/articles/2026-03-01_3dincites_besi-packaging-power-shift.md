# BESI at the Center of the Packaging Power Shift

**Source**: 3D InCites — published 2026-03-01

## Overview

BE Semiconductor Industries (Besi) is positioned as a key equipment supplier at the intersection of advanced packaging technology transitions, particularly in hybrid bonding and die-attach equipment.

## Business Position (2025–2026)

- **Die attach** represents approximately **80% of Besi's 2025 revenue**, while hybrid bonding segment is expanding
- **Applied Materials** holds a **9% stake** in Besi and collaborates on the **Kinex platform**
- Kinex is a fully integrated **D2W (die-to-wafer) hybrid bonding solution** approaching high-volume manufacturing readiness

## Key Equipment Platform

### Datacon 8800 CHAMEO ultra plus AC
- Besi's flagship **Hybrid Bonding platform**
- Ultra-high placement accuracy for high-density interconnects
- Targets 3D integration and advanced packaging applications
- Key capabilities: exceptional optical alignment, cleanliness concepts, high throughput

## Hybrid Bonding Roadmap
- Presentation at **Wafer Level Packaging Symposium 2026** (keynote): *"Scaling Interconnects Below 10 µm with Hybrid Bonding and Fluxless TCB"*
- Besi hosted/participated in **Hybrid Bonding Symposium 2026** (January 22–23, 2026, SEMI HQ, Milpitas, CA)
- Target: scaling die-to-wafer hybrid bonding below **10 µm pitch** toward high-volume production

## Competitive Context
- Major competitors in bonding equipment: EV Group (lithography-side alignment, W2W), Tokyo Electron (TEL)
- Besi focuses on **die-to-wafer** (D2W) use case; EV Group on **wafer-to-wafer** (W2W)
- Applied Materials Kinex collaboration creates integrated D2W ecosystem

## Strategic Significance
- Besi's equipment enables the **D2W hybrid bonding** use case critical for:
  - Logic-on-logic chiplet stacking (e.g., AMD MI series with SoIC-like technologies)
  - Memory-on-logic integration
  - AI accelerator die stacking
- As HVM hybrid bonding volumes grow (post-2026), Besi and EV Group are the primary equipment beneficiaries
