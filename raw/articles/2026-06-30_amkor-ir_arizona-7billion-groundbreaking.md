TEMPE, Ariz.--(BUSINESS WIRE)--Oct. 6, 2025-- Amkor Technology, Inc. (Nasdaq: AMKR) announced the groundbreaking and expanded planned investment of its new outsourced semiconductor advanced packaging and test campus in Peoria, Arizona, in collaboration with the Trump Administration.

The expanded investment includes additional cleanroom space and a second greenfield packaging and test facility, increasing total project investment by more than $5 billion, to a total of $7 billion across two phases. Amkor's investment supports the broader strategy to strengthen U.S. semiconductor leadership and will be the first U.S.-based high-volume advanced packaging facility.

Upon completion of both phases, the campus will offer over 750,000 square feet of cleanroom space and as many as 3,000 high-quality jobs. Construction of the first manufacturing facility is expected to be completed in mid-2027, with production beginning in early 2028. The new greenfield facilities will serve as a cornerstone of America's advanced packaging capabilities, supporting key customers including Apple and NVIDIA. The expanded investment is supported by the CHIPS for America Program, the Advanced Manufacturing Investment Tax Credit, and state and local governments.

Key quotes:
- Giel Rutten (Amkor President & CEO): "We're building a facility to meet our customers' most advanced needs that will help shape the future of semiconductor manufacturing in the United States."
- Howard Lutnick (U.S. Secretary of Commerce): the partnership "will bring high volume advanced packaging to the U.S. for the first time."
- Sabih Khan (Apple COO): Amkor's new facility "will package and test the Apple silicon produced at TSMC Arizona just down the road," part of Apple's $600 billion U.S. manufacturing commitment.
- Jensen Huang (NVIDIA Founder & CEO): "Amkor's new Arizona facility is a defining milestone in bringing this capability home... onshoring the AI technology stack."
- Jun He (VP, Advanced Packaging Technology and Service, TSMC): the campus "will provide customers with a streamlined, domestic ecosystem... to help define the future of local semiconductor manufacturing."

Additional figures cited: Arizona's chip industry has more than $210 billion in projects completed or planned, with TSMC's expansion alone valued at $165 billion (per Arizona Technology Council statement). The campus is positioned to complement TSMC's front-end wafer fabrication in Arizona for full end-to-end domestic semiconductor manufacturing.
