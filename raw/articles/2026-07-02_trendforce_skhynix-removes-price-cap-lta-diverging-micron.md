As Micron announced another major Strategic Customer Agreement (SCA) with General Motors, the structure of global memory makers' long-term supply agreements (LTAs) has come into focus.

## SK hynix Removes Price Cap

According to industry sources, SK hynix is now adopting contracts that eliminate the industry-standard price cap, marking a departure from Micron's approach.

- Unlike traditional memory contracts (with price ceilings to limit volatility), SK hynix's new structure allows spot market price increases to be fully reflected in contract pricing when supply shortages push market prices higher
- SK hynix may now be the only major memory supplier not applying a price cap in its long-term agreements
- This effectively reworks its LTA framework to better capture profitability during upcycle conditions

## Contract Duration Lengthening

Both SK hynix and Samsung are extending LTA durations from the traditional one-year term to **three to five years**.

## Samsung LTA Strategy

Samsung Electronics addressed supply strategies for HBM3E, HBM4, and HBM4E tailored to individual customers during its DS Division global strategy meeting. LTA strategy with major cloud and big tech clients was a key agenda item:
- Q1 2026 earnings call: Samsung stated it was pursuing LTAs for memory products in response to customer requests; had already signed contracts with some clients

## Micron's SCA Model (Contrast)

Unlike SK hynix, Micron's SCAs reportedly include:
- **Price cap**: Set at Q2 2026 market price for existing products
- **Price floor**: Applied over the full term of agreements
- **Volume commitments**: Binding
- **Next-gen products** (HBM, DDR6, LPDDR6): Priced separately through negotiated terms

### Micron SCA with General Motors (July 1, 2026)
- Long-term supply of LPDRAM, NOR, and UFS NAND for GM
- Part of Micron's 16 strategic customer contracts disclosed during Q3 FY2026 results
- U.S.-based supply foundation from Manassas, Virginia ($2B modernization, already in production)
- GM deal: automotive platform alignment, long-cycle supply security

## Market Context

SK hynix and Samsung are currently benefiting from the most profitable memory cycle on record. The removal of price caps positions SK hynix to capture full upside from the upcycle, while Micron's capped SCA approach offers customers price stability with supply guarantees.
