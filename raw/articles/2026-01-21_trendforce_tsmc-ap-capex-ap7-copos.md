## Summary

TrendForce (2026-01-21) reports on TSMC's ambitious advanced packaging capital expenditure plans through 2027, with focus on AP7 (Chiayi) facility targeting Wafer-level Multi-Chip Module (WMCM) and CoPoS.

### CapEx Projections
- TSMC Advanced Packaging CapEx: projected **24% CAGR** in 2025–27
- Reflects intensifying AI accelerator demand and need for capacity leadership
- Areas of investment: CoWoS expansion, CoPoS pilot, SoIC capacity, AP7 construction

### AP7 Chiayi Facility
- **Phase 2**: equipment installation and testing already begun (as of early 2026); production expected in 2026
- **Phase 1**: equipment move-in scheduled 2026; mass production 2027
- AP7 designated for: **WMCM** (Wafer-level Multi-Chip Module), **CoPoS**, and potentially co-packaged optics (CPO)

### Other Facilities (2025-12-04 context)
- **AP7 Phase 2**: 2026 output targeted
- **Arizona P6**: eyed as US packaging hub (Arizona Advanced Packaging site)
- **AP8** (Tainan, former Innolux): allocating capacity for SoIC, CPO, and FOPLP

### CoWoS Capacity Targets
- End-2026 target: 150,000 wafers/month (some sources cite 120K–130K more conservatively)
- Tainan AP8 began small-scale production end-2025
- AP6B Zhunan received occupancy permit December 2025
- AP5B Taichung: operations began 1H 2025

### WMCM Technology
- WMCM = Wafer-level Multi-Chip Module; allows integration of multiple chiplets on a single interposer at wafer scale
- Complements CoWoS by enabling even larger integration complexes for next-gen AI accelerators
