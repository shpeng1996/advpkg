## Summary

Intel is reportedly reconsidering offering its 18A process node to external foundry customers. Separately, Intel's CFO signalled that EMIB packaging technology could generate **billions of dollars** in revenue as early as 2H 2026, driven by strong customer engagement including Apple and Qualcomm.

## EMIB as Packaging-as-a-Service

### Revenue Potential
- Intel CFO stated EMIB adoption could generate **billions of dollars** potentially beginning **2H 2026**
- Multiple deals with customers worth billions of dollars annually reportedly close to being finalised
- This positions Intel Foundry's packaging as an independent revenue stream, separate from logic manufacturing

### Customer Traction
- **Apple**: exploring EMIB for potential future products (AI or compute-focused devices)
- **Qualcomm**: seeking EMIB expertise for chiplet integration
- **AI ASIC customers**: unspecified hyperscalers or custom chip designers
- Intel can potentially package TSMC-manufactured dies using EMIB — a heterogeneous foundry model

### Why EMIB Attracts External Customers
- EMIB's 120×120mm package exceeds NVIDIA's 100×100mm — enables larger multi-die configurations
- EMIB-T adds TSV support → direct path to HBM4 integration
- Unique capability: EMIB allows dies from different foundries to be packaged together on one substrate

## 18A External Foundry Reconsideration

- Intel had previously deprioritized 18A for external customers after losing major wins
- Revised strategy: offer 18A as foundry option alongside packaging services
- EMIB packaging is seen as the "anchor product" that draws customers into Intel Foundry ecosystem

## Implications
This article is significant because it frames Intel's advanced packaging as a competitive moat separate from process technology leadership. Even if Intel loses foundry business on logic, EMIB may generate standalone billions — a "packaging services" business model.
