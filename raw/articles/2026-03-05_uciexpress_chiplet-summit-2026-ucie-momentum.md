Chiplet Summit 2026 underscored the accelerating momentum behind the chiplet movement and the central role UCIe is playing in shaping it. With strong representation from member companies across keynotes, technical sessions, tutorials, and live demonstrations, the event highlighted how quickly UCIe has evolved from specification to implementation.

Key participants in sponsored presentations: **Arm, Marvell, Cadence, Synopsys, Siemens, and Eliyan**. Exhibitors: Alphawave Semi, Cadence, Siemens, Synopsys, Keysight Technologies, Tenstorrent, Menta, VeriSilicon, proteanTecs, InPsytech, Extoll.

## Milestone: Industry-First Live UCIe-S Interoperability Demo

Intel and Cadence showcased **the industry's first live UCIe-S interoperability demonstration** using the "Cameron Creek" test chip. By connecting independently designed Intel and Cadence chiplets implementing 16G UCIe-S PHY IP, the demo validated **true cross-vendor interoperability** and successful UCIe Interoperability Testing.

This demonstrates that UCIe silicon is **no longer theoretical — it is operational in production silicon**.

## UCIe 3.0 Wins Chiplet Summit Best in Show Award

The UCIe 3.0 Specification was honored with the **Chiplet Summit Best in Show Award for Connectivity & Interoperability**.

From Chuck Sobey, General Chair, Chiplet Summit: "The UCIe Consortium represents a broad range of leading technology companies that see the importance of expanding the chiplet ecosystem. The 3.0 specification increases die-to-die data rates, power efficiency, and system-level manageability – all critical to scaling interoperable, multi-vendor chiplet architectures."

## Keynote: Debendra Das Sharma (UCIe Consortium Chairman)

- Framed industry transition from monolithic SoCs to SiP architectures as a structural shift
- Walked through UCIe evolution: 1.0 (planar) → 2.0 (UCIe-3D vertical) → 3.0 (double bandwidth density + runtime power + manageability)
- Emphasized **UCIe as a multi-decade industry standard** designed for longevity

## UCIe Tutorial: Practical Guidance

The "Applying Die-to-Die Interfaces" tutorial (organized by Brian Rea, UCIe Marketing WG Chair) featured Intel and Google providing guidance on:
- UCIe fundamentals
- Protocol and logical layer design
- Interoperability and manageability
- Security considerations
- Real-world deployment lessons across **optical chiplets, composable SoCs, and advanced multi-die architectures**

## Significance

UCIe adoption is:
- Expanding and maturing simultaneously
- Visible in production silicon (Cameron Creek demo)
- Backed by cross-vendor demonstrations (Intel + Cadence)
- Award-winning (Chiplet Summit Best in Show)
- Covering datacenter, HPC, AI, optical, automotive domains
- Growing membership breadth and diversity

UCIe is "rapidly solidifying its position as the open, interoperable foundation for chiplet-based system design, poised to support scalable, heterogeneous computing innovation into the next decade and beyond."
