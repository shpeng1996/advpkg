ST. FLORIAN, Austria, May 19, 2026 — EV Group (EVG) announced it will showcase its latest solutions for heterogeneous integration and advanced packaging at the 2026 IEEE Electronic Components and Technology Conference (ECTC), held May 26-29 in Orlando, Florida. Highlighted solutions include EVG's GEMINI FB production wafer bonding systems, the EVG40 D2W die-to-wafer overlay metrology system, the IR LayerRelease layer transfer technology platform, and EVG's LITHOSCALE XT maskless exposure system.

EVG's process solutions enable manufacturing advances across high-bandwidth memory (HBM), complementary field-effect transistors (CFETs), and other advanced device architectures, as well as 3D/chiplet integration and advanced packaging applications. EVG participated in seven technical sessions and a professional development course at ECTC 2026 focused on hybrid bonding, IR layer transfer, and maskless exposure.

Bernd Dielacher, director of business development at EV Group, stated: "As AI and high-performance computing (HPC) continue to drive demand for advanced memory, heterogeneous integration and 3D stacking, semiconductor manufacturers increasingly need scalable and proven process integration technologies and strong ecosystem partnerships to bring next-generation devices into high-volume manufacturing."

## Supporting High-Yield Hybrid Bonding for Advanced Memory and 3D Integration

Wafer-to-wafer (W2W) hybrid bonding is critical for high-yield, scalable manufacturing of next-generation 3D NAND and DRAM, requiring sub-micron alignment accuracy and excellent bond strength. Die-to-wafer (D2W) hybrid bonding is an important enabler of chiplet integration, HBM stacks, and 3D SoC integration. EVG's GEMINI FB automated production wafer bonding system is described as the de facto industry standard for hybrid and fusion wafer bonding. The EVG40 D2W provides in-line overlay metrology supporting real-time feedback loops for die placement accuracy.

## Maskless Exposure Lithography

EVG's LITHOSCALE platform combines high-resolution stitch-free patterning with digital lithography for fan-out wafer-level packaging (FOWLP), fine-pitch wafer probe cards, advanced imaging sensors, and die traceability applications. The LITHOSCALE XT system provides up to a five-fold increase in throughput versus the previous LITHOSCALE benchmark.

## IR LayerRelease for Thin-Die Stacks

EVG's IR LayerRelease technology uses an infrared laser (transparent to silicon) combined with specially formulated inorganic layers to enable laser-initiated release of ultra-thin films from silicon carriers with nanometer precision and the highest debonding throughput in the industry. This eliminates glass substrates for advanced packaging and enables novel process flows for 3D-IC and 3D sequential integration, including hybrid/fusion bonding of ultra-thin layers on silicon carriers.
