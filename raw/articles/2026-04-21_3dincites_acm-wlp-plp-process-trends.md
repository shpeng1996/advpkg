# Trends in Semiconductor Manufacturing: Wafer-Level and Panel-Level Packaging

**Author:** ACM Research  
**Published:** 2026-04-21 (3D InCites / IMAPS)

## Summary

One of the most important shifts in semiconductor manufacturing today is not just the continued growth of wafer-level packaging (WLP), but the increasing reliance on enabling, highly specialized process technologies. As device architectures evolve toward heterogeneous integration (HI) and chiplet-based designs, key technologies — wet processing, electrochemical plating (ECP), plasma-enhanced chemical vapor deposition (PECVD), and emerging panel-level packaging (PLP) — have become central to achieving required levels of precision, performance, and scalability.

## Technology Trend Drivers

Demand for AI, 5G, HPC, and advanced consumer electronics is accelerating adoption of WLP and PLP while pushing process technologies to new limits:
- Tighter line/space geometries and higher interconnect densities require advanced wet processing, plating, and deposition systems
- Sustainability is now a parallel driver: reducing chemical consumption, minimizing waste, improving process efficiency
- Closed-loop systems, chemical recycling, and process optimization are becoming mandatory for HVM

## WLP as the Foundation

WLP remains a cornerstone of advanced packaging, encompassing:
- Fan-in and fan-out (FOWLP/FOPLP)
- 2D, 2.5D, and 3D architectures (CoWoS, SoIC, EMIB, Foveros)

Critical enabling processes:
- **High-aspect-ratio TSVs** — require tightly controlled ECP for uniform metal fill
- **Ultra-fine RDLs** — depend on PECVD for conformal dielectric deposition/passivation
- **Advanced interconnect schemes** — require wet processing for contamination control and surface readiness

## Panel-Level Packaging (PLP) Emergence

PLP is gaining traction as a complementary path to scale, but introduces new challenges:
- **Uniformity** across larger substrates (300×300mm to 510×515mm panels)
- **Materials handling** — glass vs. organic panels, different thermal coefficients
- **Process integration** — front-end-like rigor required at back-end scale

Key observation: Historically, OSAT-specific packaging tools tolerated more forgiving process tolerances. As advanced packaging evolves to include finer RDL line/space, high-aspect-ratio TSVs, and hybrid bonding, **front-end process rigor is migrating into back-end/OSAT manufacturing**.

## Process Technology Classification (ACM Research Framework)

| Process | Role in Advanced Packaging |
|---------|---------------------------|
| **ECP (Electrochemical Plating)** | Cu bump formation, RDL metal fill, TSV fill |
| **PECVD** | Conformal dielectric deposition, passivation layers |
| **Wet Processing** | Surface prep, coating, developing, stripping, etching |
| **Hybrid Bonding prep** | Surface planarization, Cu CMP, contamination control |

## Key Insight: The Equipment Supply Chain Shift

Prior to advanced packaging era: OSAT-specific tools developed by niche suppliers (packaging-specific ECP, wet tools).

Post-2024: Requirements have exceeded what OSAT-specific tools can deliver. **Front-end equipment suppliers (ACM Research, Lam Research, Applied Materials, Tokyo Electron) are increasingly entering advanced packaging process tool market** — bringing front-end precision and process control to back-end manufacturing.

This is driving the **equipment supply chain realignment** visible in: AMAT acquiring NEXX (ECD panel tools), TEL investing in Kumamoto R&D (RDL), ACM Research expanding into advanced packaging (Cu plating, surface prep).

## Sustainability Requirements

For HVM viability:
- Chemical recycling and closed-loop systems for wet baths
- Reduced DI water consumption
- Process optimization to minimize chemical drag-out
- Environmental compliance alongside yield optimization
