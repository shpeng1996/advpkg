# NVIDIA Rubin Ultra and Feynman Reportedly to Boost TSMC SoIC; Besi, Applied Materials, TEL to Benefit

**Source**: TrendForce
**Date**: March 18, 2026

## Summary

NVIDIA's next-generation AI GPU architectures—Rubin Ultra (2026–2027) and Feynman (2028+)—are both reportedly slated to leverage **TSMC SoIC** (System on Integrated Chips) 3D packaging technology. This is expected to significantly boost SoIC demand and benefit the key equipment suppliers in the hybrid bonding supply chain: **Besi**, **Applied Materials (AMAT)**, and **Tokyo Electron (TEL)**.

## Key Details

### NVIDIA's SoIC Roadmap
- **Rubin Ultra**: will use SoIC for 3D stacking of compute dies, alongside CoWoS-L for 2.5D HBM integration
- **Feynman GPU** (2028+): expected to use advanced SoIC-X (hybrid bonding) with sub-micron pitch
- Combined demand from NVIDIA and Apple (M-series) is the primary driver of TSMC SoIC capacity ramp

### TSMC SoIC Capacity Ramp
- TSMC targets **10,000–15,000 SoIC wafers/month** by end of 2026
- SoIC-X uses Cu-Cu hybrid bonding at 6µm pitch (2026 Q1 production)
- Future SoIC-W (wafer-to-wafer) targets sub-micron pitch by 2028

### Equipment Supply Chain Beneficiaries
Three companies are positioned to benefit most from the SoIC/hybrid bonding ramp:

| Company | Role | Key Product |
|---------|------|-------------|
| **Besi** | D2W hybrid bonding bonder | Datacon 8800 CHAMEO; Kinex platform |
| **Applied Materials (AMAT)** | CMP, dielectric deposition, bonding prep | Owns 9% stake in Besi (Kinex) |
| **Tokyo Electron (TEL)** | RDL deposition, cleaning, wet process | Kumamoto R&D hub (¥47B investment) |

### Market Implications
- Hybrid bonding equipment market expected to grow at **21% CAGR (2025–2030)** per Yole Group
- Besi's D2W bonder (Datacon 8800 CHAMEO) is production-qualified for TSMC SoIC
- AMAT's 9% stake in Besi's Kinex platform aligns its revenue with SoIC volume growth
- TEL positioned to capture RDL and wet process steps in advanced packaging

### Feynman GPU Implications
- NVIDIA's Feynman (2028+) reportedly targets TSMC A14 process with SoIC-X integration
- This aligns with TSMC's 2026 Tech Symposium announcement of A14 SoIC process readiness
