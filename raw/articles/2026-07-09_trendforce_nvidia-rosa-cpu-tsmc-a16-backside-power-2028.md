NVIDIA is expanding its AI server platform strategy beyond GPUs to CPUs, with its next-generation Rosa CPU emerging as a key focus. According to Commercial Times, Rosa may use TSMC's 2nm-class process and could even adopt the company's **A16 process featuring back-side power delivery**. The Rosa CPU and Feynman GPU data center platform is scheduled to launch in **2028**. The Rosa Feynman Spark platform for consumer PCs is planned to follow in 2030.

## Rosa May Adopt TSMC's A16 Process

TSMC's A16 offers up to 1.10× higher chip density than N2P. Its biggest technological breakthrough is the introduction of **Super Power Rail (SPR)**, a back-side power delivery technology that moves the power network to the back of the wafer. This improves:
- Power delivery efficiency
- Reduces IR drop
- Frees up front-side routing space for signal interconnects

If Rosa adopts A16, it would extend back-side power delivery beyond GPUs and ASICs to CPUs, with broad supply chain implications:
- CMP process steps at 2nm: already >2x versus 7nm
- A16 SPR adds another **15%–20% CMP demand** vs conventional 2nm
- **Carrier wafer ASPs: >4x higher than reclaimed wafers** (driven by SPR process)

## Rosa CPU Specifications and Architecture

- **Rigel cores** based on Arm v9.2 ISA (successor to Olympus/Vera CPU)
- Expected **128+ Rigel cores**
- IPC improvement: potentially **>50% vs Vera** (current generation)
- Improvements: larger L2 cache, better instruction delivery, more efficient memory management

## Platform Roadmap

| Rosa + Feynman | TSMC A16 (potential) | 2028 | AI datacenter |

## First Vera CPU Customers (confirmed)

Perplexity, OpenAI, Anthropic, Oracle — all confirmed users of NVIDIA Vera CPU platform. Expanding Vera CPU adoption will increase NVIDIA's wafer orders with TSMC, further supporting demand for advanced process capacity and CoWoS.
