# HBF Spurs Equipment Race; Hanmi Semiconductor Eyes First TC Bonder Deliveries in 2H26

**Published**: 2026-06-05 | **Source**: TrendForce (citing ZDNet Korea, The Bell)

---

## Overview

**High Bandwidth Flash (HBF)** is a next-generation memory technology designed for **AI inference**, using NAND flash stacked similarly to HBM (using TC bonding). Back-end semiconductor equipment suppliers are increasingly focusing on HBF as a new market opportunity.

## Equipment Race

Korean and international back-end equipment makers have begun developing **TC bonders for HBF manufacturing**:
- **Hanmi Semiconductor**: Leading the field; preparing to deliver **first batch of HBF TC bonders to customers in 2H 2026**
- **Hanwha Semitech**: Seeking position in emerging supply chain
- **ASMPT**: Working to establish foothold
- **Kulicke & Soffa (K&S)**: Working to establish foothold

Key technical note: TC bonder core architecture for HBM and HBF is **largely the same** — existing platforms can likely be adapted through customization. Bonding pitch requirements for HBF expected to be **less stringent than HBM**.

Critical challenge: **NAND flash is more susceptible to heat and pressure than DRAM** → manufacturers must minimize cracking during TC bonding.

## SanDisk's Commercialization Timeline

**SanDisk** is leading HBF standardization and development:
- **2H 2026**: First HBF samples to customers
- **Early 2027**: AI chip-integrated samples
- First-generation product: **16-layer NAND stack**

**SK hynix** is working with SanDisk on standardization and technology development.

SanDisk is also collaborating with South Korean equipment makers on HBF sample development. A supplier with monopoly position in HBM equipment was planning to provide sample equipment to SanDisk around early summer 2026.

## Market Uncertainty

Ultimate market potential of HBF remains uncertain. TC bonder suppliers could be among the **first beneficiaries** if HBF reaches commercial production. HBF standards have **not yet been finalized**.
