# Hybrid Bonding Process Flow - Advanced Packaging Part 5

SemiAnalysis frames hybrid bonding as a major manufacturing and design shift: moving from solder-bump interconnects toward direct copper-to-copper and dielectric-to-dielectric bonds for 3D stacking.

## Publicly Accessible Extracted Points

- Hybrid bonding is bumpless: die or wafers connect through copper pads in a dielectric layer, without solder or adhesive between bond interfaces.
- It enables sub-10-micron pitch and a roadmap into the hundreds-of-nanometers regime.
- Key process requirements are surface smoothness, cleanliness, and alignment accuracy.
- Particle control is central: small particles can create large bond voids, making front-end-class cleanrooms necessary.
- W2W bonding separates alignment from bonding, can achieve sub-50 nm alignment, and is mature in image sensors and NAND.
- D2W bonding is more difficult but supports known-good-die selection, making it better suited for large or lower-yield logic dies.
- Typical hybrid-bonding flows include TSV formation, hybrid-bond-layer formation, CMP, plasma activation, cleaning, pre-bonding, and annealing.
