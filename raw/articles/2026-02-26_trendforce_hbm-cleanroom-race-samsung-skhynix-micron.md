# Memory Giants' HBM Cleanroom Race: Samsung & SK hynix Fast-Track, Micron Acquires

**Source**: TrendForce
**Date**: February 26, 2026

## Summary

The three major HBM suppliers—Samsung, SK Hynix, and Micron—are engaged in an aggressive cleanroom capacity expansion race to meet surging AI demand for HBM4 and beyond. Samsung and SK Hynix are fast-tracking facility expansions, while Micron is pursuing an acquisition strategy to secure manufacturing footprint.

## Key Details

### Samsung HBM Cleanroom Expansion
- Samsung is fast-tracking cleanroom construction at its P3 fab (Pyeongtaek) dedicated to HBM4
- Target: reach **250,000 wafers/month** HBM capacity by end of 2026 (50% increase from 2025)
- Samsung's HBM4 technology uses **12-layer stacking** initially, with 16-layer H2 2026 target
- Samsung seeks to grow HBM market share from ~20% (2025) to 30–35% (2026)

### SK Hynix Cleanroom Expansion
- SK Hynix has been fast-tracking the **M15X fab** (Cheongju), which came online ahead of schedule in February 2026
- M15X is dedicated to HBM production, targeting 12-layer HBM4
- SK Hynix maintains ~50% bit-output share and ~70% of NVIDIA's HBM supply allocation
- HBM3E remains mainstream product through 2026 H1; HBM4 ramp accelerates in H2

### Micron Acquisition Strategy
- Micron is pursuing acquisitions to expand HBM packaging and advanced memory manufacturing capacity
- Unlike Samsung/SK Hynix which build new fabs, Micron is acquiring existing facilities to accelerate timeline
- Micron reportedly achieved **>11 Gbps** HBM4 data rate in qualification (above JEDEC JESD270-4 spec)
- Micron targets meaningful HBM4 market share by 2H 2026

### Industry-Wide HBM Capacity Growth
- Total HBM industry capacity projected to grow **80–100%** from 2024 to end of 2026
- Driver: NVIDIA Rubin GPU HBM4 demand + AMD MI450 memory requirements
- HBM4E development running in parallel with HBM4 ramp (target 2027 market entry at 40% share)

### TSV and Packaging Implications
- HBM manufacturing requires TSV (Through-Silicon Via) etching and Cu-Cu bonding for die stacking
- The cleanroom race is simultaneously a TSV and advanced packaging capacity race
- Tighter pitch (HBM4: 55µm TSV pitch) requires more precise cleanroom environment
