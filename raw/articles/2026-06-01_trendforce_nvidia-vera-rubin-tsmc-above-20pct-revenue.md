## Summary

As NVIDIA's GTC Taipei kicked off, market attention turned to the rollout of its next-generation AI platform Vera Rubin, expected to enter mass production and begin shipments in the second half of 2026.

## NVIDIA Overtakes TSMC Revenue Mix

- After becoming TSMC's largest customer in 2025, NVIDIA is expected to account for over 20% of TSMC's revenue in 2026.
- TSMC 2025 annual report data: Customer A (NVIDIA) revenue share surged from 12% (2024) to 19% (2025), overtaking Customer B (Apple, which fell from 22% to 17%).
- NVIDIA CEO Jensen Huang confirmed NVIDIA is TSMC's largest customer during a GSA podcast with Jodi Shelton.
- TSMC began volume production of NVIDIA's Vera Rubin chips on its 3nm node earlier in 2026.
- Major OEMs (Foxconn, Quanta, Wistron) ramping Vera Rubin deployments 2H26; mass shipments projected to start Q3 2026.
- Vera Rubin first shipments reportedly rolling out in July to AI customers (per Wccftech/Economic Daily News).

## Higher Rack Value Drives Power & Cooling Gains

- Vera Rubin rack unit price estimated at ~US$7.8 million per rack — substantially higher than GB300.
- Power modules: ~32% increase in value content vs prior generation.
- Cooling system content: ~12% projected rise.
- Vera Rubin platform adopts 110kW power architecture.
- CSPs evaluating HVDC solutions and shifting toward 800VDC systems.
- Liquid-cooling coverage expands beyond GPU compute modules to include networking switches.
- Beneficiary Taiwanese suppliers: Delta Electronics, Lite-On Technology, Auras Technology, Asia Vital Components.

## Context: CoWoS and Advanced Packaging Centrality

- Vera Rubin's 3nm production at TSMC with CoWoS advanced packaging remains the critical bottleneck.
- Jensen Huang stated Taiwan will be "very busy" in 2H26; 2027 described as "beyond imagination" in terms of workload.
- TSMC and ASE named as key manufacturing partners for Vera Rubin platform ramp.
