# STMicroelectronics Joins Panel-Level Packaging Race with New French Pilot Line, Launching 3Q 2026

**Date:** September 19, 2025 | **Source:** TrendForce

## Summary

As leading foundries and packaging giants such as TSMC and ASE ramp up panel-level packaging (PLP) capacity, STMicroelectronics has emerged as an unexpected new entrant. STMicroelectronics is launching a panel-level packaging pilot line at its facility in France, with the launch targeted for Q3 2026.

## Key Facts

- **Company:** STMicroelectronics (ST) — European IDM, headquarters Geneva/Catania
- **Action:** Building a new panel-level packaging (PLP) pilot line in France
- **Timeline:** Launch targeted for Q3 2026
- **Significance:** ST joins TSMC (CoPoS), Samsung (PLP), ASE, and Powertech as a PLP participant — notable as an IDM entering a space previously dominated by foundries and OSATs

## Context: IDM Strategy

STMicroelectronics has historically been a vertically integrated manufacturer (IDM) handling its own packaging in-house. Entering the panel-level packaging race represents:
1. **Vertical integration extension** — ST develops advanced packaging capability in-house rather than relying on external OSATs
2. **European industrial policy alignment** — France is a priority location for semiconductor strategic autonomy under EU Chips Act
3. **Market positioning** — Panel-level packaging enables larger chip sizes at lower cost-per-die, relevant for ST's automotive and industrial power device markets

## Competitive Landscape Context

At the time of this article (September 2025), the panel-level packaging competitive landscape included:
- **TSMC:** CoPoS pilot line (310×310mm glass substrate, AP7 campus); June 2026 completion target
- **ASE:** Panel-level fan-out (PLFO) programs gaining traction
- **Powertech:** FOPLP trial yields reaching ~90%
- **Samsung:** PLP development programs in Korea
- **STMicroelectronics:** French pilot line — NEW entrant (this article)
