# Where co-packaged optics (CPO) technology stands in 2026

Co-packaged optics (CPO) integrates optical components directly into a single package, minimizing electrical path length, reducing signal loss, enhancing signal integrity, and containing latency. It improves power efficiency by reducing the need for high-power electrical drivers, repeaters, and retimers — shortening the copper trace enough to potentially remove DSP/retimer functionality and significantly reduce power per bit.

**2025 progress recap:**

- January 2025: Marvell showcased a custom XPU architecture combining XPU compute silicon, HBM, and other chiplets with 3D SiPho (silicon photonics) engines on the same substrate, eliminating the need for electrical signals to leave the package into copper cables or across a PCB. Marvell's 3D SiPho engine supports 200 Gbps electrical and optical interfaces; connections between XPUs can achieve data rates and distances 100x longer than electrical cabling.
- May 2025: Broadcom unveiled its third-generation 200G-per-lane CPO technology. Broadcom's CPO journey started in 2021 with Tomahawk 4-Humboldt; the second-generation Tomahawk 5-Bailly became the industry's first volume-production CPO solution.
- October 2025: Broadcom claimed Meta tested its CPO solutions for one million link hours without a single link flap in high-temperature lab characterization.
- November 2025: Ayar Labs announced integration of its TeraPHY optical engines into Global Unichip Corp's (GUC, Hsinchu, Taiwan) advanced packaging and ASIC design workflow — a step toward future CPO deployment, addressing architectural, power/signal-integrity, mechanical, and thermal integration challenges.

**Outlook for 2026 and beyond:**

Industry watchers (Yole Group) see large-scale CPO deployment between 2028–2030; pluggable optical modules will remain competitive in the meantime. LightCounting predicts pluggable modules will continue to account for the majority of optical links in data centers throughout the decade, even as transceiver tech gradually shifts optics closer to the ASIC.

Seamless attachment of optical engines to switch ASICs/XPUs requires packaging approaches including 2.5D interposers, TSVs, fan-out wafer-level packaging, and 3D integration enabled by hybrid bonding. IDTechEx projects the CPO market will exceed $20 billion by 2036, growing at a 37% CAGR from 2026–2036.
