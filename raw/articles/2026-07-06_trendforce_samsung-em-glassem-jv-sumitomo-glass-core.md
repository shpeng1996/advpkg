While Samsung Electro-Mechanics already operates a glass substrate pilot line at its Sejong plant, the company has taken another key step toward scaling up production. It has signed a definitive agreement with Dongwoo Fine-Chem, a subsidiary of Japan's Sumitomo Chemical Group, to establish a joint venture producing "glass core" for next-generation substrates.

## Joint Venture Details — "GlaSSEM"

- **JV Name**: GlaSSEM (tentative)
- **Partners**: Samsung Electro-Mechanics (66%) + Dongwoo Fine-Chem / Sumitomo Chemical Group (34%)
- **Total Capital**: approximately KRW 480 billion
- **Timeline**: formal establishment within 2026; full-scale operations 2H27
- **Location**: Dongwoo Fine-Chem's Pyeongtaek plant, Gyeonggi Province, South Korea
- **Rationale**: combines Samsung EM semiconductor substrate design capabilities with Sumitomo Chemical Group materials technology and Dongwoo Fine-Chem manufacturing infrastructure

## TSMC Glass Substrate Test Data (JPCA Show 2026)

Separately, TSMC presented its "glass substrate for CoWoS" roadmap to supply chain partners at JPCA Show 2026 (Japan), confirming collaboration with Japan's Ibiden and Taiwan-based Innolux on glass core substrate development.

TSMC's first disclosed test data shows:
- **Coplanarity (COP)**: +16% improvement
- **Effective CTE**: −19% reduction
- **Effective modulus**: +31% increase
- **Power integrity — resistance**: −27%
- **Power integrity — inductance**: −42%

These improvements translate into significantly reduced thermal deformation and mechanical stress in large AI chips.

## Industry Context

Despite these advances, analysts note key manufacturing hurdles remain:
- Through-glass via (TGV) technology has yet to be fully stabilized
- 2026 is a key validation year for equipment/materials suppliers
- TSMC CoPoS pilot production targeted for 2027; mass production 2H28
- TSMC's next push beyond CoPoS: glass core substrates, commercial-scale adoption likely after 2030

Samsung Electro-Mechanics Sejong plant already hosts a glass substrate pilot line (previously reported: AI substrate facility with KRW 8T electro-mechanics investment as part of Samsung's KRW 140T plan).
