# SK hynix Reportedly Tests Intel EMIB 2.5D Packaging With HBM Amid TSMC CoWoS Tightness

**Published**: 2026-05-11 | **Source**: TrendForce

SK hynix is reportedly exploring cooperation with Intel in advanced 2.5D packaging, a move that could signal potential shifts in the AI chip packaging supply chain. According to ZDNet Korea, sources say SK hynix is conducting R&D on Intel's EMIB-based 2.5D packaging technology using its own HBM, while also reviewing related materials and component suppliers for future mass production. The company is reportedly testing the integration of HBM and logic semiconductors using EMIB-integrated substrates supplied by Intel.

Currently, the global 2.5D packaging supply chain is dominated by TSMC, with SK hynix maintaining a close partnership with TSMC on HBM and 2.5D packaging R&D. TSMC's CoWoS (Chip-on-Wafer-on-Substrate) technology has been facing severe supply constraints amid surging AI semiconductor demand, prompting several major tech firms to view Intel's EMIB as a potential alternative.

From SK hynix's perspective, early R&D on Intel EMIB is seen as increasingly important. Although the company does not directly mass produce 2.5D packaging, developing HBM with consideration for 2.5D packaging structures and characteristics could help improve yields and stability. SK hynix is currently operating a small-scale domestic line dedicated to 2.5D packaging R&D.

## Intel Ramps EMIB Capacity as AI Chipmakers Explore Alternatives

Intel is moving to expand its EMIB packaging ecosystem amid rising interest in CoWoS alternatives. The company has begun placing full-scale equipment orders related to EMIB production capacity expansion, a move that industry observers believe may indicate the company has secured major clients.

Google and Meta are being cited as major clients for Intel Foundry's EMIB-based packaging:
- **Google**: Confirmed EMIB adoption for next-generation Tensor Processing Unit (TPU) v8e AI chip, targeted for launch in the second half of 2027
- **Meta**: Considering EMIB for certain next-generation products within its MTIA AI training and inference accelerator lineup
- **Marvell** and **MediaTek**: Reportedly reviewing potential EMIB adoption

## EMIB vs. CoWoS: Technical Trade-offs

By embedding small silicon bridges directly into the substrate for die-to-die connections, EMIB eliminates the need for a large and costly interposer, simplifying the structure and improving manufacturing yields. The design also reduces thermal-expansion mismatches from different CTE materials, lowering warpage risks and improving long-term reliability.

However, EMIB also has inherent limitations. Silicon bridge's smaller area and routing density can constrain bandwidth, increase transmission distance, and result in slightly higher latency than CoWoS. As a result, EMIB is seen as more attractive to ASIC customers than GPU vendors, which require extremely high bandwidth and ultra-low latency.
