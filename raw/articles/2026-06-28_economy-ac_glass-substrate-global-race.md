# AI Packaging Game-Changer 'Glass Substrates' Ignite Global Race Toward Mass Production

Glass-core substrates — positioned as a next-generation alternative to organic substrates for advanced AI chip packaging — are reportedly the focus of an intensifying global race between Chinese and Japanese supply chains, each pursuing different timelines toward mass production.

## China

- Chinese display and substrate makers including BOE, Visionox, AKM Meadville, and Yuntian Semiconductor are reported to be moving aggressively into glass substrate development, in part leveraging existing display-panel manufacturing know-how.

## Japan

- Japanese materials and equipment suppliers Ibiden and Dai Nippon Printing (DNP) are reported to be targeting mass production of glass-core substrates around 2028.
- AGC and Shibuya Corporation are named as suppliers of TGV (through-glass via) processing equipment supporting this roadmap.

## Technical challenges

- TGV (through-glass via) reliability — specifically microcracking and yield loss during via formation and subsequent thermal cycling — is cited as the primary technical bottleneck delaying mass production timelines.

## Patent landscape

- The article notes that the existing patent landscape around glass-substrate and TGV technology is concentrated among Intel, Corning, and Schott, which may pose IP/licensing considerations for new entrants in China and elsewhere.
