SK hynix Inc. has decided to purchase mass-production hybrid bonding equipment for the development of next-generation high-bandwidth memory (HBM), according to industry sources on March 31, 2026.

The company placed an order in February for one hybrid bonding inline system jointly developed by U.S.-based Applied Materials and Netherlands-based BE Semiconductor Industries (Besi), known as the "Kinex" system. The system is estimated to cost about 20 billion won. It integrates Applied Materials' chemical mechanical polishing (CMP) and plasma processing equipment with Besi's hybrid die bonder, and is expected to be installed in a development line in the near term. This marks SK hynix's first purchase of hybrid bonding equipment designed for mass production.

An industry official said that while hybrid bonding is likely to be adopted in HBM from the next-next generation onward, the current order — though for mass-production equipment — is intended as a preparatory step for future development.

Hybrid bonding involves directly bonding copper (Cu) surfaces by placing processed chip dies onto wafers. Prior to bonding, CMP is used for planarization, followed by plasma-based post-treatment in a vacuum environment. Direct Cu-to-Cu bonding enables higher integration density, shorter interconnect lengths, improved device performance and reduced power consumption.

Besi is widely regarded as a global leader in hybrid bonding equipment, particularly die bonding systems, with technological advantages in both precision and throughput.

The jointly developed Applied Materials-Besi system is already in mass production use at TSMC, where it has been used to commercialize AMD's 3D V-Cache technology (vertically stacking high-speed cache memory atop processors). The system is also expected to be used for custom AI ASICs for Broadcom.

SK hynix has not disclosed how it plans to apply hybrid bonding to HBM. According to industry sources, the most likely approach is die-to-wafer (D2W) bonding, where the first DRAM core die is placed on a base die wafer, followed by die-to-die (D2D) stacking of additional DRAM dies. Another method under consideration involves bonding two DRAM dies at the wafer level first, then stacking these paired units — potentially halving the stacking steps required for a 16-layer structure. No final method has been determined; multiple approaches will be tested to find an optimal solution balancing yield and performance.

In addition to the Applied Materials-Besi system, SK hynix plans to introduce hybrid bonding equipment developed by Hanwha Semitech for quality testing in the near future.

Samsung Electronics has also reportedly adopted Besi equipment for development purposes and recently introduced hybrid bonders from SEMES for quality testing, although the SEMES equipment is said to be less mature.
