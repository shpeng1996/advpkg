## Summary

Intel has entered the final stage of its Project Pelican advanced packaging complex in Malaysia, expected to be operational in 2026. Intel also unveiled EMIB-T — an enhanced EMIB design incorporating TSV technology — targeting HBM4 support.

## Key Details

- **Malaysia Complex (Project Pelican)**:
  - Status: Final construction stage as of March 2026, operational target: 2026
  - Part of Intel's strategy to build advanced packaging capacity outside Taiwan
  - Ties into US domestic supply chain via Amkor partnership (Korea, Portugal, Arizona facilities)

- **EMIB-T (Enhanced EMIB)**:
  - New variant of EMIB integrating through-silicon via (TSV) technology
  - Designed to support HBM4 integration
  - Enables more flexible chiplet configurations than original EMIB

- **Amkor Partnership (announced Dec 2025 / IFTLE 651)**:
  - Amkor will implement EMIB assembly processes at Korea, Portugal, and upcoming Arizona facilities
  - Creates alternative supply source for EMIB packaging
  - Strengthens US domestic advanced packaging supply chain

- **Customer Traction**:
  - Apple and Qualcomm reportedly seeking EMIB expertise (job postings, Nov 2025)
  - Intel EMIB gaining traction with AI ASIC and smartphone clients
  - Could potentially package TSMC-manufactured dies (foundry-agnostic approach)

- **Foveros Status**:
  - Foveros Direct 3D stacking (bump-less Cu-to-Cu bonding) continues development
  - Supporting next-gen compute chiplet stacking

## Strategic Significance

Intel is transforming its advanced packaging from an internal captive capability into a foundry service for external customers, directly competing with TSMC's CoWoS and Samsung's packaging services.
