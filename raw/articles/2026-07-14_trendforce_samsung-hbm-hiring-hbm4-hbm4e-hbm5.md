Samsung is accelerating its HBM roadmap with a broad talent push. Samsung Electronics is recruiting across the entire HBM development process, from design and packaging to reliability evaluation and customer technical support. Following the successful mass production of sixth-generation HBM4 and customer sampling of seventh-generation HBM4E this year, as well as the unveiling of its eighth-generation HBM5 technology, the company is strengthening its talent pool to accelerate both product development and customer qualification.

Samsung will recruit experienced professionals across six HBM-related roles from July 13 to July 27:
1. HBM core die design (Memory Business)
2. HBM base die design (Memory Business)
3. HBM reliability evaluation (Memory Business)
4. HBM package development (Memory Business)
5. HBM application engineering (Memory Business)
6. Next-generation HBM packaging process development (Semiconductor Research Center)

Industry sources view Samsung's simultaneous recruitment across every stage of HBM development as part of its strategy to expand HBM4 mass production while advancing HBM4E and HBM5 development in parallel.

## How Samsung's HBM Hiring Supports Its Roadmap

- Core die design engineers: improve speed and power efficiency of next-gen DRAM for HBM4E and HBM5
- Base die design engineers: strengthen Samsung's custom HBM capabilities
- Packaging and reliability engineers: address increasing stack heights and thermal challenges of HBM4E and HBM5
- Packaging process development: covers multi-stack hybrid copper bonding, TSV module development, and HBM copper pad planarization
- Samsung plans to advance hybrid bonding together with its Heat Path Block (HPB) technology to reduce package thickness and thermal resistance in high-stack HBM products
- Application engineers: shorten customer qualification cycles

## Broader Semiconductor Hiring Strategy

Samsung Electronics is hiring across 82 job categories within its Device Solutions (DS) division. Notably, none of the listed positions are related to next-generation power semiconductor R&D, which some analysts interpret as Samsung reducing focus on the power semiconductor business.

SK hynix also posted recruitment for experienced professionals on June 24, including HBM Foundry Process Integration and HBM Digital Design positions. This suggests SK hynix is strengthening foundry and system design capabilities as HBM increasingly relies on capabilities beyond memory technology — advanced packaging, logic design, and advanced processes are becoming more important to HBM competitiveness.
