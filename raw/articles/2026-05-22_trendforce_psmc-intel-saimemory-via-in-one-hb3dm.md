# PSMC Joins Intel, SAIMEMORY to Demo 9-Layer Via-in-One Architecture for High Bandwidth 3D Memory

**Published:** 2026-05-22 | **Source:** TrendForce

As Intel's collaboration with SoftBank subsidiary SAIMEMORY on Z-Angle Memory (ZAM) continues to draw intense industry attention, new details are emerging ahead of the VLSI Symposium 2026 scheduled for June.

Taiwan's Powerchip Semiconductor Manufacturing Corp. (PSMC) has entered the picture, set to co-present alongside Intel and SAIMEMORY at VLSI 2026, jointly unveiling the "Via-in-One TSV" architecture.

## Technical Architecture: Via-in-One TSV

The architecture delivers:
- **Memory bandwidth:** ~0.25 Tb/s/mm²
- **Data transfer power:** <0.35 W/mm²
- **Energy per bit:** <0.7 pJ/bit
- **Stacked layers:** 9-layer DRAM stack (custom DRAM wafers)
- **Silicon substrate thickness:** ~3 µm (ultra-thin, lowers TSV resistance)
- **TSV dimensions:** 10 × 85 µm² per trench, 20 µm pitch (~13.7K TSVs per layer)
- **Contact resistance:** O-type design, 40% lower than C-type

Based on a 171 mm² die area for a 10 GB module, the architecture was estimated to reach ~5.3 TB/s per module—more than double HBM4's ~2 TB/s per stack.

## Fusion-Bonded Wafer Integration

Intel, SAIMEMORY, and PSMC are introducing a multi-wafer via-last process to enable **fusion-bonded wafer integration**, centered on the 'via-in-one TSV' structure demonstrated in stacked custom DRAM wafers.

Functional validation of the full nine-layer DRAM stack (operating 0.95V–1.2V) and successful reliability testing have been completed.

## Partnership Structure

| Role | Company |
|------|---------|
| Design & IP management | SAIMEMORY (SoftBank subsidiary) |
| 3D stacking & memory architecture | Intel |
| Pilot production & manufacturing | PSMC + Shinko Electric Industries (Japan) |

**Timeline:**
- Prototype completion: 2027
- Commercial mass production: 2029

## Competitive Context vs HBM

ZAM-based HB3DM is positioned as a potential HBM alternative:
- HBM4: ~2 TB/s per stack
- HB3DM (ZAM): ~5.3 TB/s per module (2.6× HBM4)
- Intel plans to unveil ZAM-based HB3DM formally at VLSI Symposium 2026 (June)

The architecture uses a Z-Angle Memory design and via-last TSV process, distinguishing it from HBM's CoW (Chip-on-Wafer) approach with micro-bumps.
