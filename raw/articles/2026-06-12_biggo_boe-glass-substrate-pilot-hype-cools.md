# Glass Substrate Packaging Hype Cools: BOE's Pilot Line Operational but Not Yet in Mass Production

BOE Technology disclosed on June 12, 2026 that its glass-substrate packaging carrier pilot line has achieved fully automated operation with a designed capacity of 1,000 units/month, but has not yet reached mass-production yield. Market estimates suggest BOE's current actual monthly capacity is around 300 wafers, expected to expand toward the 1,000/month design target by end-2026, with a gradual production ramp beginning in 2027.

2026 is broadly viewed across the industry as the inaugural commercialization year for glass substrate packaging. China's A-share-listed glass-substrate-adjacent supply chain (panel makers, equipment, and materials suppliers) surged roughly 74% between April and early June 2026 before pulling back as the market reassessed near-term mass-production timing.

The core technical barrier remains TGV (Through-Glass-Via) processing: yield-rate improvement and large-panel warpage control are cited as the key obstacles standing between pilot-line operation and true mass production. China's domestic glass-substrate supply chain — spanning panel leaders down to equipment and materials vendors — has laid out roadmaps but remains largely in R&D/verification phase; several companies in the chain are still operating at a loss, underscoring that near-term revenue contribution is limited even as strategic positioning accelerates.

This tempers (without reversing) the narrative of China rapidly closing the glass-substrate gap with South Korean and Taiwanese players — pilot-line progress is real, but the yield/warpage bottleneck means mass production at scale is still roughly 12-18 months out per current estimates.
