# Applied Sees >30% Equipment Growth, >50% Packaging Surge in 2026; China Remains Top Market in 2Q

**Published:** 2026-05-15 | **Source:** TrendForce

Riding sustained AI-driven demand across both logic and memory, Applied Materials has struck a more bullish tone for 2026, projecting over 30% growth in its semiconductor equipment business and more than **50% growth in packaging revenue**.

The outlook marks a notable upgrade from the company's February earnings call, when Applied had guided for semiconductor equipment business growth of more than 20% for the calendar year.

## Growth Drivers

Surging AI infrastructure spending from hyperscalers is prompting chipmakers such as TSMC and Samsung Electronics to expand capacity, further boosting demand for Applied Materials' chipmaking and packaging tools. CEO Gary Dickerson emphasized Applied's leading position in memory equipment, underpinned by strength in DRAM wiring, patterning, and peripheral logic steps.

Applied Materials also recently entered an agreement with ASMPT to acquire its NEXX business (US$120M), a leading supplier of large-area advanced packaging deposition equipment for wafer-level packaging, 2.5D/3D ICs, and fan-out technologies.

## China and MATCH Act Risk

The proposed MATCH Act would designate SMIC, Huawei, Hua Hong, and China's leading memory makers as "covered facilities," banning DUV immersion lithography exports and restricting allied firms from servicing installed tools. Despite the policy headwinds, China remained one of Applied Materials' largest markets (27% of 2QFY26 sales).

Applied Materials expects approximately $710 million revenue impact from U.S. export restrictions.

CFO Brice Hill said the company expects both its China business and global ICAPS segment to be flat to slightly higher over the calendar year. CEO Gary Dickerson stated all related risks have already been factored into quarterly guidance and full-year outlook.
