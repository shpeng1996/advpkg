# SK Hynix Dethroned Samsung After 26 Years: Now Choosing DDR5 Profits Over HBM4 Ramp

On June 22, 2026, SK Hynix briefly overtook Samsung Electronics as South Korea's most valuable company by common-share market cap (2,080 trillion won / $1.35T vs Samsung's 2,067 trillion won) — ending a 26-year Samsung reign — driven by SK Hynix shares rising 340% YTD on HBM dominance. The lead reversed the next day (June 23) after a nearly 10% KOSPI crash (largest point drop on record, circuit breakers triggered twice) tied to overheated leveraged semiconductor ETFs; both stocks fell >12% and Samsung's total cap (including preferred shares, ~2,246 trillion won) regained the lead.

## Key Claims

1. SK Hynix is deliberately delaying conversion of some HBM3E lines scheduled for HBM4 upgrade, retooling that capacity for DDR5 production instead — choosing ~90% DDR5 operating margins over HBM4 ramp speed.
2. HBM production consumes ~3x the wafer capacity per GB versus DDR5, due to TSV stacking, lower yields, and CoWoS packaging bottlenecks — this architectural fact is the root cause of the DDR5 shortage (DDR5 contract prices rose 90–95% QoQ in Q1 2026, 58–63% further in Q2).
3. SK Hynix Q1 2026 results: revenue 52.58T won (+198% YoY), operating profit 37.61T won (+405% YoY), operating margin 72% — a new all-time semiconductor-industry record, reportedly exceeding Nvidia's 65% margin same period. HBM is >40% of SK Hynix revenue; it holds ~57–62% global HBM share and ~60-70% of Nvidia Rubin-platform HBM4 volume (Goldman Sachs estimate).
4. Samsung began HBM4 mass production Feb 12, 2026 (first to do so), using a 4nm FinFET base die; cumulative HBM4 revenue crossed $1B within four months, with $1.2B+ expected by end of June 2026. Customers include Nvidia, AMD, Google, Amazon, Microsoft, Meta, Broadcom.
5. On June 24, 2026, SK Hynix's board approved a Nasdaq ADR listing (ticker SKHY), filing a Form F-1 with the SEC for up to 17.79M new shares (~2.5% of shares outstanding); trading targeted as early as July 10, 2026. SK Hynix crossed $1T market cap in late May 2026 (joining TSMC and Samsung as the only three Asian firms at that level).
