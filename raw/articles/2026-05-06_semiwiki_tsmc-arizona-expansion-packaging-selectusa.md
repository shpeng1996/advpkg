TSMC SVP, Deputy Co-COO & Chief Information Security Officer, Dr Cliff Hou, spoke at the US Department of Commerce's SelectUSA Investment Summit. Taiwan remained the largest foreign delegation for a third straight year, with TSMC indicating it may further expand its US investment footprint amid rising demand for advanced semiconductor manufacturing and AI infrastructure.

Asked whether TSMC would continue investing in the US, Dr. Cliff Hou said there were "many different possibilities," adding that the company was prepared to capture new business opportunities and future growth.

US Commerce Secretary Howard Lutnick said companies were sharply increasing investments in the US, particularly in AI infrastructure and semiconductors. TSMC and Micron Technology alone had committed hundreds of billions of dollars to manufacturing projects.

## TSMC Doubles Global Expansion Pace

TSMC will carry out nine phases of construction projects in 2025 and maintain the same pace in 2026—approximately twice the historical average of four phases per year. This accelerated construction pace applies to both Taiwan and overseas sites.

### Arizona Fab Status:
- **Fab 1 (N4 / 4nm)**: Already in mass production; yields comparable to Taiwan operations; N4 capacity increasing 1.8× in 2026
- **Fab 2 (3nm)**: Construction complete; equipment installation scheduled 2H 2026; mass production 2H 2027
- **Fab 3 (2nm)**: Under construction
- **Fab 4**: Construction to begin in 2026
- **Advanced Packaging Facility 1 (AP1)**: Construction to begin 2H 2026; operation 2028; tentatively planned for SoIC + CoW technologies
- **Advanced Packaging Facility 2 (AP2)**: Timeline dependent on customer demand

TSMC has also secured an additional site across from the existing Arizona campus for future expansion.

## Scale Beyond Current Roadmap

Industry sources said TSMC has internally confirmed plans for at least five to six additional facilities beyond the current roadmap of six fabs and two advanced packaging plants.

Strong demand from US customers, competition from Intel and Samsung for advanced chip orders, and efforts by Apple, Google, and AMD to diversify foundry sourcing beyond TSMC are all likely to accelerate the company's US expansion.

Sources also noted that Elon Musk's Terafab initiative and growing concerns over electricity supply are contributing factors, making further upward revisions to TSMC's US investment increasingly expected by the market.

US Commerce Secretary referenced a total commitment of more than US$1 trillion tied to semiconductor-related infrastructure in the US (excluding data centers).
