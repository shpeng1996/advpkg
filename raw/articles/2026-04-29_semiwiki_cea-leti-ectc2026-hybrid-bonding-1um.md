GRENOBLE, France – April 28, 2026 – CEA-Leti will present seven papers and posters on technologies shaping the next decade of advanced heterogenous integration at the Electronic Components and Technology Conference (ECTC) May 26-29 in Orlando, Fla.

These developments cover a variety of technologies, including reducing interconnect pitches through hybrid bonding interconnection, low-temperature processes, superconducting interconnects and fan-out wafer-level packaging.

Hybrid bonding, which is based on direct copper-to-copper and dielectric-to-dielectric connection technology, enables ultra-fine pitch vertical interconnects. It is emerging as the primary path for high-density 3D integration, as micro-bump approaches reach their scaling limits.

## Key Papers

**"Die-To-Wafer Hybrid Bonding Technology down to 1 μm pitch for Multi-Die Stacking Integration"** (Session 31, Friday May 29, 2:00–2:20 PM)
- Author: Melissa Najem
- First demonstration of a die-to-wafer hybrid bonding test vehicle with a pitch as small as 1µm.

**"Hybrid Bonding with Ultra-Low Temperature Annealing: Morphological and Electrical Validations"** (Session 3, Wednesday May 27, 9:30–9:50 AM)
- Author: Margot Faure
- First successful direct hybrid bonding annealed at ultra-low temperatures down to 100°C.

**"Enabling Low-Temperature Fine-Pitch Hybrid Bonding: Role of Nanocrystalline Copper Microstructures and Pre-Bond Surface Treatments"** (Session 3, Wednesday May 27, 11:35–11:55 AM)
- Author: Mathieu Loyer (in collaboration with STMicroelectronics)
- Nanocrystalline copper microstructures enabling low-temperature fine-pitch hybrid bonding.

**"Electroplated Indium Micro-Bumps: Toward Scalable Low Temperature Ultra-Fine Pitch Interconnects"** (Session 40, Wednesday May 27, 4:45–5:05 PM)
- Author: Maria-Luisa Calvo-Munoz
- Low-temperature ultra-fine pitch interconnect alternative.

**"Impact of Copper Density on Via-to-Via Hybrid Bonding: Morphological and Electrical Characterizations"** (Session 39 Poster, Thursday May 28, 10:00–12:00 PM)
- Author: Agathe Lerat

**"Fine-Pitch Thermally Resistive Superconducting 3D Interconnects for Quantum Systems"** (Session 39 Poster, Thursday May 28, 10:00–12:00 PM)
- Author: Pablo Renaud
- Packaging infrastructure for quantum computing (thermal resistance and electromagnetic isolation constraints).

**"RADAR System-in-Package with Integrated Antennas based on Fan-Out Wafer-Level Packaging RDL-First"** (Session 39 Poster, Wednesday May 27, 2:30–4:30 PM)
- Author: Arnaud Garnier
- Fan-out wafer-level packaging for millimeter-wave RF applications (5G/6G and automotive radar).

## Significance

Low-temperature processing enables heterogeneous integration where temperature-sensitive materials (polymers, certain semiconductors) must coexist with standard CMOS processes. The ability to form robust interconnects below typical thermal budgets opens pathways for stacking previously incompatible technologies.

As transistor gate-scaling slows, system-level integration through advanced packaging delivers the performance, power, and form-factor improvements that end-users expect.
