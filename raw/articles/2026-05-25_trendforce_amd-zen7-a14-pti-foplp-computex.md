With the CEOs of AMD and NVIDIA arriving in Taiwan back-to-back ahead of COMPUTEX in early June, their visits to key supply chain partners for next-generation CPU and GPU programs have drawn close attention.

AMD has already begun early preparations for its next-generation Zen 7 platform. The Zen 7's Core Complex Die (CCD), codenamed Grimlock, is expected to adopt TSMC's A14 node, while also evaluating Powertech Technology's (PTI) fan-out panel-level packaging (FOPLP) solution.

The product timeline aligns with TSMC's Fab 25 P1 facility in Taichung, which is scheduled to enter pilot production in 2027 and ramp into mass production in 2028, supporting AMD's next-generation chip rollout roadmap.

Zen 7 CCD is also expected to integrate next-generation 3D V-Cache technology. The Zen 7 architecture is said to feature 2MB of L2 cache and 4MB of L3 cache per core, alongside FP512 capabilities and integrated AI acceleration embedded directly within the CCD. Zen 7 is said to deliver a 15–25% IPC gain over Zen 6, with roughly 8 percentage points attributed to cache architecture enhancements. With 16 cores per CCD and next-generation 3D V-Cache, L3 cache capacity could reach as much as 224MB per CCD.

## PTI as Key Potential Advanced Packaging Partner

Taiwan's PTI is expected to play a key role in AMD's upgrade related to the new 3D V-Cache structure. AMD CEO Lisa Su personally visited PTI during her Taiwan trip, highlighting the company's growing role in AMD's future packaging strategy.

AMD recently publicly confirmed for the first time that PTI has successfully completed the world's first validation of panel-based 2.5D EFB (Elevated Fanout Bridge) interconnect technology. This is a landmark: 2.5D bridge interconnect technology extends from silicon wafer substrates to panel substrates for the first time, a key breakthrough for FOPLP entering CPU packaging.

AMD has also tapped Parade Technologies to develop a next-generation ASIC-like solution for high-speed interconnect applications, with the 6nm/12nm-based design already entering pilot production.

COMPUTEX 2026 runs from June 2 to June 5 in Taipei, with both Jensen Huang (NVIDIA) and Lisa Su (AMD) in Taiwan.
