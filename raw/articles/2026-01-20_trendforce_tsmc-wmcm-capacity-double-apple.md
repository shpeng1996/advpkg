# TSMC Reportedly Expands WMCM Packaging for Apple, Capacity May More Than Double by 2027

**Publisher**: TrendForce | **Date**: January 20, 2026

## Summary

TSMC is aggressively expanding its WMCM (Wafer-Level Multi-Chip Module) packaging capacity for Apple, with plans to more than double capacity by 2027 to support the transition from InFO to WMCM across Apple's mobile chip lineup.

## Key Findings

### WMCM Capacity Expansion Plan
- TSMC WMCM capacity may **more than double by 2027** compared to 2025/2026 baseline
- Primary expansion sites: **AP3** (existing facility, being upgraded) and **AP7 Phase 2** (Chiayi, Apple-dedicated)
- AP7 Phase 2 is specifically reserved for Apple's WMCM requirements
- Target: **60,000 wafers/month** WMCM capacity by end of 2026, scaling further in 2027

### Apple's WMCM Roadmap
- Apple confirmed as the primary and exclusive customer driving WMCM expansion
- **A20 chip (iPhone 18, 2026)**: First WMCM adoption in volume production
- A20 Pro/Ultra variants expected to also transition to WMCM
- Future Apple Silicon (M5 Pro/Max, A21) likely to adopt WMCM for continued scaling benefits

### WMCM vs. InFO Technology Comparison
| Feature | InFO (legacy) | WMCM (new) |
|---------|--------------|------------|
| Architecture | Single die in RDL | Multiple dies in shared RDL |
| Integration | Wafer-level fan-out | Multi-die fan-out |
| Thermal efficiency | Baseline | Improved (iPhone 18 claim) |
| Form factor | Compact | Slightly larger, more capable |
| Production start | 2016 | 2026 (volume) |

### AP3 Upgrade Program
- AP3 (existing TSMC packaging facility) is receiving equipment upgrades for WMCM production
- AP3 upgrade targets: produce WMCM alongside legacy InFO in transition period
- AP3 + AP7 together form the WMCM production base for 2026–2027

### Market Implications
- TSMC WMCM capacity expansion is essentially Apple-exclusive in the near term
- The WMCM technology may open to other customers after Apple's exclusivity window (potentially 2027–2028)
- InFO to remain in production for lower-end Apple chips and other customers during transition
- Broader implications: WMCM validates multi-die fan-out packaging for mobile, potentially accelerating chiplet adoption in smartphone SoCs
