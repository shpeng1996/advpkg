# What TSMC's CoPoS Move Really Means

## Key Claims

1. CoPoS (Chip-on-Panel-on-Substrate) replaces the circular wafer with a larger rectangular panel — more chips processed per run, lower unit cost, enables larger/more complex chip assemblies than wafer-based CoWoS.
2. Comparative positioning: CoWoS = wafer-based, mature, stable, used for today's AI GPUs; CoPoS = panel-based, aimed at next-gen larger AI chips, mass production targeted "2028 at the earliest"; FOPLP = panel-based, aimed at thin/dense chips for mobile/automotive/IoT (lower-end than CoPoS).
3. TSMC CEO C.C. Wei framed the CoPoS pilot line as a deliberate response to (a) chronic CoWoS capacity constraints and (b) AI chip designs approaching the physical/size limits of wafer-level packaging.
4. CoPoS will not replace CoWoS in the near term — CoWoS remains the AI-packaging backbone for the foreseeable future; CoPoS fills a complementary, larger-package niche.
5. Named initial Taiwanese CoPoS supply-chain vendor list by category: Equipment/process — Chroma ATE, Scientech, C Sun, GMM; Packaging/process equipment — APT (印能), GPTC (弘塑); Inspection/automation — Ta Liang Technology, V5 Technologies, Favite; Materials/carriers — Gudeng Precision; Others — Li-Din, SemTek, Manz (亞智科技).
