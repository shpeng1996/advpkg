# TSMC Sees 2Q Sales Up 10% QoQ to $40.2B; Margins Rise to 65.5%–67.5%, Capex at Top End of $52–56B

*TrendForce — April 16, 2026*

## Q2 2026 Revenue Guidance

- Revenue: **$39.0B–$40.2B** (YoY +~32%, QoQ +~10%)
- Assumption: exchange rate USD 1 = NT$31.7

## Margin Guidance (Q2 2026)

- **Gross margin**: 65.5%–67.5%
- **Operating margin**: 56.5%–58.5%
- CFO Wendell Huang: gross margin expected to improve, supported by higher utilization and cost optimization; partially diluted by overseas fab expansion
- Effective tax rate: ~20% for Q2, 17%–18% for full year

## Key Forward-Looking Notes

- **2nm ramp in 2H26**: Expected to dilute full-year gross margin by 2–3%
- **3nm margins**: Projected to exceed corporate average in 2H26
- **Overseas fab expansion**: Weighing on margins 2%–3% near-term, potentially widening to 3%–4% over time
- **FY26 CapEx**: At high end of **$52–56B range**; focused on 5G, AI, and HPC
- **Advanced packaging**: ~8% of TSMC revenue in 2025; expected to exceed 10% in 2026

## Q1 2026 Record Performance (Reference)

- TSMC 1Q26 net income soared 58% YoY to NT$572.5B (record), EPS NT$22.08
- Gross margin beat forecast at 66.2%

## Context for Advanced Packaging

- TSMC CapEx at high end confirms continued heavy investment in N2, advanced packaging, and overseas fabs
- Advanced packaging CapEx: approximately 10–20% of total CapEx budget
- CoWoS capacity expansion remains a priority (see related: CoWoS-L/S 115K–140K wsm target by end-2026)

*(Source: TSMC)*
