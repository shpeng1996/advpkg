Amid surging DRAM and NAND prices, Samsung's newly released second-quarter earnings guidance once again exceeded market expectations. Preliminary second-quarter operating profit reached KRW 89.4 trillion (US$65.6 billion), up 1,810.3% year over year. This would translate into an operating profit margin of 52.3%.

The result marks Samsung's highest quarterly operating profit on record, extending its record-breaking earnings streak to three consecutive quarters. The figure surpasses the quarterly operating profits of U.S. tech giants NVIDIA and Apple. Specifically, it surpassed NVIDIA's previous record of US$53.5 billion (approximately KRW 81.9 trillion) posted in the first quarter of fiscal 2027 (ended April 2026).

The company's preliminary second-quarter revenue came in at roughly KRW 171 trillion, up 129.3% from a year earlier.

## Memory Prices Fuel Samsung's Record Quarter

The strong results were primarily driven by Samsung's booming memory semiconductor business within its Device Solutions (DS) division. Average selling prices (ASPs) reportedly climbed more than 50% quarter over quarter for DRAM and over 60% for NAND. Samsung Electronics is negotiating aggressively with customers to raise its third-quarter DRAM ASP by as much as 20% from the previous quarter.

TrendForce's latest memory pricing survey indicates that the DRAM market will remain extremely tight in the third quarter of 2026, with contract DRAM prices expected to rise another 13–18%.

However, despite another strong quarter expected from its memory business, losses in Samsung's foundry and System LSI operations are likely to deepen, as employee bonus expenses continue to be allocated across the broader semiconductor division.

Context: Samsung's projected 2Q26 operating profit alone surpassed Samsung's cumulative operating profit over the entire three-year period from 2023 to 2025.
