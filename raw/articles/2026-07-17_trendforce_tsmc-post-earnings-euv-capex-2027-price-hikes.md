Following TSMC's Q2 2026 earnings call, foreign brokerages released fresh assessments of TSMC's outlook.

## CapEx Projections (Brokerage Forecasts)
- 2026: upper end ~US$62–64 billion
- 2027: Citi forecasts US$77 billion
- 2028: Citi US$86 billion; GF Securities most bullish at US$90 billion

## EUV as Key CapEx Driver
- Aletheia Capital links CapEx increase to ASML's upgraded FY26 revenue guidance of €43–45 billion
- Morgan Stanley: ASML capacity allocation keeps TSMC as its largest EUV customer
- Equipment cost pressure: Morgan Stanley estimates ~US$3 billion for 5% equipment price increase + ~US$8 billion in equipment prepayments
- ASML seeking ~10% price hike for mature DUV systems (TSMC is resisting)

## 2027 Wafer Pricing
- Morgan Stanley: TSMC to raise leading-edge wafer prices 5%–10% in 2027
- Goldman Sachs: modeling low- to mid-single-digit price increase for 2027

## Advanced Node Capacity Ramp
- N2-family capacity CAGR: >70% from 2026 to 2028 (Citi)
- N3: expanding rapidly for AI chip demand
- 5nm: expected to decline from 2027 as demand migrates to N3/N2; NVIDIA Rubin transition accelerates shift
- 2nm accounted for 3% of Q2 2026 revenue (first quarter at scale)

## Arizona Investment Analysis
- Aletheia Capital: $265 billion total = four new fabs (likely 2 front-end + 2 advanced packaging)
- Morgan Stanley: a potential reshaping of TSMC's advanced-node manufacturing footprint overseas

(Source: Reuters, The Information, Citi, GF Securities, Morgan Stanley, Goldman Sachs, Aletheia Capital)
