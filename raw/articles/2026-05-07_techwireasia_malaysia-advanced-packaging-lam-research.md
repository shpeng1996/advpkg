# Malaysia's advanced packaging opportunity is in the middle end

**Source:** TechWire Asia, Dashveenjit Kaur, May 7, 2026
**Interview subjects:** Andrew Goh (Corporate VP & GM Southeast Asia, Lam Research) and Lee Chee Ping (MD Strategic & Technical Marketing, Advanced Packaging Group, Lam Research) at SEMICON Southeast Asia 2026, MITEC Kuala Lumpur.

## Key Claims

- **Malaysia's realistic near-term play is advanced packaging (middle-end), not front-end fabs.** Malaysia has only two fabs doing low legacy processes; front-end window is "extremely narrow."
- **Malaysia's advanced packaging maturity is ~10 out of 100.** Base of assembly, test, and some packaging exists in Penang and Klang Valley but nowhere near cutting-edge.
- **Panel-level packaging is a key Lam Research investment area.** Lam acquired Austrian wet-processing specialist SEMSYSCO (Nov 2022) for panel-level cleaning and plating. Panel formats: 510mm or 600mm (vs. 300mm wafers) — dramatic area increase.
- **HBM shortage cascades to conventional memory.** Manufacturers pulling resources toward HBM has broken traditional allocation system; conventional DDR4/DDR5 now trades on spot market.
- **Lam's HBM-related tool revenue grew >50% year-over-year** in its most recent financial reporting.
- **Photonics is the next major shift.** Silicon photonics packages integrate conventional chips with optical components to move data using light; reduces data-center energy and bandwidth costs.
- **Malaysia+Singapore complementarity** could enable leapfrog in advanced packaging if Malaysia picks a specific niche and structures FDI/talent pipelines around it.

## New Knowledge Added

1. **Lam Research's strategic view**: Middle-end (advanced packaging) is Malaysia's realistic near-term play. Front-end fab ambition is "a bit too [much] for now." This is a major equipment vendor directly stating regional specialization boundaries.
2. **Panel-level packaging products**: Lam's panel-level products are already "available in the market for R&D with customers" but commercial customers are "very aggressively pushing for the final product." Ecosystem (materials, substrates, inspection) still assembling.
3. **HBM shortage cascade**: HBM overcrowding has turned conventional memory into a spot market — a market structure change with broad supply chain implications.
4. **Malaysia's FDI model for talent**: Historical lesson: FDI → engineers learn by doing → local capability builds. Malaysia needs to identify specific niche first, then structure FDI accordingly.
5. **Lam not in Malaysia National Semiconductor Strategy discussions** (as of SEMICON SEA 2026). Suggests strategy may lack industrial partnership alignment.
