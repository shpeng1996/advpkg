BESI, an industry leader in hybrid bonding technology critical to HBM packaging, is reportedly attracting takeover interest. According to Reuters, sources say BE Semiconductor (BESI) has received takeover approaches as demand for its chip-packaging technology has become increasingly important for semiconductor equipment makers. The company is said to be working with an investment bank to evaluate the approaches.

U.S. chip-equipment maker Lam Research is among the suitors that have held discussions with BESI. Other potentially interested parties include equipment manufacturer Applied Materials, which acquired a 9% stake in BESI in April 2025 and became its largest shareholder.

BESI and Applied Materials have been long-term partners in hybrid bonding. The technology directly links chips through copper-to-copper connections, enabling faster data transfer and lower power consumption in advanced semiconductors.

## BESI Market Position

Dutch equipment maker BESI is widely regarded as the leading company in hybrid bonder technology, with the Datacon 8800 CHAMEO and Kinex platforms for D2W hybrid bonding. Competition is emerging from Korean companies including LG Electronics, SEMES, Hanmi Semiconductor and Hanwha Semitech, as well as Singapore-based ASMPT.

## JEDEC Height Standard Uncertainty

Aside from takeover rumors, Besi has come under the spotlight amid potential changes to HBM thickness standards. JEDEC is considering relaxing thickness standards for next-generation HBM stacks (from ~775µm for HBM4 to ~900µm for HBM4E). Such a move could delay hybrid bonding adoption, as chipmakers could continue using thermocompression (TC) bonding—a cheaper, proven technology.

If thickness standards are relaxed, chipmakers may face less urgency to move beyond existing bonding equipment. This could slow the shift toward hybrid bonding, which lies at the core of BESI's long-term growth strategy.

## Strategic Context

The takeover interest reflects the broader consolidation trend in the semiconductor equipment industry. A Lam Research or Applied Materials acquisition of BESI would:
- Give the acquirer leadership in the hybrid bonding equipment market
- Complete a full-stack advanced packaging equipment offering
- Accelerate hybrid bonding technology development with larger R&D budgets
- Note: Applied Materials already holds 9% stake, making it a natural acquirer

This follows Applied Materials' acquisition of ASMPT's NEXX panel ECD division in May 2026, confirming major equipment OEMs are aggressively consolidating advanced packaging capabilities.
