TSMC's CoWoS supply-demand gap is reportedly expected to narrow from roughly 20% to about 10% by the end of 2026, as the company continues to expand advanced packaging capacity.

TSMC's own monthly CoWoS capacity is projected to reach 120,000–140,000 wafers in 2026, with an additional 50,000–60,000 wafers per month contributed by OSAT partners, bringing total CoWoS-related capacity to roughly 200,000 wafers per month.

CoWoS demand has grown at a CAGR of over 80% between 2022 and 2027, driven almost entirely by AI accelerator packaging needs.

TSMC's next-generation panel-level packaging technology, CoPoS, is reportedly on track for qualification in June 2026, with a pilot line expected by mid-2027. NVIDIA's Feynman platform is expected to be the first customer for CoPoS, with mass production targeted for 2028–2029 at TSMC's Chiayi (AP7) and Arizona facilities.

The narrowing supply gap reflects TSMC's aggressive capacity buildout in response to sustained AI-driven demand, even as the company simultaneously pushes forward next-generation panel-level packaging to address longer-term capacity and cost constraints.
