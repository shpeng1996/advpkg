# Intel Foundry Gathers Customers and Partners, Outlines Priorities

At Intel Foundry Direct Connect (announced Apr 29, 2025), Intel Foundry shared progress on multiple generations of process and advanced packaging technologies, plus new ecosystem programs and partnerships.

**Process technology:**
- Intel 14A (successor to Intel 18A): early PDK distributed to lead customers; multiple customers intend to build test chips. Features PowerDirect direct-contact power delivery, building on Intel 18A's PowerVia backside power delivery.
- Intel 18A: in risk production, expected to reach volume manufacturing in 2025.
- Intel 18A-P: new variant for enhanced performance across a broader customer set; design-rule compatible with Intel 18A.
- Intel 18A-PT: builds on 18A-P; can be connected to a top die using Foveros Direct 3D with hybrid-bonding interconnect pitch less than 5 micrometers (µm).
- First production 16nm tape-out in the fab now; engaging lead customers on a 12nm node and derivatives built in collaboration with UMC.

**Advanced packaging:**
- System-level integration using Intel 14A on Intel 18A-PT, connected via Foveros Direct (3D stacking) and embedded multi-die interconnect bridging (2.5D bridging, i.e. EMIB).
- New packaging offerings: **EMIB-T**, to enable future high-bandwidth-memory needs; two new Foveros architecture additions — **Foveros-R** and **Foveros-B** — providing additional flexible packaging options.
- New engagement with **Amkor Technology** to increase customer flexibility in choosing advanced packaging technology.

**Manufacturing:**
- Fab 52 in Arizona "ran the lot" — first wafer processed through the facility on Intel 18A. Intel 18A volume production begins in Oregon fabs first, with Arizona ramping later in 2025. Intel 18A and 14A R&D/production are entirely U.S.-based.

**Ecosystem:**
- New Accelerator Alliance programs: Intel Foundry Chiplet Alliance (focused on infrastructure for government and key commercial chiplet markets) and Value Chain Alliance, alongside IP Alliance, EDA Alliance, Design Services Alliance, Cloud Alliance, and USMAG Alliance.
- Ecosystem partners highlighted: Synopsys, Cadence, Siemens EDA, PDF Solutions, MediaTek, Microsoft, Qualcomm.

Quote from CEO Lip-Bu Tan: "Intel is committed to building a world-class foundry that serves the growing need for leading-edge process technology, advanced packaging and manufacturing... The work we are doing to drive an engineering-first culture across Intel while strengthening our partnerships throughout the foundry ecosystem will help us to advance our strategy, improve our execution and win in the market long term."
